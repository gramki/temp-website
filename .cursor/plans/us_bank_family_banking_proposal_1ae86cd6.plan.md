# Plan: US Mid-Sized Bank Family Banking Value Proposition

## Objective

Create a parallel 5-part proposal for a mid-sized US bank (similar to PNC Bank scale) that adapts the India value proposition to US banking context. All scenarios, metrics, regulations, products, and financial analysis must be localized for the US market.

## Document Structure (Parallel to India Proposal)

```
ibuki/ibuki-family-banking-value-prop-us/
├── README.md                          (Executive overview, US context)
├── family-banking-product-proposal.md  (US family financial needs)
├── solution-vision.md                  (AI agents for US market)
├── agents-and-their-roles.md          (Architecture - largely same)
├── value-to-customers.md              (US customer value in USD)
└── value-to-bank.md                   (US bank business case in USD)
```

- US family banking landscape (what exists, what's missing)

- US communication channels (SMS, email, voice, apps)
- Competitive landscape (mid-sized banks offering family features)
- Market opportunity sizing (addressable households)

**Key Adaptations:**

- Replace ₹ with $ (USD)
- Replace Indian bank names with US mid-sized bank examples
- Replace WhatsApp with US channels (SMS, email, voice)

- Replace Indian regulatory context with US (FDIC, CFPB, state regulations)

**Deliverables:**

- Market size estimate (e.g., "X million households in target segment")
- Competitive positioning
- US-specific value proposition

### 2. family-banking-product-proposal.md - Market Need

**Research Needed:**

**A. US Family Financial Scenarios:**

- US household bill payment patterns (mortgage, utilities, insurance, subscriptions)
- US family expense splitting (couples, roommates, multi-generational)
- US tax scenarios (federal, state, local; deductions, credits, filing)
- US savings goals (college 529 plans, retirement IRAs, emergency funds)
- US credit management (credit scores, debt consolidation, credit cards)
- US investment scenarios (401k, IRAs, brokerage accounts, college savings)
- US loan scenarios (mortgages, auto loans, student loans, HELOCs)
- US insurance scenarios (health, life, auto, home, disability)
- US estate planning (wills, trusts, beneficiaries)
- US family financial coordination (joint accounts, authorized users, guardianship)

**B. US Banking Products & Services:**

- Checking accounts (types, fees, features)
- Savings accounts (high-yield, money market, CDs)
- Credit cards (rewards, cashback, balance transfer)
- Mortgages (conventional, FHA, VA, refinancing)
- Auto loans (new, used, refinancing)
- Personal loans (unsecured, secured)
- Home equity products (HELOCs, home equity loans)
- Investment products (brokerage, managed accounts, robo-advisors)
- Insurance products (life, health, auto, home)
- Retirement products (401k, IRAs, 403b)

**C. Current US Bank Offerings:**

- Analyze 3-5 mid-sized US banks (e.g., PNC, Regions, Fifth Third, KeyBank, Huntington)
- What family features exist? (joint accounts, family banking, shared goals)
- What's missing? (coordination, proactive assistance, family context)
- Coverage analysis: individual vs. family, product vs. solution

**D. US Regulatory Context:**

- FDIC insurance limits and rules
- CFPB regulations (fair lending, disclosures)
- State banking regulations
- Privacy laws (state-specific)
- Data sharing regulations

**Key Adaptations:**

- Replace ₹ amounts with $ (e.g., ₹25,000 → $500-1,000 monthly bills)
- Replace Indian products (UPI, NEFT) with US (ACH, wire, Zelle, Venmo)
- Replace Indian tax system with US (IRS, state taxes, deductions)
- Replace Indian scenarios with US equivalents:
  - "Salary credit" → "Paycheck deposit"
  - "EMI" → "Monthly payment"
  - "SIP" → "Automatic investment"
  - "Form 16" → "W-2, 1099"
- Replace Indian family structures with US (nuclear, blended, multi-generational)
- Replace Indian financial behaviors with US patterns

**Deliverables:**

- 10 journey types adapted for US context
- 60+ US-specific family financial scenarios
- Coverage analysis of US mid-sized banks
- Gap identification (US-specific)

## Key Differences: India vs. US

| Dimension | India | US |

|-----------|-------|-----|

| **Currency** | ₹ (INR) | $ (USD) |

| **Payment Systems** | UPI, NEFT, IMPS | ACH, Wire, Zelle, Venmo |

| **Communication** | WhatsApp dominant | SMS, Email, Voice, Apps |

| **Regulatory** | RBI, SEBI | FDIC, CFPB, State regulators |

| **Tax System** | Income tax, GST | Federal, State, Local taxes |

| **Products** | Savings, FDs, SIPs | Checking, Savings, 401k, IRAs |

| **Family Structure** | Extended, joint families | Nuclear, blended families |

| **Banking Penetration** | Growing digital adoption | High digital adoption |

| **AI Adoption** | Emerging | More mature |

| **Market Size** | 50-60M urban households | TBD (research needed) |
