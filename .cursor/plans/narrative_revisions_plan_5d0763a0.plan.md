# Incorporating Key Facts into the Business Model Narrative

**Target file:** [business-model-narrative.md](org-8.0/what-we-sell/business-model-narrative.md)

---

## What Gets Interleaved into the Narrative

### A. Channel strategy for community banks (Point 1)

**Where:** "Who We Serve" opening paragraph (line 29), expanded to describe a three-tier go-to-market.

**Current text:**

> "We sell to regional and large banks in the United States, and to technology providers who serve them."

**Revised direction:** Expand this into a short paragraph that makes the go-to-market architecture explicit. Zeta engages directly with regional and large banks. Community banks and credit unions — the institutions that most need democratized technology — are reached through technology providers who have pre-existing distribution relationships with these institutions. This is a deliberate channel design, not a gap. It also sharpens the "Technology Providers" customer segment, which is currently underdeveloped in the narrative.

This also strengthens the "Democratize Banking" mission by showing that reach to smaller institutions is part of the design, not an afterthought.

### B. Platform compounding evidence (Point 4)

**Where:** "How We Sustain" section, inner source paragraph (line 163).

**Current text:**

> "This flywheel only works if inner source is treated as planned engineering work, not as a side activity. It requires governance, incentives, and cultural reinforcement."

**Revised direction:** Acknowledge that while inner source as a *formalized model* is new, the underlying pattern — evolving the platform based on what is learned in each delivery — has been operational for five years across multiple Engagements. The frameworks and product lines reflect compounding from real production experience, not from a lab. The inner source model codifies and accelerates what was already happening organically. This shifts the tone from aspirational to evidenced.

### C. Evolved approach, not just clearer articulation (Point 5)

**Where:** "What This Means" closing section (lines 171-177).

**Current text:**

> "The revised business model framing is not a new strategy. It is a clearer articulation of what we have always believed and what we are building."

**Revised direction:** The core strategy — democratize banking — has not changed. But the approach has evolved substantially based on five years of delivering products to banks in production. The five-layer leverage model, the Engagement Engineering methodology, the Hubs-Streams-Loops framework — these are not theoretical constructs. They are the distillation of hard-won learning from real Engagements, real production environments, and real customer feedback. This framing is stronger than "clearer articulation" because it signals adaptation and earned insight, not just better messaging.

## What Goes in a New Epilogue

Points 2 ($5M ARR minimum) and 3 (Exploration investment) are specific economic facts that ground the narrative in operational reality but would break the Sinek-style prose flow. They belong in a separate section at the end.

### Proposed epilogue: "Key Facts and Context"

Placed after the closing, before EOF. Written in a more direct, factual tone (not narrative prose). Short paragraphs or a structured format. Contains:

- **Engagement economics**: Minimum $5M ARR per Engagement. This is a critical LOB platform decision, not a point solution or lightweight vendor association. Customers select Zeta to run a major line of business.
- **Exploration investment**: Zeta invests $100K-$200K in pre-commitment Exploration for regional banks. For larger banks, Exploration investment may reach $1M. This is a deliberate capital allocation — it ensures Zeta deeply understands each bank's landscape before committing to an Engagement.
- **Go-to-market tiers**: Direct engagement with large and regional banks; indirect reach to community banks and credit unions through technology provider partners.
- **Platform compounding track record**: Five years of production Engagements have driven measurable evolution of frameworks and product lines. The inner source model formalizes this compounding.
- **Items for further discussion**: A short list of topics this document intentionally does not address — pricing model, margin structure, competitive win rates, capacity constraints, international expansion — flagged as subjects for separate board-level review. This signals awareness and maturity rather than avoidance.
