## Revenue Mix Analysis

Applying the book's Chapter 9 (Economics) diagnostic framework:

| Component | PayZapp | Pixel | Combined | Share |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| SaaS (Platform) | $2.4M | $4.9M | **$7.3M** | **17%** |
| Studio (Services) | $19.0M | $12.6M | **$31.6M** | **75%** |
| Non-SaaS | $1.9M | $1.2M | **$3.1M** | **7%** |
| **Total** | **$23.3M** | **$18.7M** | **$42.0M** | 100% |

**Diagnostic interpretation**: At 75% studio/services revenue, the HDFC engagement is operationally a **transformation delivery engagement** — not a product-led platform relationship. The platform exists and is real (SaaS revenue confirms it), but the economic center of gravity is studio engineering.

Contrast with the US Axon/FIS engagement (from Mercury Axon, July 2025): ~78% SaaS revenue — a fundamentally different economic profile from the same company.

## Engagement Health Signals

### Positive Signals

| Signal | Evidence |
|---|---|
| Product-market fit confirmed | PayZapp top-10 UPI app; Pixel doubling transactions in 6 months |
| Deepening product footprint | Four surfaces live, Sigma relaunching, cross-sell pipeline active |
| Industry recognition | Celent Model Bank Award 2024 |
| Engagement depth (Pixel) | 14.98 txns/active user/month — deep usage, not vanity adoption |
| JV discussion | Payments Factory JV with HDFC under discussion — highest-tier commitment signal |

### Risk Signals

| Signal | Evidence | Interpretation |
|---|---|---|
| Studio reduction (25%) | Both PayZapp and Pixel studios reduced | Bank recalibrating cost of delivery |
| Sigma merged with Pixel studio | Consolidation of Zeta teams | Bank seeking fewer vendor touchpoints |
| CS agent reduction (~20% in PZ) | Headcount cut | Cost optimization |
| Insourcing evaluation | Bank evaluating feasibility of insourcing CS and operations | **Active dependency-reduction assessment** |
| Outcome-based vendor models (public) | HDFC shifting IT vendor engagement to outcome-based payments | Structural shift away from team-size-based pricing |

### Interpretation

The HDFC engagement shows a **simultaneous deepening and contraction** pattern:

- **Deepening**: More product surfaces, more products in pipeline, JV discussions, growing transaction volumes
- **Contraction**: Fewer Zeta people, lower services spend, insourcing exploration

This is consistent with a maturing enterprise relationship where the **platform value is being recognized** but the **services premium is being challenged**. The bank wants the technology; it is questioning whether it needs (or wants) the studio at current scale.

From the book's framework (Chapter 10 — Scaling), this is the natural trajectory of a well-executed solutions engagement: the platform earns its place, and the delivery overlay should shrink proportionally. The strategic question is whether Zeta is **driving** this transition (deliberately shifting toward platform economics) or **experiencing** it (reacting to HDFC's cost optimization).

### HDFC Competitive Context

HDFC Bank is also partnering with other fintechs for adjacent capabilities:
- **PhonePe**: Co-branded RuPay credit card launched June 2025
- **Juspay**: "Smart Gateway" partnership for payment orchestration

Zeta is not HDFC's exclusive technology partner. The bank maintains relationships with multiple technology providers across different capabilities. Zeta's position is strongest in the payment app (PayZapp) and digital credit card (Pixel) surfaces, but the bank actively engages alternative vendors for new capabilities.
