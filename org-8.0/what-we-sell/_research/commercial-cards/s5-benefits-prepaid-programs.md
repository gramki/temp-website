## Structural Dynamics

### Why Benefits Cards Are Recurring-Revenue Programs

- **Payroll-deduction stickiness:** Benefits cards are funded through recurring payroll deductions (HSA contributions, FSA elections, meal allowances). Once an employer integrates card-based benefits into payroll, switching costs are high — requiring re-enrollment, new card issuance, HR system reconfiguration, and employee communication.
- **Annual enrollment cycles:** Benefit elections renew annually during open enrollment, creating predictable recurring revenue with automatic re-enrollment as the default for most account types.
- **Employer retention rates:** HSA custodians like HealthEquity report employer retention rates well above 90%, driven by integration depth with HR/payroll systems and employee familiarity with card-based experiences.
- **Float income as baseline revenue:** Prepaid balances generate float income regardless of transaction volume, providing revenue stability even in low-spend periods.

### Shift from Reimbursement to Card-Based Delivery

- India is undergoing an accelerating shift from paper-based reimbursement to electronic card/wallet-based benefits, driven by the RBI mandate eliminating paper PPIs (2021) and employer demand for auditability and tax compliance.
- The proposed increase in meal card exemptions from Rs 50 to Rs 200 per meal (Draft Income-tax Rules 2026) is expected to dramatically accelerate card-based meal benefit adoption, as the tax savings become material (up to Rs 1,05,600/year vs. Rs 26,400 currently).
- In the USA, auto-substantiation via IIAS has reduced manual reimbursement claims for FSA/HRA, making card-based spending the default mode. Stacked card technology (one card, multiple benefit purses) further simplifies the experience.
- UPI-native wallets in India (678M merchant QR codes vs. 8.96M POS terminals) are emerging as an alternative distribution channel, potentially bypassing traditional card rails for certain benefit types. Sources: [CashBook](https://cashbook.in/blogs/corporate-expense-tracking-india-upi), [Outlook Money](https://www.outlookmoney.com/tax/new-tax-regime-push-spurs-rise-in-wallet-based-employee-benefits)

### Multi-Benefit Platforms vs. Single-Benefit Providers

- **Multi-benefit consolidation is the dominant trend.** Forma's 2025 benchmark reports 54% of companies manage 2+ LSAs on one platform and 62% manage 2+ pre-tax programs on one platform.
- **India:** Pluxee's 2-in-1 card (meal + other benefits on a single card) and Zaggle's expanding product suite (prepaid + expense management + SaaS) reflect this consolidation. Zaggle has added corporate credit cards, forex cards, and Mastercard co-branded prepaid cards alongside its core benefits offering.
- **USA:** Benepass, Lively, and WEX each offer HSA + FSA + HRA + LSA + commuter on unified platforms, competing against legacy single-category providers.
- **Platform winners** are those that can deliver multiple benefit types through a single card/app while maintaining compliance (IIAS, MCC enforcement, tax substantiation) across categories.

### HR Platform Integration as Distribution

- Benefits card providers increasingly distribute through HRIS integrations. ADP connects to 900+ insurance carriers for benefits enrollment. BambooHR automates HSA/FSA enrollment with payroll deduction sync. Workday exposes APIs for benefits data and payroll synchronization.
- In India, Zeta's partnership with Excelity Global (Asia's largest payroll administrator, processing $20B in annual payroll) is a direct example of this distribution strategy.

## Key Findings

### India
- **Massive regulatory tailwind:** The proposed increase from Rs 50 to Rs 200 per meal (Draft Income-tax Rules 2026) would quadruple the annual tax-free meal benefit from ~Rs 26,400 to ~Rs 1,05,600, making card-based meal benefits dramatically more attractive. Combined with the proposed gift card limit increase (Rs 5,000 → Rs 15,000), this could unlock significant new demand.
- **Zeta is a critical infrastructure provider:** Zeta powers Pluxee India (11,000+ corporates, 3.5M+ consumers) and has direct bank partnerships with IDFC FIRST, RBL, and others. Its position as the technology backbone for India's largest benefits brand by client count is a strategic asset.
- **Zaggle is the largest listed pure-play:** With Rs 498 Cr quarterly revenue (+48% YoY), 45M+ cards issued, and expansion into credit cards, forex, and Mastercard partnerships, Zaggle represents the most visible public benchmark for this market.
- **UPI-native competition emerging:** India's 678M merchant QR codes (vs. 8.96M POS terminals) create a viable alternative to card-based delivery through UPI wallets, potentially disintermediating traditional card rails for certain benefit categories.
- **Bank engagement is broad but shallow:** 10+ banks have launched or partnered on corporate prepaid/benefits cards, but most rely on fintech partners (Zaggle, Zeta) for the technology and program management rather than building proprietary platforms.

### USA
- **HSA is a $159B market growing at 16% annually**, with investments ($73B, up 30% YoY) becoming an increasingly important component. The market is projected to exceed $208B by end of 2027.
- **Consolidation favors scale:** Top 5 providers control 75% of HSA assets. HealthEquity alone manages $34.4B in HSA assets across 10.1M accounts after its $2B WageWorks acquisition.
- **Stacked cards are the modernization vector:** Visa's multi-account linking and platforms like First Dollar, InComm Benefits, and Benepass are enabling one-card-multiple-benefit experiences, reducing card production costs ($5/card savings) and increasing utilization.
- **LSAs are the fastest-growing category:** Dedicated spending accounts for caregiving, commuter, and family formation grew 50%+ YoY, outpacing all-inclusive LSAs (23% growth). LSAs have expanded beyond tech into mainstream industries.
- **IIAS/MCC compliance is a meaningful technology moat:** HSA/FSA card programs require integration with SIGIS-certified auto-substantiation systems, creating technical barriers to entry for card issuers.
- **Custodial revenue dominates economics:** HealthEquity's custodial revenue ($545M) exceeds both service fees ($478M) and interchange ($176M), making float income the primary profit driver in the HSA segment.

## Gaps and Unresolved Questions

- **India employer adoption rates:** No reliable survey data found on the percentage of Indian employers currently offering card-based benefits (meal, fuel, gift). The 68% figure from Mercer Marsh relates to benefits broadly, not card-specific programs. `[unverified — needs manual confirmation]`
- **India market size disaggregation:** The $11.88B India gift card market includes consumer and corporate segments; the corporate-benefits-specific portion could not be isolated. `[unverified — needs manual confirmation]`
- **Pluxee India cardholder count precision:** The 3.5M consumer figure comes from Pluxee.in marketing; independent verification of active card count was not found.
- **Edenred India-specific revenue:** Edenred does not separately disclose India revenues. The "Rest of World" segment (€258M, 10% of total) includes India but is not broken out further. `[unverified — needs manual confirmation]`
- **FSA total market size:** Unlike HSAs (which have robust Devenir data), there is no comparable centralized data source for total FSA market size, total accounts, or total balances. EBRI's 3.2M-account database is a sample, not a census. `[unverified — needs manual confirmation]`
- **LSA total market size:** No reliable total addressable market (TAM) figure for Lifestyle Spending Accounts was found. Forma's benchmarks cover 300 employers but do not provide industry-wide sizing.
- **Specific HSA custodian market share:** While Devenir reports top-5 concentration at 75%, individual custodian market share percentages (Fidelity, UMB, Optum, HealthEquity individually) were not available in publicly accessible sources.
- **2025 tax bill HSA expansion details:** Reports reference a 2025 Congressional bill expanding HSA contribution limits and eligibility effective January 2026, but the specific enacted provisions could not be independently verified at time of research. `[unverified — needs manual confirmation]`
- **NovaBenefits positioning:** NovaBenefits appears primarily focused on health insurance brokerage and wellness rather than prepaid card issuance. Its inclusion as a "benefits card" player may overstate its card-program relevance.
- **SBI, Kotak, ICICI benefits card programs:** No evidence found of these major Indian banks having launched dedicated employee benefits card programs (as opposed to general prepaid cards). This may be a gap in their offerings or in the available public information.
