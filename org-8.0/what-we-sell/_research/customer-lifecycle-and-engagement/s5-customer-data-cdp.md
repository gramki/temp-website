## Key Findings

### CDP and Customer 360 Adoption in Banking

- **Majority of banks lack a unified customer view.** 57% of banking executives (CleverTap 2024) and 60% of Tier 1 banks (Celent 2025) report they have not achieved a single customer view or cite existing data architectures as barriers to decision intelligence. This is the dominant finding across all survey data.
- **CDP market penetration correlates tightly with institution size.** 81% of firms with >$10B revenue have completed or in-progress CDP deployments vs. only 27% of the smallest firms — suggesting Tier 1 banks are far more likely to have a CDP than Tier 2–3 institutions, which are underserved.
- **BFSI is a top-3 but not top-1 CDP vertical.** Retail/e-commerce leads adoption; BFSI is estimated at 12–18% of CDP market revenue. This means the banking CDP opportunity is real but still maturing.
- **Warehouse-native CDPs are outgrowing the category.** Composable/warehouse-native CDP vendors are growing at 7.8% vs. 1.3% industry average — this architectural shift has major implications for banks already investing in cloud data warehouses (Snowflake, Databricks).
- **The category boundary between CDP, MDM, and CRM is dissolving in banking.** Salesforce "Data 360 for Financial Services," Pega Customer Decision Hub, and Reltio MDM all compete for the same "unified customer view" budget. Banks typically need both MDM (for regulatory accuracy and golden record) and CDP (for real-time activation and personalization) — but increasingly purchase them as integrated stacks.

### Named Bank Deployments — What the Evidence Shows

- **Tier 1 banks** tend toward enterprise platforms and build-internally approaches: Wells Fargo (Pega CDH at enormous scale — 32B records/month), HSBC (Pega global framework), Westpac ($2B Unite program). These are multi-year, hundreds-of-millions programs.
- **Tier 2 banks** ($10B–$100B) adopt vendor platforms: CNB Bank ($6B, Salesforce FSC), Fulton Bank (Reltio MDM), Synovus (Profisee MDM), Truist (custom Client Pulse).
- **Tier 3/community banks** ($1B–$10B) are where CDP vendor deployments are beginning to land: Extraco Banks ($2.5B, Treasure Data), Mascoma Bank (Salesforce Data Cloud unified 66 systems), Ponce Bank (54+ systems consolidated).
- **India:** HDFC Bank using Lemnisk CDP; UnionBank Philippines adopted Informatica MDM after Citibank acquisition.
- **Fintechs and neobanks:** SoFi (mParticle), Lulo Bank (mParticle) — these adopt CDPs earlier due to cloud-native architecture and smaller legacy footprint.

### MDM and Identity Resolution

- **MDM is a larger, more mature market than CDP in banking.** The MDM market ($19.9B–$20.6B in 2023) dwarfs the CDP market ($3.3B–$9.7B in 2025). BFSI is consistently the #1 or #2 vertical for MDM — banks have been buying MDM for decades for KYC, AML, and regulatory compliance.
- **Key MDM vendors with proven banking deployments:** Informatica (UnionBank Philippines, RBC Wealth Management), Reltio (Fulton Bank, First Tech FCU), Profisee (Synovus), Precisely (global bank AML), Verato (investment services firm — 150M+ identities).
- **Identity resolution is an adjacent $1.7B market** growing at 10–16% CAGR, with financial services as a primary vertical driven by KYC/AML requirements.

### Consent Management

- **Consent management is a ~$800M–$915M market (2024–2025)** growing at 19.5% CAGR toward $5.2B by 2033. BFSI is among the highest-adoption verticals.
- **Regulatory pressure is compounding:** CCPA/CPRA (finalized Sep 2025, effective Jan 2026), 20+ state privacy laws, DPDP Act (India, 18-month implementation from Nov 2025), GDPR, and CFPB 1033 all create overlapping consent obligations for banks.
- **India's DPDP Act creates a particularly acute dual-compliance challenge** — RBI mandates 7-year data retention while DPDP defaults to 1-year post-purpose deletion. Most BFSI entities qualify as Significant Data Fiduciaries requiring DPIAs, annual audits, and DPO appointment.

### Account Aggregator (India) and CFPB 1033 (USA)

- **India's AA ecosystem has achieved massive scale:** 252.9M users, 2.61B financial accounts enabled, 754 FIUs live, ~700 entities across banking/insurance/securities. Growth is 13% MoM in consent requests. This is the world's largest consent-based financial data sharing infrastructure.
- **AA is forcing architectural change in Indian banks.** FIPs must serve real-time data via AA APIs with encrypted data pipelines and consent management — this requires banks to reorganize internal customer data architectures around consent-based access rather than product-based silos.
- **CFPB 1033 in the US is in limbo.** Despite October 2024 finalization, enforcement is blocked by litigation and agency reconsideration. The April 2026 compliance deadline is "no longer a safe planning assumption." However, open banking may continue evolving through private FDX-standard API agreements.

### Build vs. Buy by Bank Tier

- **Tier 1 ($100B+):** Predominantly build or heavily customize enterprise platforms. Westpac's $2B Unite program is illustrative — these are multi-year infrastructure programs, not vendor purchases. Wells Fargo's Pega CDH deployment is similarly custom.
- **Tier 2 ($10B–$100B):** Mixed vendor adoption — Salesforce FSC, Reltio MDM, Profisee MDM, Pega CDH are common choices. Typical CDP license cost: $100K–$300K/year with 4–5 week deployment.
- **Tier 3 ($1B–$10B):** Emerging CDP adoption from vendors like Treasure Data, Salesforce Data Cloud, Lemnisk. POC-to-production timelines of 3–6 months. This tier represents the growth frontier for CDP vendors.
- **Hybrid "build and buy"** is the emerging consensus — purchased CDP platform + custom data engineering on top of cloud data warehouses (Snowflake, Databricks).

## CDP vs. MDM vs. Customer 360 — Definitional Clarity for Banking

| Dimension | CDP | MDM | Customer 360 |
|-----------|-----|-----|--------------|
| **Core purpose** | Real-time activation, personalization, marketing | Data governance, golden record, regulatory compliance | Outcome (unified view) that both CDP and MDM help achieve |
| **Data types** | Structured + unstructured + behavioral + digital interactions | Structured enterprise data (CRM, core, ERP) | All of the above |
| **Update speed** | Real-time | Batch or near-real-time | Depends on underlying platform |
| **Identity resolution** | AI-driven (deterministic + probabilistic) | Rules-based / deterministic | Either approach |
| **Primary users** | Marketing, CX, product | IT, compliance, finance, risk | All business lines |
| **Banking buyer** | CMO / Head of Digital | CDO / CTO / CISO | C-suite initiative (spans both) |

In banking, MDM is the compliance backbone (KYC accuracy, AML, golden record), while CDP is the activation layer (next-best-action, real-time offers, journey orchestration). Most banks need both — and increasingly the market is converging, with vendors like Salesforce (Data 360 for FS) and Informatica (MDM + data quality + governance) offering integrated stacks.

## Raw Notes

### CDP vs. MDM Market Positioning in Banking

The banking customer data market is fragmenting across four categories:
1. **Traditional MDM** (Informatica, Reltio, Profisee, Precisely) — compliance and golden record
2. **CDP platforms** (Treasure Data, mParticle, Salesforce Data Cloud, Lemnisk, Redpoint) — activation and personalization
3. **Decisioning platforms** (Pega CDH, FICO, SAS) — next-best-action using customer data
4. **Cloud data platforms** (Snowflake, Databricks, Google BigQuery) — infrastructure layer that both CDP and MDM can sit on

The convergence trend: Salesforce "Data 360 for Financial Services" bundles CDP + CRM + analytics; Informatica bundles MDM + data quality + governance; Pega bundles decisioning + customer data + process automation.

### Architectural Implications

India's Account Aggregator ecosystem has created a production-scale consent architecture that is ahead of any Western equivalent. The 252.9M user base and 2.61B accounts demonstrate that consent-based data sharing works at population scale when backed by regulatory mandate (RBI) and digital public infrastructure (India Stack). CFPB 1033 aims for similar functionality but through private-sector API standards (FDX) rather than regulated infrastructure — and faces political/legal headwinds that AA did not.

For banks: the AA model requires FIP integration (serving data via APIs), which means banks must have their customer data organized, accessible, and API-ready. This is functionally equivalent to the "customer data foundation" that CDP and MDM vendors sell — suggesting that in India, regulatory mandate is doing what vendor sales teams are still trying to accomplish in the US and EU.

### Data Quality as the Persistent Bottleneck

The UnionBank Philippines case (KYC data quality from 35% → 100% in one year) and the Celent finding (60% of Tier 1 banks cite data architecture as a barrier) reinforce a pattern: the customer data problem in banking is not primarily a software problem but a data quality and integration problem. Banks have accumulated decades of product-organized, siloed, duplicate-ridden customer data. No CDP can solve this without underlying MDM / data quality remediation — which is why the MDM market ($20B) is 2–6× larger than the CDP market ($3–10B).

### Cornerstone Advisors Trend: Data Strategy Effectiveness Rising

The Cornerstone 2026 finding that banks rating their data strategy "very effective" nearly tripled (6% → 17%) is a leading indicator of accelerating investment. However, 17% "very effective" means 83% are still not fully confident in their data strategy — the opportunity gap remains enormous.
