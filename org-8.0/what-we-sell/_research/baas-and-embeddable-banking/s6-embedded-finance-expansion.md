## 5. Key Findings (Structured Bullets)

### Product Mix Is Shifting Away from Payments-Only

- **Payments still dominates** the embedded finance revenue mix: consumer payments alone accounted for ~55% of embedded finance revenue in 2021 ($12B of $22B total; Bain 2022). Top SaaS platforms now generate >50% of total revenue from embedded payments (BCG/Adyen 2024).
- **Banking & cards is the fastest-growing segment by revenue multiple**: projected 5.5x growth from $2B (2021) to $11B (2026) — faster than payments' ~1.75x growth over the same period (Bain 2022).
- **Embedded lending is small but accelerating**: 5–6% of lending revenues in 2023, projected 20–25% by 2033 (McKinsey, cited by Alloy). Embedded B2B lending projected to grow 5x from $12B to $50–75B loan volume (Bain 2022).
- **Accounts remain underpenetrated**: most SMEs still source banking outside their SaaS platforms despite using those platforms as "operating systems" (BCG 2025). Digital deposit account opening ranked as top investment priority for 16–19% of retail banking executives surveyed in 2025.

### Platform Expansion from Payments → Full Financial Stack

- Gig platforms (Uber, Lyft, DoorDash) have all launched **embedded bank accounts + debit cards** for workers, replacing traditional bank relationships for millions of gig workers.
- Vertical SaaS platforms (Toast, Mindbody) are embedding **lending/capital** products leveraging real-time transaction data for underwriting. Toast has deployed $1B+ since 2019.
- Retail giants (Walmart, Amazon) are building **full financial ecosystems**: Walmart's OnePay now spans credit cards, debit, savings, digital wallets, P2P, and BNPL; Amazon Lending offers lines of credit up to $1M via Goldman Sachs.
- Shopify Balance demonstrates the **payments → accounts transition**: a full business bank account with sub-accounts, APY, and faster payouts — powered by Stripe Treasury + Fifth Third Bank.

### Supply Side Is Broadening API Surface Beyond Payments

- **Column, Unit, Treasury Prime** all now offer accounts + cards + lending through unified APIs. Column processes $2T+ in annual TxV and is #1 real-time payments issuer in the US.
- **Stripe** expanded from payments → Treasury (accounts) → Issuing (cards) → Capital (lending). Made embedded components for Issuing + Treasury GA in Jan 2025.
- **Marqeta** grew TPV 31% to $382.5B in 2025 ($625M revenue), diversifying beyond Block into BNPL, flex credentials, and new geographies (Uber UK).
- **Galileo/SoFi** supports 157.9M enabled accounts across card issuing, payments, deposits, lending. Landing enterprise brands (US Treasury, Southwest Airlines, Wyndham Hotels).
- **Mambu** expanding payments to LATAM/APAC and launching Data Lake for analytics. **Thought Machine** competes on deeper customization with higher setup cost.

### Regulatory Tailwinds Are Enabling Non-Payment Products

- **Section 1033 (USA)**: Largest institutions must comply by April 2026. Covers checking, savings, credit cards, and digital wallets — directly enabling embedded account switching and data portability that benefits non-bank embedded finance providers.
- **Account Aggregator (India)**: 263.7M linked accounts and ₹1.6 lakh crore in facilitated loans in FY2025. The framework has moved from pilot to mainstream, embedding into lenders' core credit lifecycles. NBFCs (not banks) lead at 60% of AA-driven lending.
- **PSD3/FIDA (EU)**: Provisional agreement reached Nov 2025; applicable H2 2027. FIDA extends data sharing to mortgages, savings, investments, pensions — creating a regulatory foundation for embedded accounts and lending beyond payments.

### Geography: USA vs. India Concentration

- **USA**: Dominates embedded banking + cards (Shopify Balance, Uber Pro Card, DoorDash Crimson, Lyft Direct all USA-first). Embedded lending through platform capital products well established (Toast Capital, Amazon Lending). Open banking (Section 1033) creates near-term catalyst for embedded accounts.
- **India**: Concentrated in embedded payments (UPI-dominated) and rapidly scaling embedded lending via Account Aggregator. Embedded bank accounts are less developed for non-bank platforms. Key players are HDFC Bank, ICICI Bank, KreditBee, M2P Fintech, Decentro, Setu operating as infrastructure.
- **India market is $24B (2025)** vs. **USA at $115.66B (2025)** — ~5x gap, but India growing faster (12.4% vs. 6.9% annual growth).

### BaaS Funding: Maturing, Not Declining

- Global fintech revenues grew 21% in 2024 (vs. 13% in 2023) — the sector is maturing rather than contracting (BCG).
- Disclosed embedded finance startup funding exceeded $400M in 2025 (YouLend $1B credit facility; Tabby $160M; Swan €42M).
- B2B embedded finance identified as primary growth driver: 49% of future growth expected from B2B(2X) and financial infrastructure (BCG 2025).
- European startups captured 62% of embedded finance funding in 2025.

## 8. Gaps & Unresolved Questions

1. **Product-level revenue breakdown**: BCG's $185B TAM is split across "payments, capital solutions, accounts, card issuing" but the exact dollar allocation to each is not publicly disclosed. Bain's 2022 report gives USA-specific splits but is now 3+ years old. Need updated per-product data.

2. **Embedded accounts adoption metrics**: No public data on how many non-bank platform users have an embedded bank account (e.g., what % of Shopify merchants use Shopify Balance, what % of DoorDash Dashers use Crimson). These activation rates are critical for sizing.

3. **India embedded banking (non-payment)**: Extensive data on AA-driven lending but limited public evidence of embedded bank accounts offered by non-bank platforms in India (comparable to Shopify Balance or Uber Pro Card in the USA). M2P Fintech and Decentro offer infrastructure, but platform adoption data is sparse.

4. **Square/Block comprehensive embedded finance data**: Cash App, Square Loans, Square Checking, and Afterpay together form a full embedded finance stack, but consolidated product-level data is not publicly available with clean segmentation. [unverified — needs manual confirmation]

5. **Galileo standalone financials**: Galileo is reported within SoFi's Technology Platform segment alongside Technisys, making it difficult to isolate Galileo-specific revenue and growth. Combined segment had 157.9M accounts and $114.6M quarterly revenue (Q3 2025).

6. **PSD3/FIDA implementation specifics**: The regulatory text is provisionally agreed but not yet final. Exact data-sharing scope under FIDA and timeline for specific product categories (mortgages, pensions, insurance) remain subject to the standardization process.

7. **Embedded insurance**: Both Bain and BCG identify embedded insurance as an emerging revenue pool, but quantitative sizing is not publicly available. Platform examples (e.g., Lyft Direct's Avibra wellness perks) are thin.

8. **Credit card embedded models**: Bain (2022) noted embedded credit card penetration at <1% in 2021. No updated data on whether this has meaningfully changed by 2025. Walmart's OnePay credit card (fall 2025, via Synchrony) is the most prominent new entrant — impact data not yet available.

9. **AI-driven underwriting impact on embedded lending**: Multiple sources cite AI-powered underwriting as a 2025–2026 trend, but no published data quantifies default rate improvements or volume expansion attributable to AI vs. traditional models in embedded lending contexts.
