## Shift 1: Regulatory Convergence Forcing Unified Trust Infrastructure

### Evidence

**1. Regulation count for a cross-border bank (USA + EU).**  
A bank operating across the USA and EU must simultaneously comply with at least 15–20 distinct identity, privacy, and authentication regulations spanning:

- **USA (federal):** Bank Secrecy Act (BSA/AML), USA PATRIOT Act (CIP/CDD), FinCEN Beneficial Ownership Rule, GLBA (financial privacy), FCRA (consumer reporting), ECPA, EFTA/Reg E, FFIEC Authentication Guidance, FTC Safeguards Rule, CCPA/CPRA (California), state-level biometric privacy laws (BIPA in Illinois), NY DFS Cybersecurity Regulation (23 NYCRR 500).
- **EU:** GDPR, AMLD5/AMLD6, eIDAS 2.0, PSD2/PSD3 (SCA requirements), DORA (Digital Operational Resilience Act), EU AI Act (high-risk obligations for financial services from August 2026), ePrivacy Directive.
- **Cross-cutting overlaps:** FATF Recommendations (40+9), Basel Committee operational risk guidelines.

Each regulation independently imposes identity verification, consent, authentication, or privacy obligations — often requiring the same underlying technical capability (consent management, identity proofing, MFA) built and maintained separately.

Source: KYC-Chain, "The Great Compliance Divergence" (2026) — https://kyc-chain.com/us-eu-compliance-split-2026/; AIGovHub, "Europe 2026 Regulatory Roadmap" — https://www.aigovhub.io/guides/europes-2026-regulatory-roadmap-guide-aml-digital-identity-ai-compliance

**2. Global AML/KYC systems spending: $2.9B in 2025 (+12% YoY).**  
Americas account for $1.45B; EMEA $1B+ (16% increase); APAC ~$388M. This is more than double 2021 levels. Key cost drivers include real-time screening, ML risk scoring, deepfake detection, biometric/liveness verification, and private GenAI infrastructure (tens of millions per institution).

Source: Be Verified / Chartis Research — https://beverified.org/news/aml-and-kyc-spending-to-hit-3-billion-as-banks-struggle-with-ai-growing-pains/

**3. Compliance burden growing faster than headcount.**  
A Bank Policy Institute survey of 20 major US banks (2016–2023) found:
- IT budget allocation to compliance grew **40%** (from 9.6% to 13.4%)
- C-Suite time on compliance increased **75%** (24% → 42%)
- Board time on compliance increased **63%** (27% → 43%)
- Employee compliance hours rose **61%**, despite only 20% workforce growth

Source: Bank Policy Institute — https://bpi.com/survey-finds-compliance-is-growing-demand-on-bank-resources/

**4. Compliance fatigue is industry-wide.**  
- **Europe:** 51% of financial services professionals cite "keeping up with changing regulations" as their top challenge; 44% cite high operational costs (Sumsub 2025, n=225).
- **USA:** 42% cite staying current as their top challenge; 36% lack sufficient internal expertise; 54% still rely on spreadsheets to track security controls (Omega Systems, n=300+, Nov 2025).

Sources: Financial IT — https://financialit.net/news/compliance/compliance-fatigue-emerges-leading-challenge-europes-financial-services-new-sumsub; Omega Systems — https://omegasystemscorp.com/insights/blog/omega-systems-report-exposes-growing-compliance-fatigue-in-financial-services/

**5. EU vs. USA regulatory divergence creates dual-track compliance cost.**  
The EU has centralized under AMLA's "Single Rulebook" with harmonized requirements across 27 member states. The USA is fragmenting — FinCEN's Corporate Transparency Act faces constitutional challenges, while individual states (NY, CA, DE) implement their own transparency acts. Banks operating in both jurisdictions face fundamentally different compliance architectures.

AMLA is scaling from 120 staff / €13.6M (2025) to 432 staff / €96M (2028), with ~70% funded by supervised entities.

Source: KYC-Chain — https://kyc-chain.com/us-eu-compliance-split-2026/; Lexology — https://www.lexology.com/library/detail.aspx?g=1c5fc446-04db-4536-8a2c-5501f9303e03

## Shift 2: Digital-First Identity Replacing Branch-Based Verification

### Evidence

**1. US bank branch network shrinking steadily.**  
Between 2017 and 2025, the US branch network contracted by **14.8%**, dropping from 86,469 to 73,649 branches. Net closure pace has moderated from ~200/month during COVID peak (2021–2022) to ~50/month in 2024–2025, but the structural reduction is persistent and irreversible.

Source: NCRC / S&P Global Market Intelligence — https://ncrc.org/bank-branch-closures-slow-but-shifting-demographics-cloud-the-picture/; S&P Global — https://www.spglobal.com/market-intelligence/en/news-insights/articles/2025/5/pace-of-us-bank-branch-closures-picks-up-in-q1-2025-88699893

**2. India: ATM decline signals digital channel dominance.**  
India's ATM network contracted to 251,057 (from 253,417) in FY2024-25, with private banks leading closures. Meanwhile, bank branches grew 2.8% to 164,000 — but concentrated in Tier-1 centers. The divergence reflects digital channel dominance for routine transactions. RBI data confirms this is driven by surging digital payment volumes.

Source: Business Standard / RBI — https://www.business-standard.com/finance/news/tier-1-drives-new-bank-branches-in-fy25-atms-decline-125122901071_1.html

**3. India Aadhaar eKYC volumes: 23.56 billion cumulative transactions.**  
In FY2024-25 alone, Aadhaar authentication transactions reached 27.07 billion (2,707 crore), with eKYC specifically processing 44.63 crore in March 2025 alone (+6% YoY). Cumulative eKYC transactions since inception reached 23.56 billion (2,356 crore). Face authentication is the fastest-growing modality at 1.42 billion cumulative transactions.

Source: UIDAI Press Release — https://www.uidai.gov.in/en/media-resources/media/press-releases/18883; PIB — https://pib.gov.in/PressReleasePage.aspx?PRID=2124910

**4. Global mobile banking penetration reaching majority adoption.**  
2.17 billion people used mobile banking worldwide in 2025, a 35% increase since 2020. By region: Europe leads at 76%, USA at 61%, with Asia-Pacific generating the highest mobile banking revenue ($740B). Scandinavian countries (Denmark, Norway, Sweden) exceed 87%.

Source: ABSRBD — https://www.absrbd.com/post/mobile-banking-statistics; Statista — https://statista.com/statistics/1452621/share-of-online-mobile-banking-users-in-selected-countries-worldwide/

**5. Digital account opening adoption growing but uneven.**  
Mobile-leading banks opened deposit accounts at 381 per 1,000 active customers in 2023 (+35% from 2021). However, average digital share of account openings remains only 17% globally (McKinsey), with mobile leaders significantly outperforming. Abandonment rates remain high: 68%+ of digital applications abandoned (up from 63% in 2020).

Source: McKinsey — https://www.mckinsey.com/featured-insights/sustainable-inclusive-growth/charts/banks-mobile-mastery; FICO — https://www.fico.com/blogs/shifting-customers-branches-digital-account-opening

### Geographic Variation

- **USA:** 14.8% branch contraction; digital onboarding adoption moderate; high abandonment rates signal infrastructure gap
- **India:** Aadhaar infrastructure provides world-leading eKYC at national scale (27B+ annual authentications); digital-first is default for new banking relationships
- **EU:** Highest mobile banking penetration (76%); eIDAS 2.0 will make digital wallets mandatory acceptance by 2027, accelerating branch-to-digital migration

## Shift 3: Passwordless Authentication Becoming the Security Baseline

### Evidence

**1. FIDO Alliance Passkey Index — rapid adoption curve.**  
Across leading service providers (Amazon, Google, Microsoft, PayPal, Target, TikTok):
- **93%** of accounts are passkey-eligible
- **36%** have enrolled a passkey
- **26%** of all sign-ins now use passkeys
- Passkey sign-ins have a **93% success rate** vs. 63% for legacy authentication
- Sign-in time reduced by **73%** (8.5 seconds vs. 31.2 seconds)
- Login help desk incidents reduced by **81%**

Source: FIDO Alliance Passkey Index 2025 — https://fidoalliance.org/fido-alliance-launches-passkey-index-revealing-significant-passkey-uptake-and-business-benefits/

**2. Enterprise passkey deployment is mainstream.**  
- **87%** of enterprises in the US and UK have deployed or are actively rolling out passkeys
- **49%** of current implementers report adoption rates exceeding 75%, surpassing initial expectations
- **97%** are willing to fully transition to passkey-based authentication

Source: FIDO Alliance Enterprise Report — https://fidoalliance.org/new-fido-alliance-report-87-of-enterprises-in-the-u-s-and-uk-are-deploying-passkeys; Liminal Passkey Adoption Study 2025 via FIDO Alliance

**3. Credential compromise remains the dominant breach vector.**  
- **22%** of breaches in the DBIR 2025 involved compromised credentials as initial access
- Credential stuffing accounts for a median of **19%** of all authentication attempts daily (up to 25% for enterprise)
- Only **49%** of passwords across a user's accounts are distinct (median), confirming widespread reuse

Source: Verizon DBIR 2025 — https://www.verizon.com/business/resources/articles/credential-stuffing-attacks-2025-dbir-research/

**4. US federal mandate for phishing-resistant MFA.**  
Executive Order 14028 (May 2021) mandates phishing-resistant authentication for all Federal Civilian Executive Branch agencies. OMB memo M-22-09 requires Zero Trust architecture with phishing-resistant MFA as a core requirement. As of March 2024, 49 of 55 leadership requirements were fully completed. CISA explicitly identifies WebAuthn/FIDO2 as the gold standard for phishing resistance.

Source: CISA — https://www.cisa.gov/sites/default/files/2023-01/fact-sheet-implementing-phishing-resistant-mfa-508c.pdf; GAO Compliance Report — https://www.gao.gov/assets/d24106343.pdf

**5. PSD2 SCA driving MFA across EU transactions.**  
SCA under PSD2 has reduced card fraud within the EEA. However, total EU fraud losses rose from €3.5B (2023) to €4.2B (2024) as fraudsters exploit SCA exemptions and shift to less-protected channels (credit transfers: €2.20B in losses). Card fraud remains notably higher outside the EEA where SCA is not mandated.

Source: EBA-ECB Joint Payment Fraud Report 2025 — https://www.ecb.europa.eu/press/pr/date/2025/html/ecb.pr251215~e133d9d683.en.html

|---|---|---|

| Tier 2 | Passwordless CIAM platform for competitive differentiation | Consumer expectation now set by Big Tech; falling behind risks customer attrition |
| Tier 3 | Managed passwordless-as-a-service | Cannot staff phishing-resistant auth engineering; need turnkey solution |

### Geographic Variation

- **USA:** Federal zero trust mandate creates policy tailwind; CISA explicitly promotes FIDO2/WebAuthn; banking regulators have not yet mandated but follow federal lead
- **EU:** PSD2 SCA already mandates MFA for payments; PSD3 will likely expand scope; passkeys offer SCA-compliant authentication with lower friction
- **India:** Aadhaar biometric authentication is the dominant model; FIDO2 passkeys would complement for non-Aadhaar transactions and cross-border use cases

## Shift 4: Synthetic Identity Fraud Forcing Identity Infrastructure Investment

### Evidence

**1. Synthetic identity fraud: $35B+ in losses, fastest-growing financial crime in the US.**  
The Federal Reserve identifies synthetic identity fraud as the fastest-growing type of financial crime in the United States. Losses crossed the $35B mark in 2023 (FiVerity data). Unlike traditional identity theft, SIF creates entirely fictional identities by combining real PII from multiple sources, making detection exceptionally difficult.

Source: Federal Reserve Bank of Boston — https://www.bostonfed.org/news-and-events/news/2025/04/synthetic-identity-fraud-financial-fraud-expanding-because-of-generative-artificial-intelligence.aspx; FedPayments Improvement — https://fedpaymentsimprovement.org/strategic-initiatives/payments-security/synthetic-identity-payments-fraud/

**2. Total US identity fraud and scam losses: $47B in 2024.**  
Javelin Strategy's 2025 Identity Fraud Study reports $47B in total losses ($27B traditional identity fraud + $20B scams). Traditional identity fraud increased 19% YoY, affecting 18 million Americans. Account takeover specifically reached $15.6B (+23% YoY), with SAR filings for ATO increasing 36% in 2024.

Source: Javelin Strategy 2025 — http://javelinstrategy.com/research/2025-identity-fraud-study-breaking-barriers-innovation; Federal Reserve — https://www.frbservices.org/news/fed360/issues/021726/fraud-mitigation-account-takeover

**3. Deepfake fraud surging 4× globally, banking among most targeted sectors.**  
Sumsub's 2024 report (analyzing 3M+ fraud attempts) finds deepfakes increased 4× globally from 2023 to 2024, now representing 7% of all fraud attempts. ## Key Findings

1. **The identity infrastructure market is structurally consolidating.** Over $15B in M&A deals (2021–2025) confirms that point-solution identity is being replaced by platform identity. SailPoint's $12.8B IPO valuation demonstrates public market conviction. 89% of enterprises want fewer identity vendors.

2. **Regulatory convergence is the forcing function, not technology preference.** A cross-border bank faces 15–20 simultaneous identity/privacy/authentication regulations across USA and EU. IT compliance budgets grew 40% (2016–2023) while C-Suite time on compliance grew 75%. The cost of per-regulation compliance projects is unsustainable — unified infrastructure is the only rational economic response.

3. **Passwordless authentication has crossed the adoption threshold.** With 87% of US/UK enterprises deploying passkeys, 93% sign-in success rates, and 81% help desk reduction — passwordless is no longer experimental. The question for banks is not whether but how fast. Federal mandates (EO 14028) and PSD2 SCA create regulatory floor.

4. **Fraud has become an identity infrastructure problem.** $47B in US identity fraud losses (2024), with synthetic identity fraud at $35B+ and deepfakes growing 4×, cannot be addressed by standalone fraud engines. 76% of fraud occurs after KYC onboarding — identity must be continuous, not point-in-time. FinCEN's 2024 deepfake alert makes this a regulatory expectation.

5. **AI agent identity is the next frontier — and no one is ready.** 95% of enterprises run AI agents autonomously, but only 23% have a formal identity strategy for them. Machine identities outnumber humans 82–87:1. The EU AI Act creates a hard deadline (August 2026) for identity infrastructure supporting high-risk AI in financial services. This is the single largest greenfield identity infrastructure opportunity.

6. **Bank-as-identity-provider is proven but geographically uneven.** Australia (ConnectID, 10M+ customers) and Canada (Interac Verified, 141M interactions) demonstrate viable models. eIDAS 2.0 will compel all EU banks to accept digital wallets by 2027. The IDaaS market ($10.8B in 2025, 31.2% BFSI) represents a revenue opportunity banks are only beginning to capture. Payment networks (Mastercard, Visa) are moving faster than most banks.

7. **Privacy has shifted from compliance cost to architectural foundation.** €7.1B in cumulative GDPR fines, India's DPDPA mandating privacy-first architecture, and the EU AI Act adding new privacy obligations for financial AI collectively make privacy-by-design a core engineering requirement rather than a legal checkbox.
