## 4. Key Findings

### Market Structure
- The global reconciliation software market is **USD 3.3B–5.5B** (2025, depending on scope definition) with a **CAGR of 12–15%**, making it a meaningfully sized, fast-growing segment within banking operations technology.
- **BFSI is the dominant vertical** — banking institutions drive demand due to transaction volume, multi-system complexity, and regulatory reporting requirements.
- The market is **structurally fragmented**: specialist vendors (SmartStream, Duco, AutoRek) compete with platform vendors (FIS, Fiserv/Trintech, SAP, Oracle) and financial close specialists (BlackLine, Trintech).

### Real-Time Payments Are Reshaping Reconciliation
- The shift to real-time payments (FedNow, SEPA Instant, UPI, Pix, Faster Payments) creates a fundamental mismatch: **payments settle in seconds but reconciliation still runs in overnight batches**. This "reconciliation lag" is now a recognized structural risk.
- Instant payment volumes are growing from **266B transactions (2023) to 575B by 2028** — a 2.2× increase that batch processes cannot absorb.
- The EU SEPA Instant mandate (2025) forces all PSPs to support instant credit transfers as default, triggering infrastructure modernization across European banking.

### Automation Gap Remains Wide
- **56–88% of payments and banking firms** still rely on spreadsheets for reconciliation, depending on geography and segment (Kani Payments, AutoRek surveys — primary evidence).
- **86% of payment firms lack data quality** needed for reporting (AutoRek). Teams spend **~700 hours/year** on data preparation alone before reconciliation begins (Kani).
- **44% of reconciliation errors** trace to human intervention and system integration issues — not data errors per se but process and tooling failures.

### Vendor Differentiation Lines
- **Specialist reconcilers** (SmartStream, Duco, AutoRek): purpose-built, deep matching logic, banking-native; differentiate on exception management sophistication, no-code configuration, and regulatory reporting integration.
- **Platform incumbents** (FIS, Adenza/Nasdaq, SAP, Oracle): reconciliation embedded in broader trade lifecycle, treasury, or ERP stacks; differentiate on end-to-end integration and installed base.
- **Financial close vendors** (BlackLine, Trintech): reconciliation as a component of close automation; strongest in GL/balance-sheet reconciliation for mid-market and regional banks; Trintech's acquisition of Fiserv's reconciliation business signals convergence.
- **Cloud-native challengers** (Duco, AutoRek, Modern Treasury, Validata/ConnectIQ): no legacy deployment baggage; faster implementation; growing by displacing batch-era tools.

### Regulatory Pressure Is Intensifying
- The EU's proposed **new Settlement Finality Regulation** and the **CPMI 2025–27 work programme** signal ongoing tightening of settlement accuracy and reconciliation standards.
- **ISO 20022 migration** across SWIFT, domestic payment schemes, and central bank systems requires reconciliation platforms to ingest richer, more structured message formats — a capability gap for older tools.
- Compliance cost expectations **doubled from 38% to 80%** of firms anticipating increases (AutoRek survey, 2023→2024), directly funding reconciliation modernization budgets.

### Structural Questions for Opportunity Analysis
1. **Where does reconciliation end and settlement begin?** Vendors like SmartStream and Adenza blur the line — the engagement area definition should specify whether settlement systems are in-scope or adjacent.
2. **Nostro reconciliation as a sub-segment**: Nostro/vostro reconciliation is a high-value, high-complexity niche within banking reconciliation. SmartStream, Validata, and Trintech explicitly serve it; others do not differentiate.
3. **Inter-system reconciliation as the hidden volume driver**: Most bank reconciliation volume is inter-system (core banking ↔ payments ↔ GL ↔ card systems) rather than external/counterparty — this is where spreadsheet dependence is highest and vendor penetration lowest.
4. **AI/ML matching claims need scrutiny**: Every vendor now claims AI-powered matching. Independent benchmarks or customer-validated match rates are scarce. Duco's published maturity model ($37.9M savings for 1,000 controls) is the most granular public quantification.
5. **Managed services vs. software licensing**: FIS ORS, SmartStream CoE, and Duco's cloud-only model represent a shift toward managed/as-a-service reconciliation. This changes the competitive frame from "buy software" to "outsource the function."
