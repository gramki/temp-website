## Key Findings

- **The vendor-addressable digital identity TAM for banking is substantial.** Across the six sub-segments, the aggregate vendor-addressable market for banking identity infrastructure exceeds USD 28 B in 2025 and is projected to surpass USD 58 B by 2030.
- **BFSI is the single largest vertical** in virtually every identity sub-segment — accounting for 28–36% of spend depending on the category.
- **Identity is no longer a back-office line item.** 42% of BFSI firms now view CIAM as a profit center (Thales 2025), and 37% of NA corporate banks rank digital identity as a top-3 IT priority (Celent 2025).
- **Authentication infrastructure is the fastest-growing sub-segment**, driven by passkey mandates, SMS OTP retirement, and FIDO2 adoption. Passwordless MFA is growing at 26.5% CAGR.
- **Fraud and identity analytics is the highest-stakes sub-segment** — synthetic identity fraud alone creates USD 23 B+ in projected losses by 2030, driving urgency for identity risk scoring, behavioral biometrics, and device intelligence.
- **Geographic growth patterns differ materially.** APAC shows the highest CAGR (17.1% for identity verification), Europe benefits from regulatory tailwinds (GDPR, eIDAS 2.0, EU Digital Identity Wallet), and the US remains the largest absolute market.
- **India is a distinct micro-market** with Aadhaar-based eKYC processing 37.3 crore transactions/month and a fast-growing identity verification market (USD 494 M in 2024 → 1.72 B by 2033).
- **Passkey adoption has reached inflection.** 15 billion accounts now support passkeys, 87% of US/UK enterprises are deploying them, and three countries have mandated SMS OTP retirement for financial services in 2026.

**Banking-specific context:**
- The smallest sub-segment by absolute size but strategically critical because consent management is a prerequisite for every other identity function (CIAM, eKYC, analytics)
- GDPR (EU), CCPA/CPRA (California), India's DPDPA (2023), and Brazil's LGPD are forcing banks to implement consent-as-infrastructure
- Banks operating across jurisdictions face the highest complexity burden — consent requirements differ by geography, product, and channel
- Consent management is increasingly embedded within CIAM platforms rather than purchased standalone, which makes this market harder to isolate

### Implications for a Converged Trust Platform

A platform vendor that can address CIAM + eKYC + consent + fraud analytics + authentication as a unified layer targets a converged addressable market of ~USD 20–25 B in 2025 (excluding enterprise IAM, which has different buyers). By 2030, this converges to ~USD 42–53 B.

The strategic insight: **no single vendor today spans all six sub-segments.** The market remains fragmented:
- CIAM vendors (Ping, Okta, Microsoft) don't typically do eKYC or fraud analytics
- IDV vendors (Jumio, Onfido/Entrust, Socure) don't do CIAM or consent
- Fraud platforms (NICE Actimize, Featurespace, BioCatch) don't do authentication or consent
- IAM vendors (CyberArk, SailPoint) focus on enterprise/workforce, not customer identity

This fragmentation creates the opportunity for a **converged identity and trust platform** that stitches together customer identity, verification, consent, fraud signals, and authentication into a single decisioning layer — particularly for banks operating across multiple geographies and regulatory regimes.

## Gaps and Unresolved Questions

### Data Gaps

1. **Bank tier revenue pools.** No published source provides identity/security vendor spend broken down by bank asset tier ($100B+, $10–100B, $1–10B). The estimates above are constructed from Deloitte benchmarks and Celent survey data. These need validation through primary research or vendor channel data.

2. **Build vs. buy ratios.** No analyst report quantifies build-vs-buy patterns for identity infrastructure by bank tier. The patterns described are directional, derived from vendor marketing materials, analyst commentary, and industry interviews.

3. **India CIAM market.** India-specific CIAM data is sparse. The eKYC market data exists (driven by Aadhaar), but the CIAM platform market for Indian banks specifically is not broken out in available research.

4. **Consent management — BFSI share.** No published report isolates the BFSI share of the consent management platform market. The market is typically segmented by component and geography, not by vertical in the banking context.

5. **Forrester Wave for CIAM.** No current Forrester Wave results were available through web search at the time of research. Forrester does publish "The Forrester Wave: Customer Identity And Access Management" reports, but the latest edition content was not accessible.

6. **Revenue breakdown by geography within banking.** While regional identity market sizes exist, no source provides banking-specific identity spend by geography (i.e., what US banks spend vs. EU banks vs. Indian banks on identity platforms specifically).

7. **Double-counting risk.** Overlap exists between sub-segments (e.g., CIAM platforms include authentication features; fraud platforms include identity analytics). The aggregated TAM is constructed to minimize but cannot fully eliminate this overlap.
