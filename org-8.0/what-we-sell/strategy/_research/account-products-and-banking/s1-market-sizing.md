## 9. Key Findings

### Vendor-Addressable TAM vs. Total Market

- **Total core banking software market** ranges from USD 12.4B (Grand View Research) to USD 18.1B (360iResearch) for 2024–2025, depending on definition scope. Mordor Intelligence places it at USD 17.2B for 2025.
- **Critical distinction:** Much Tier 1 spending ($100B+ asset banks) is in-house engineering and maintenance, not vendor-addressable. JPMorgan, Goldman Sachs, and other G-SIBs are increasingly building/hollowing out cores internally. The Tier 1 vendor-addressable portion is significantly smaller than headline numbers suggest.
- **Tier 2–3 banks ($1B–$100B assets) represent the primary vendor-addressable opportunity.** Community and regional bank technology TAM estimated at USD 15–20B (Levera Partners), dominated by Fiserv, Jack Henry, and FIS.
- **Vendor concentration in US community banking:** Fiserv holds 25.9% market share (1,155 CU clients), Jack Henry 12.0% (535 clients). Top 3 vendors control majority of the segment. Yet only 53% of banks are satisfied with their core provider (ABA 2025), indicating significant switching opportunity.

### Composite Market Sizing (Vendor-Addressable TAM)

| Sub-Segment | Estimated Market Size (2025) | Growth (CAGR) | Vendor-Addressable Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Core banking platforms | USD 12–18B | 9–14% | Tier 1 largely in-house; vendor TAM skews to Tier 2–3 |
| Digital banking platforms | USD 37–46B | 17–21% | Broad definition includes mobile, web, self-service |
| Cash management / treasury tech | USD 10–20B (BFSI portion: ~27%) | 7–10% | USD 2.8–5.4B BFSI-specific |
| Account origination / onboarding | USD 2.7–6.1B | 15–17% | Cross-industry; banking-specific portion smaller |
| Deposit operations / servicing | USD 5.8B | 13% | Narrower segment |
| Limit management / exposure | Not independently sized | — | Bundled into core banking / risk |

### Growth Dynamics

- Cloud-native core banking deployments growing at 16.7% CAGR vs. on-premise at ~4–5%, signaling a structural shift.
- Digital banking platforms are the fastest-growing sub-segment at 17–21% CAGR.
- India is the fastest-growing geography at 11.3–16.7% CAGR.
- Asia-Pacific overall fastest growing region at 10.6% CAGR for core banking.

### Deal Flow and Modernization Velocity

- IBS Intelligence tracked 1,429 deals in 2025 (up from 1,368 in 2024), indicating accelerating deal velocity.
- Core System Partners tracked 113 transformations globally (2021–2025), with 84.5% choosing cloud.
- Despite strong intent (80%+ planning IT spend increases, 98% of community banks planning modernization), Cornerstone Advisors consistently finds "planned core banking system replacements continue to fall well short of actual deployments" — the intent-to-action gap remains wide.
- UK banks spending £3.3B annually on legacy maintenance alone (24% of IT budgets), with half describing it as a "bottomless pit."

### Vendor Landscape

- **Incumbents (public):** FIS Banking Solutions ($7.3B), Fiserv Financial Solutions (portion of $21.2B), Jack Henry (~$2.4B), Temenos ($1.1B), Q2 ($795M), Alkami ($444M, 33% growth).
- **Indian platform vendors:** Infosys Finacle and TCS BaNCS do not disclose product-level revenue; bundled within broader services revenue (Infosys $19.3B, TCS BFSI TCV $4.0B).
- **Next-gen challengers:** Thought Machine ($570M raised, $2.7B valuation), Backbase ($2.7B valuation, $209M+ revenue, 150+ clients), Mambu ($265M raised).
- Market is fragmenting at the next-gen layer (top 3 vendors hold only 30.1% of transformation deal share).

## 10. Gaps and Unresolved Questions

1. **Average deal size by tier** — No public source provides reliable average contract value for core banking replacements segmented by Tier 1, Tier 2, Tier 3. Analyst firms (Celent, Datos Insights) have this data behind paywalls. Needs manual inquiry or licensed report access.

2. **Vendor-addressable TAM isolation** — No analyst report explicitly sizes the "vendor-addressable" core banking TAM after excluding in-house Tier 1 spend. This must be derived by subtracting estimated Tier 1 in-house spending from the total market. Key assumption: Tier 1 banks (~30 global G-SIBs + ~50–70 large regionals) account for 40–60% of total core banking spending, but much of that is internal engineering staff and proprietary systems.

3. **Limit management / cross-product exposure systems** — No standalone market sizing exists. This capability is embedded within core banking platforms, risk management systems, and treasury solutions. Analyst coverage treats it as a feature, not a market.

4. **Finacle and BaNCS product-level revenue** — Neither Infosys nor TCS discloses product-specific revenue for their banking platform products. Estimates from third parties range from $300M–$600M each but cannot be verified.

5. **Gartner Magic Quadrant data** — The 2024 Magic Quadrant for Global Retail Core Banking exists but is fully paywalled. Summary vendor positioning available through vendor marketing materials (Temenos, Oracle, Thought Machine cite inclusion) but not independently verified.

6. **Forrester Wave for Digital Banking** — No current (2024–2025) Forrester Wave for digital banking engagement platforms was found via public search. The most recent public Wave may predate 2024. Needs verification.

7. **Core banking deal sizes in India** — India-specific deal sizes and implementation timelines for core modernization are not publicly available. RBI-mandated modernization (e.g., cooperative banks) may represent a distinct deal pool.

8. **Tier 2–3 bank in-house build rate** — While Tier 1 in-house building is documented (JPMorgan, Goldman), the rate at which Tier 2 banks ($10B–$100B) attempt in-house core development is not quantified. Anecdotal evidence suggests it is rare but increasing for digital-only subsidiaries.
