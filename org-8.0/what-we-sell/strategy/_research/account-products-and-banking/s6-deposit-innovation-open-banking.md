### Key Pattern

The ratio is roughly **10:1** — legacy cores take 9–18 months for a single product, modern platforms deliver equivalent capability in weeks-to-months. But the real comparison is more nuanced:

- **Product overlay tools (Zafin, SunTec)** can deliver deposit pricing modernization in 4–8 weeks *without* replacing the core. This is a "good enough" path for banks unwilling to migrate.
- **Cloud-native core replacement (Thought Machine, Mambu, Temenos)** delivers broader capability (not just pricing but full product configuration, lifecycle management, real-time processing) but requires 6–18 months for initial deployment.
- **The strategic question** is whether overlay tools erode the business case for full core replacement or merely delay it.

## 5. Account Portability and Switching

### Is Open Banking Driving Switching?

**Evidence from UK (most mature market):**

- CASS volumes are sustained (~1M/year for three consecutive years) but not accelerating dramatically. 2025 recorded 1,054,521 vs. 2024's ~1.2M — arguably a slight decline.
- Open banking is primarily driving *aggregation and comparison* rather than full account switching. The dominant use case remains AIS (80% of API calls) — viewing balances and transactions across accounts.
- PIS (payment initiation) is growing faster (66% YoY) and shifting the user composition, but this is about *payments* not *account migration*.
- The "soft switching" pattern is dominant: 52% of new checking accounts in Q3 2025 were additional accounts, not replacements. Customers are multi-banking, not switching.
- Among those who do switch, interest earned (34%) is the third-most-cited reason, behind digital experience (44%) and customer service (36%).

**Academic evidence:**

- Bank of England working paper: "Customer data access and fintech entry: Early evidence from open banking" — finds open banking facilitates fintech entry and credit access but does not document a clear causal link to deposit pricing competition. Source: https://www.bankofengland.co.uk/working-paper/2024/customer-data-access-and-fintech-entry-early-evidence-from-open-banking

**Caveat:**

- Research (Warwick University, 2025) suggests open banking's "silent revolution" may be stalling — incumbent banks are slowing implementation and acquiring fintechs rather than competing directly. Source: https://wrap.warwick.ac.uk/id/eprint/190288/8/ [unverified — academic paper]

### Business Account Switching

- Business account switching remains significantly harder than personal. Limited data available.
- CASS supports business switching but volumes are not separately reported in publicly available data.

### JPMorgan Deposit Token (JPMD)

- JPMorgan launched JPMD on Base blockchain as a *deposit token* (distinct from stablecoin)
- Represents actual bank deposits with FDIC insurance and interest payouts
- Designed for institutional 24/7 cross-border payments and on-chain settlement
- Source: https://www.jpmorgan-official.com/kinexys/content-hub/deposit-tokens

### Competitive Threat Assessment

Stablecoins threaten bank deposits through two vectors:
1. **Transaction diversion** — stablecoins settling $33T in transaction volume annually could displace bank-intermediated payments
2. **Yield competition** — if regulated stablecoins begin offering yield (which is currently limited by the GENIUS Act's prohibition on interest for non-bank issuers, but deposit tokens issued by banks can offer interest), they become direct competitors to savings accounts
3. **Banks as issuers** — banks can issue their own stablecoins/deposit tokens, potentially creating a new deposit-gathering channel but requiring modern infrastructure to manage tokenized liabilities alongside traditional deposits

## 8. Product Overlay vs. Core Replacement: The Strategic Question

### The Zafin/SunTec Path (Overlay Without Core Replacement)

**What it does:**
- Externalizes product pricing and rate management from the core
- Enables same-day pricing changes, personalized rates by customer segment, benchmark-linked products
- Deploys in weeks (4–8 weeks for MVP)
- Does not require core replacement

**Results (Zafin case studies):**
- Tier 1 US bank: 8-week deployment, >50% increase in CD approved volumes
- Tier 1 NA bank: 1-month MVP, 56% increase in CD originations, 5M+ daily rate calls, 100% STP

**What it doesn't do:**
- Cannot create fundamentally new product types (group accounts, savings circles, complex sub-accounts)
- Cannot address underlying core limitations in real-time processing, event-driven architecture, API-first design
- Does not improve time-to-market for products that require structural changes to the account model
- Creates an additional integration layer that adds architectural complexity

### The Core Replacement Path (Thought Machine, Mambu, Temenos, etc.)

**What it does:**
- Full product configuration flexibility (any product type, any pricing model, any lifecycle)
- Real-time, event-driven processing
- API-first architecture (critical for open banking compliance and BaaS)
- Eliminates legacy maintenance burden (up to 64% of IT budget)

**Deployment timelines:**
- 6–18 months for initial go-live
- But subsequent products launch in days/weeks rather than months

**The tension:**
- Banks under immediate competitive pressure (losing deposits, losing account openings) may choose overlays for speed
- Banks with a 3–5 year horizon and open banking/BaaS ambitions need core replacement
- Some banks do both: overlay for immediate wins, core replacement on a parallel track
- Overlay vendors (Zafin, SunTec) may actually *delay* core replacement by removing the most acute pain

## 9. Key Findings

1. **Fintechs are winning the battle for new relationships.** 44% of all new checking accounts in 2024 went to fintechs. Chime alone captures 13% of new checking accounts — more than any single traditional bank. The absolute number of traditional bank deposits is still growing, but the *marginal* customer is increasingly going to fintechs.

2. **Deposit volumes at neobanks are now material.** Revolut (£30.2B), SoFi ($37.5B), Monzo (£16.6B), Starling (£12.1B), N26 (>€10B) — these are no longer rounding errors. Combined, the top neobanks hold well over $100B in deposits. SoFi grew deposits by $4.6B in a single quarter.

3. **The rate gap is structural, not temporary.** Fintechs offer 10–13x the national average savings rate (4–5% vs. 0.39%). The branch cost burden (~1.5–2% per customer) means traditional banks *cannot* match fintech rates without fundamentally changing their cost structure.

4. **"Soft switching" is the dominant pattern.** Consumers are multi-banking, not switching. 52% of new accounts are additions, not replacements. This means open banking's value is in aggregation and comparison, not in facilitating mass account migration. Banks should worry less about switching and more about losing primary deposit status.

5. **Open banking is real in the UK and India, nascent in the US.** UK: 15M users, 22B annual API calls, 1M switches/year. India: 2.1B linked accounts, 21.7M monthly consents, 913 FIUs. US: Section 1033 first deadline April 1, 2026 — the US is 5–7 years behind the UK. EU PSD3 won't take effect until H2 2027.

6. **Legacy cores impose a 10:1 time-to-market penalty.** 9–18 months for a new deposit product on legacy systems vs. weeks on modern platforms. Product overlay tools (Zafin, SunTec) can compress this to 4–8 weeks for pricing-only changes but cannot address deeper product structure limitations.

7. **Stablecoins are a credible medium-term threat to bank deposits.** $300B+ market cap, $33T annual transaction volume. The GENIUS Act creates a regulated framework that could accelerate competition. JPMorgan's deposit token strategy signals that large banks see tokenized deposits as inevitable.

8. **Deposit product innovation requires modern infrastructure.** Goal-based savings, group accounts, conditional interest, savings circles, and subscription banking all require flexible product engines that legacy cores cannot easily provide. The complexity of these products is increasing, not decreasing.

9. **India's Account Aggregator ecosystem is the fastest-growing open data network.** 78% YoY growth in consent processing, 2.1B linked accounts (60%+ of India's financial accounts), and expanding beyond lending into risk management and early-warning signals. This is creating infrastructure-level demand for modern, API-first account systems.

10. **The Cornerstone data tells an important story about executive confidence vs. execution.** 80%+ of bank executives feel confident about 2026, yet fintech competitors are capturing 44% of new accounts. There is a perception gap between how bank leaders view their competitive position and what the market data shows.

## 10. Gaps and Unresolved Questions

1. **Chime deposit balances remain undisclosed.** Despite IPO (June 2025) and quarterly earnings, Chime does not separately report total deposits held. Since Chime is not a bank (deposits are held at partner banks Bancorp and Stride), the deposits don't appear in Chime's balance sheet. Need to check partner bank FDIC call reports.

2. **Is deposit competition actually driving core replacement decisions?** The overlay path (Zafin, SunTec) appears to solve the most urgent pricing problems without core replacement. Need interviews with bank CTOs who have made the replace-vs-overlay decision and their reasoning.

3. **Section 1033 litigation risk.** The CFPB rule faces industry legal challenges. If the rule is delayed or weakened, the US open banking timeline could shift significantly. Monitor court proceedings.

4. **PSD3 AISP/PISP adoption data for EU is surprisingly scarce.** Unlike the UK (which publishes detailed OBIE statistics), EU-wide open banking usage data is not centrally tracked. The European Banking Authority does not publish equivalent API call volumes.

5. **Deposit token economics are unclear.** If banks issue deposit tokens, do they cannibalize traditional deposits? Do tokenized deposits have different reserve/capital treatment? Is the infrastructure cost justified by the use case? JPMorgan is the only named bank with a live deposit token — need more examples.

6. **Business deposit competition is underresearched.** Most data focuses on consumer/retail banking. The business deposit market (SME, commercial, treasury) may have different dynamics, but neobank commercial offerings (Starling, Revolut Business) are growing.

7. **J.D. Power Banking Satisfaction Study data** was not found in web search. This is a paywalled source that would provide valuable customer experience benchmarking data. Needs manual acquisition.

8. **Cornerstone Advisors "What's Going On in Banking 2026" full report** is paywalled. Key claims about "$3T deposits flowing to fintech investing platforms" need verification against the full methodology.

9. **African and Latin American markets.** Significant deposit innovation is happening in these regions (Bankly savings circles in Nigeria, digital wallets across East Africa) but data is fragmented and hard to verify.

10. **Stablecoin yield restrictions.** The GENIUS Act appears to prohibit non-bank stablecoin issuers from paying interest, but bank-issued deposit tokens can pay interest. The competitive dynamics of this asymmetry need further analysis.
