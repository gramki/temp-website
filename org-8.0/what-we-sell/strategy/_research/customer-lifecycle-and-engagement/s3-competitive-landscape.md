## Competitor Profiles

### Category 1: Banking CRM / Customer Experience

#### Salesforce Financial Services Cloud (Agentforce Financial Services)

- **Revenue/ARR:** Salesforce total revenue FY2025 (ended Jan 31, 2025): $37.9B, up 9% YoY. FY2026 Q4 results show Agentforce ARR reaching $800M (up 169% YoY) with 29,000 deals closed. FSC-specific revenue not broken out; Data Cloud ingested 112 trillion records (up 114% YoY). Company expects to surpass $40B total revenue in FY26. **Source:** Salesforce FY25 Annual Report, Salesforce Q4 FY26 Press Release | **URL:** https://s205.q4cdn.com/626266368/files/doc_financials/2025/ar/Salesforce-FY25-Annual-Report.pdf, https://www.salesforce.com/news/press-releases/2026/02/25/fy26-q4-earnings/
- **Bank customers:** ANZ (deployed Agentforce 360 in 2026 replacing 20 legacy platforms), Absa Group (10M customers, agent-first banking), Al Rayan Bank (UK), FNB South Africa, Paraná Banco (Brazil — 55% engagement increase), Absa Group (Africa). Eight of the top 10 U.S. banks use Twilio/Salesforce ecosystem. **Source:** Salesforce customer stories, Microsoft/ANZ case studies | **URL:** https://ecommercenews.com.au/story/anz-rolls-out-ai-powered-crm-to-reshape-business-banking, https://www.salesforce.com/customer-stories/absa-group/
- **Geographic focus:** Global — strong in North America, UK, Africa, Latin America, Asia-Pacific
- **Bank tier focus:** Tier 1–3; broad coverage but strongest adoption at Tier 1–2 due to integration complexity
- **Product scope:** CRM (Sales Cloud, Service Cloud), customer 360 (Data Cloud), engagement orchestration (Agentforce), analytics (CRM Analytics). Covers origination-adjacent (referral management), customer data/identity (Data Cloud unified profiles), engagement/offers (Marketing Cloud), servicing (Service Cloud, Agentforce)
- **Point solution vs. platform:** End-to-end CX platform; horizontal with financial services industry layer
- **Recent M&A/partnerships:** Agentforce launch (2024–2025) transforming product strategy; 3,000+ companies in first 100 days. No major FS-specific acquisitions.
- **Vulnerabilities:** (1) Not purpose-built for banking — requires significant SI effort and middleware for core banking integration; (2) Expensive total cost of ownership for Tier 2–3 banks; (3) Agentforce still nascent — 169% growth but from low base; (4) Forrester Q1 2025 Wave notes many FS firms "struggle to justify CRM modernization ROI" with Salesforce; (5) Limited depth in banking-specific decisioning (no credit risk, no pricing optimization); (6) Lacks embedded origination/onboarding natively

#### Pegasystems (Pega Customer Decision Hub + CRM)

- **Revenue/ARR:** Total revenue FY2025: $1.746B (up 17% YoY). Subscription revenue: $1.518B (86% of total). Pega Cloud: $696M (up 25%). Net income: $393M. Free cash flow: $491M (up 45%). FS segment not separately reported but historically ~35–40% of total revenue. **Source:** Pega FY2025 earnings | **URL:** https://longbridge.com/en/news/275512717
- **Bank customers:** NatWest (19M customers, 60% of sales via Pega-generated NBA, 3.6B personalized interactions/year across 33 channels), Nationwide Building Society (selected CDH as centralized decisioning brain), CIMB Niaga (Indonesia — first Pega Cloud migration in banking, Feb 2026), Lloyds Banking Group (FICO platform deployment). **Source:** Pega customer stories | **URL:** https://pega.com/customers/natwest-unlocks-customer-decision-hub, https://www.prnewswire.com/apac/news-releases/cimb-niaga-and-virtusa-complete-a-first-of-its-kind-modernization-on-pega-cloud-302698104.html
- **Geographic focus:** Global — strongest in UK, Europe, North America, expanding in Asia-Pacific
- **Bank tier focus:** Tier 1–2 (mega-banks, large regionals); limited penetration in Tier 3 due to implementation complexity
- **Product scope:** CRM, next-best-action decisioning (CDH), case management, customer service, process automation, KYC/AML workflows. Covers engagement/offers, servicing, origination workflows. Does not cover origination underwriting, loyalty/rewards, or account opening natively.
- **Point solution vs. platform:** End-to-end engagement and decisioning platform with deep process automation
- **Recent M&A/partnerships:** Forrester TEI study shows $217M incremental annual revenue and $385M retained revenue from CDH; investment payback in 6 months. Virtusa partnership for CIMB Niaga Pega Cloud migration. **Source:** Pega Forrester TEI | **URL:** https://pega.com/forrester-tei-decision-hub
- **Vulnerabilities:** (1) Historically complex and expensive to implement — long time-to-value; (2) Forrester Q1 2025 Wave positions Pega as "enterprise-grade platform" requiring customization vs. verticalized solutions; (3) Cloud migration still underway — many banking customers remain on-premises; (4) Lacks native CDP capabilities — relies on integration with external CDPs; (5) No embedded loyalty/rewards or origination underwriting; (6) Smaller company ($1.7B) competing against Salesforce ($40B+) for CRM mindshare

Product revenue split: ~81% core banking, ~19% other (including Infinity digital engagement). **Source:** Temenos Q4 2025 results | **URL:** https://www.temenos.com/press_release/temenos-announces-q4-2025-results-22tw4tk10/
- **Bank customers:** 850+ clients on Infinity platform (as of 2022 milestone); 600+ digital clients globally serving 150+ markets. - **Bank tier focus:** Tier 2–3 (mid-size and smaller banks); some Tier 1 in emerging markets
- **Product scope:** Digital banking frontend, customer 360 view, real-time campaign management (AI-driven), Smart Banking Advisor, business banking onboarding (75% time reduction), omnichannel delivery. Core banking is primary product; engagement is add-on.
- **Point solution vs. platform:** End-to-end banking platform with engagement as a layer on top of core
- **Recent M&A/partnerships:** 2026 Capital Markets Day with raised FY-28 targets. No major engagement-specific acquisitions.
- **Vulnerabilities:** (1) Engagement/CRM is secondary to core banking — less investment and innovation vs. pure-play engagement vendors; (2) North American presence is weak relative to FIS/Fiserv/Jack Henry; (3) Customer engagement features tied to Temenos core — not easily deployable standalone; (4) No standalone CDP, NBA, or contact center capabilities; (5) Recent BNPL client termination creating headwinds in FY26; (6) AI capabilities less advanced than Personetics or Pega CDH for decisioning

### Category 2: Customer Data Platform (CDP) / 360

#### Twilio Segment (Twilio CDP)

- **Revenue/ARR:** Twilio total revenue FY2025: ~$4.4B [unverified — needs manual confirmation]. Segment CDP revenue not separately reported but is a core growth driver within Twilio Communications + Data platforms.
- **Bank customers:** Eight of the top 10 U.S. banks by assets and nine of the top 10 U.S. insurers use Twilio as customer engagement provider. Named a Leader by IDC MarketScape for CDP in Financial Services. **Source:** Twilio Segment blog | **URL:** https://segment.com/blog/idc-names-twilio-a-leader-for-cdp-in-financial-services
- **Geographic focus:** Global — strongest in North America, growing in Europe and APAC
- **Bank tier focus:** Tier 1–2 (enterprise banks); growing in Tier 3 via self-serve
- **Product scope:** Real-time customer data unification, identity resolution, audience segmentation, predictive traits (57% usage surge in 2025), data warehouse integration (composable CDP), first-party data activation across channels
- **Point solution vs. platform:** Point solution (CDP) within broader Twilio engagement platform (communications + data)
- **Recent M&A/partnerships:** 2025 CDP Report highlights composable martech trend; integrations with data warehouses (Snowflake, BigQuery). mParticle positioned as competitor.
- **Vulnerabilities:** (1) Not banking-specific — no regulatory compliance or consent management purpose-built for FS; (2) Requires integration with separate engagement/decisioning tools; (3) No embedded banking workflows (origination, servicing, compliance); (4) Privacy regulation changes (cookie deprecation) create both opportunity and risk; (5) mParticle and enterprise CDPs (Adobe, Oracle) compete directly

#### Oracle Unity CDP

- **Revenue/ARR:** Oracle total cloud revenue FY2025: ~$26B [unverified — needs manual confirmation]. CDP-specific revenue not reported. Oracle CX Cloud applications are a declining segment relative to cloud infrastructure growth.
- **Bank customers:** Named a Leader in 2023 IDC MarketScape for CDP in Financial Services Industry. Customer names not broadly disclosed for banking-specific CDP. **Source:** Oracle | **URL:** https://www.oracle.com/cx/customer-data-platform/idc-marketscape-financial-services-industry/
- **Geographic focus:** Global — strongest in North America and Europe
- **Bank tier focus:** Tier 1–2 (large enterprise banks already on Oracle stack)
- **Product scope:** Real-time data ingestion and identity stitching from 100+ sources, golden record creation, no-code segmentation, flexible deployment (data cloud and private cloud/on-premises behind firewall). Oracle Customer 360 for Financial Services adds predictive intelligence.
- **Point solution vs. platform:** Part of broader Oracle CX and data platform; deployable standalone
- **Recent M&A/partnerships:** No major CDP-specific banking acquisitions. Oracle Financial Services Data Foundation Cloud Service supports banking data needs.
- **Vulnerabilities:** (1) Oracle CX is losing market share to Salesforce and Adobe in broader CX market; (2) Perceived as legacy vendor despite cloud modernization; (3) On-premises deployment option is differentiator but also signal of legacy customer base; (4) No embedded NBA, engagement orchestration, or servicing capabilities; (5) Pricing can be prohibitive for Tier 2–3 banks; (6) Fewer banking-specific case studies vs. Personetics or Pega

## Key Findings

- **No single vendor covers the full customer lifecycle.** The landscape is fragmented across CRM (Salesforce, Pega, BUSINESSNEXT), CDP (Segment, Adobe, Oracle), NBA/decisioning (Personetics, FICO, Pega CDH, SAS), loyalty (FIS, Fiserv, Cardlytics), contact center (NICE, Genesys, Five9), conversational AI (Kasisto, Clinc, Glia), and origination (nCino, Blend, MeridianLink, core vendors). Banks must assemble 5–8 vendor solutions to cover the complete lifecycle.

- **Personetics is the only purpose-built AI engagement vendor at scale for banking** (150M+ monthly active users, 130+ FIs, 35 markets). However, it covers only the engagement/insights lifecycle stage and depends on digital banking platform vendors for distribution. This creates a clear gap for an end-to-end platform player.

- **The CRM market is bifurcating** (per Forrester Wave Q1 2025): verticalized FS solutions (BUSINESSNEXT, Salesforce FSC, Creatio, ServiceNow) vs. enterprise platforms requiring customization (Microsoft, Oracle, Pega). Banks choosing platform vendors face higher implementation costs and longer time-to-value.

- **Contact center is consolidating rapidly.** NICE ($2.95B revenue), Genesys (~$2.2B cloud ARR), Five9 ($1.15B) are winning mega-bank deals ($45M+ TCV). Verint acquired by Thoma Bravo ($2B). AI ARR is growing 50–66% YoY across all vendors. Banking-specific voice/digital servicing is becoming a winner-take-all market.

- **Origination-to-onboarding is commoditizing.** Core vendors (FIS, Fiserv, Jack Henry) all have account opening modules; nCino ($541M), Blend ($124M), and MeridianLink ($2B acquisition) provide cloud alternatives. The differentiation is shifting from origination to post-onboarding engagement and lifecycle management.

- **AI governance and explainability are emerging as competitive differentiators.** SAS 2026 predictions highlight "verified intelligence" as a trust metric. Banks are demanding AI transparency across decisioning (credit, NBA, engagement). Most vendors lack robust AI governance frameworks — this is a vulnerability across the landscape.

- **India banking CRM is dominated by BUSINESSNEXT** (HDFC Bank 100K+ users, ICICI Bank 50K+ users), with SBI choosing HCLSoftware Unica. Salesforce and Pega are competing in the Indian market but face challenges against entrenched local vendors with proven large-scale deployments.

- **Bank modernization signals are strongest in CRM replacement and AI-powered engagement.** 26 verified bank signals show active modernization across all tiers and geographies. The most common signal types are: CRM platform replacement (ANZ, Lake City Bank, FNB), AI/GenAI-powered engagement (CIBC, Lloyds, CBA, BofA), and next-best-action deployment (NatWest, Huntington, KeyBank).

- **CDP adoption in banking remains early.** While 8 of top 10 U.S. banks use Twilio, and Adobe/Oracle have banking case studies, the CDP market in banking is less mature than in retail/CPG. Banking-specific CDP use cases (identity resolution across accounts, household-level views, regulatory compliance) are underserved by horizontal CDP vendors.

- **Core vendor M&A is reshaping the landscape.** FIS acquired Amount ($150M+ annual applications) for origination; MeridianLink going private (Centerbridge, $2B); NCR Voyix sold Digital Banking ($2.45B to Veritas); NCR Atleos acquired by Brink's ($6.6B); Verint acquired by Thoma Bravo ($2B). Consolidation creates both integration opportunities and vendor risk for banks.
