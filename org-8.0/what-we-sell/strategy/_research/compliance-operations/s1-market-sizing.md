## 2. Key Findings

### 2A. Compliance Case Management / Workflow / GRC

- **The GRC platform market is massive but poorly bounded.** Estimates range from $64.6B (2025, Custom Market Insights) to $78.9B (2030, NextMSC), with some sources citing a much narrower $695M for "GRC software" specifically. The wide disparity reflects inconsistent definitions — the largest figures include enterprise risk, IT security, audit, and ESG alongside compliance.
- **Case management for investigations** is a cleaner proxy for compliance operations: $7.4B (2024) growing to $20B by 2033 at 11.8% CAGR. This includes fraud, AML investigations, and incident management across BFSI and other verticals.
- **BFSI captures ~24% of workflow automation spend**, implying a ~$5.6B banking workflow automation market in 2025. This includes process orchestration, document management, and operational workflow beyond pure compliance.
- **Banking compliance solution market** is sized at $16.9B (2025) growing modestly to $30B by 2035 at 5.9% CAGR (WiseGuy Reports). This covers the full stack of banking-specific compliance software.
- **Pega's CLM launch** (Dec 2025) positions agentic AI as the next wave in compliance workflow, automating onboarding, document processing, screening, and risk assessment end-to-end.

### 2B. Compliance Staffing Costs

- **$206B in global financial crime compliance spending** (LexisNexis, 2023) — this is the single most-cited figure in the industry. It declined slightly from $213.9B in 2021, possibly reflecting efficiency gains or methodological changes.
- **Labor dominates**: 57–79% of compliance budgets go to personnel depending on the study. LexisNexis (2020) puts it at 57% labor / 40% technology; Veil Governance research says 79% personnel / 21% technology. The gap may reflect different scopes (financial crime compliance vs all compliance).
- **Celent's $190B figure** (2024) — $34.7B IT + $155.3B operations — is the most useful decomposition for our purposes. It directly separates technology spend from operational (largely labor) spend.
- **Banks spend 6–10% of revenue on compliance** (secondary source, directional). With the top 100 global banks generating ~$3T in revenue, this implies $180–300B in compliance costs — consistent with LexisNexis and Celent ranges.
- **Compliance officer salaries** are moderate ($83–92K US average) but aggregate cost is enormous due to team sizes. Banks dedicate up to 15% of FTEs to KYC/AML alone (Pega vendor claim). Wells Fargo added 2,300+ compliance-related positions in a single year.
- **65% of compliance teams spend >20% of staff hours on low-value tasks** (SteelEye 2025) — alert triage, data normalization, first-level review. This represents the operational efficiency gap that workflow automation targets.

### 2C. Exam Preparation Costs

- **Exam preparation is a largely hidden cost center.** No market research firm publishes a standalone "exam prep market size." The cost is buried in personnel hours, temporary staff, and diverted management attention.
- **The APPIT compliance cost breakdown allocates 12% to audit and examination.** Applied to the $206B LexisNexis figure, that implies ~$25B globally for audit/exam-related activities. Applied to the Celent $190B figure, it suggests ~$23B.
- **Preparation timelines range from days to 5 weeks** depending on documentation maturity. Organizations with continuous evidence capture can prepare in days; those without spend 3–5 weeks scrambling.
- **OCC charges $137/hour for special examinations** (2026 rates), with assessment rates rising 2.65–16% in 2025 depending on bank size tier.
- **Wolters Kluwer (OneSumX) and Ncontracts** are the leading vendors in exam management software for banks, but this remains a niche within larger compliance management suites — no standalone market sizing exists.

### 2D. RegTech Investment Flows

- **RegTech investment recovered strongly in 2024**: $7.5B vs $4.4B in 2023 (71% increase), though 2023 represented a ~36% decline from 2022 peak levels.
- **Deal volume is declining even as deal size grows**: only 313 deals in 2024, indicating consolidation and larger later-stage rounds.
- **Cumulative sector funding**: $36.9B across 2,400 companies, 26 unicorns, 709 acquisitions, 84 IPOs (Tracxn).
- **Key M&A themes (2023–2025)**:
  - Corlytics' roll-up strategy (ING SparQ + Clausematch) to build end-to-end regulatory lifecycle platform
  - PE firms taking majority stakes in maturing RegTechs (Marlin → Napier AI, Verdane → Corlytics)
  - Strategic acquisitions for data/AI capabilities (ComplyAdvantage → Golden)
  - Large infrastructure rounds (GSS $47M Series A2 for sanctions screening)
- **RegTech market size**: $14.88B (2025) with forecasts diverging between $34.85B (conservative, 18.7% CAGR) and $70.64B (aggressive, 23.1% CAGR) by 2030.

### 2E. Compliance Operations TAM — Isolating the "Operations" Slice

The critical distinction for our engagement area: **detection tools** (AML engines, screening, monitoring) vs. **operations platforms** (case management, workflow orchestration, exam prep, reporting orchestration, audit trail).

**Constructing the operations TAM:**

| Layer | Market proxy | Size (2025 est.) | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Detection** (AML engines, screening) | AML software + sanctions screening | ~$5–6B | Well-covered by existing banking-ops research |
| **Case management / investigations** | Case management for investigations market (BFSI share) | ~$3–4B | ~50% of $7.4B market is BFSI |
| **Workflow automation** (compliance-specific) | BFSI share of workflow automation | ~$5–6B | 23.6% of $23.8B market |
| **Regulatory reporting** | Regulatory reporting technology | ~$5–7.5B | From existing research |
| **Exam & audit management** | 12% of compliance spending (technology portion) | ~$1–2B | Very rough — no direct market data |
| **GRC / compliance program management** | Banking compliance solution market | ~$16.9B | Broad — includes detection overlap |

**Bottom-up estimate of compliance operations TAM (excluding detection):**

- Case management / investigations (BFSI): **$3–4B**
- Workflow automation (compliance-specific BFSI): **$5–6B**
- Exam / audit management technology: **$1–2B**
- Reporting orchestration (technology layer): **$2–3B** (subset of reg reporting)
- **Total compliance operations technology TAM: ~$11–15B (2025)**

**Top-down cross-check using Celent data:**

- Celent total compliance: $190B ($34.7B IT + $155.3B operations)
- If compliance operations technology = ~7–10% of the $155.3B operations spend being converted to technology, that implies ~$11–15B addressable
- This is consistent with the bottom-up estimate

**Top-down cross-check using LexisNexis data:**

- LexisNexis: $206B total FCC spending; technology = 40% = ~$82B
- Detection technology (AML, screening, monitoring) = roughly half = ~$40B
- Operations technology (case mgmt, workflow, reporting, exam, audit) = ~$40B
- But this includes operational labor being managed/augmented by technology, so the pure software TAM is a fraction — $11–15B is plausible as the addressable software market

## 3. Gaps and Unresolved Questions

### Critical Gaps

1. **No standalone exam preparation market sizing exists.** This is a genuine white space in market research. The cost is real but buried in personnel budgets and temporary labor. Constructing a defensible estimate requires primary research (surveys of bank compliance officers on hours/costs dedicated to exam prep).

2. **The 57% vs 79% personnel split is unresolved.** LexisNexis (2020) says 57/40/3 labor/tech/other; Veil Governance says 79/21 personnel/technology. The difference may be scope (FCC-only vs all compliance) or methodology. Need to obtain primary reports to reconcile.

3. **BFSI-specific GRC market size is not broken out cleanly.** The $64.6B GRC market spans all industries. Banking's share is repeatedly called "dominant" but no source provides a clean percentage. A reasonable assumption is 25–35% based on BFSI's share of adjacent markets, implying $16–23B — but this overlaps with the banking compliance solution market ($16.9B).

4. **RegTech funding data for 2022 is imprecise.** The $4.4B (2023) and $7.5B (2024) are from Statista behind a paywall. The 2022 figure is estimated at $7–8B based on contextual references but not directly confirmed.

5. **Compliance operations vs detection market split is not published by any analyst firm.** The $11–15B operations TAM is our construction, not a cited figure. It needs to be presented as an analytical estimate, not a market research finding.

### Secondary Gaps

6. **Thomson Reuters Cost of Compliance survey** provides qualitative trends (teams growing, budgets flat, turnover stable) but does not publish absolute dollar values for total compliance spending. The report is behind a registration wall.

7. **ACAMS does not publish a public salary survey.** The 2024/2025 data is from PayScale and Lutine Bell, not directly from ACAMS. ACAMS press releases reference salary premiums for CAMS certification but do not publish comprehensive survey data publicly.

8. **Oliver Wyman's "automate 70% of manual compliance work" claim** lacks methodological detail. It appears in a thought leadership piece, not a research report. Useful directionally but not citable as rigorous.

9. **The $270B annual compliance spending figure** (APPIT Software Solutions) is not traceable to a primary source. It may be a rounded-up version of the LexisNexis $206B + additional regulatory compliance costs outside financial crime. Treat as unverified.

10. **No data found on Fenergo or Encompass M&A.** These are relevant compliance operations vendors but specific deal data was not surfaced in this research pass.

## 5. Synthesis for Engagement Area Sizing

### Recommended framing for compliance operations TAM:

**Narrow definition (compliance operations technology only):** $11–15B (2025)
- This is the addressable software/platform market for case management, workflow automation, exam management, reporting orchestration, and audit trail in banking compliance
- Growing at ~12–15% CAGR based on weighted average of component markets
- Projected to reach $22–35B by 2030

**Medium definition (compliance operations spend including labor):** $155B (2024, Celent)
- This represents the total operational cost of running compliance (people + process + technology)
- The technology substitution opportunity within this $155B is the real prize

**Broad definition (all financial crime compliance):** $206B (2023, LexisNexis)
- Total cost of financial crime compliance globally
- Compliance operations is a subset; detection/monitoring is the other major piece

### The strategic insight for Zeta:

The compliance operations market is where the **labor-to-technology conversion** is happening. Detection technology is relatively mature (AML engines, screening). But the operational layer — case management, workflow, exam prep, reporting orchestration — is still heavily manual (65% of teams spending >20% of hours on low-value work). This is the $155B operations pool where technology penetration is lowest and the automation opportunity is greatest.
