## 2. Key Findings

### A. Operations dominate the bank cost base

- **>60% of bank technology budgets** go to run-the-bank activities (BCG 2025, Celent 2024).
- **Personnel costs represent 30–50% of total NIE**; technology adds 15–20%, regulatory/compliance 3–15% (Coforge FY2024).
- **JPMC NIE: $91.8B** in 2024 (up from $87.2B). Citigroup improved efficiency ratio 340 bps only through 20,000-person reduction.
- **The "60–70% of cost base" thesis is directionally supported** when combining RTB technology (>60% of tech spend) with operations personnel and compliance costs. No single source states this as a consolidated ratio.

### B. Productivity declining despite massive tech investment

- **US bank productivity −0.3% annually since 2010**, despite 9% annual tech spending growth to $650B (McKinsey).
- **Mortgage origination cost** rose 127% from $5,100 to $11,600 (McKinsey).
- **Banks achieve only 48% of cost-saving targets** (BCG 2025).
- **60–70% of mid-level staff time** spent on internal coordination, not client-facing work (McKinsey).

### C. Headcount growing in compliance/fraud despite AI

- **94% plan to add fraud/AML FTE in 2026** (up from 88% in 2025); 85% see AI as augmentation (SEON).
- **Financial examiners** fastest-growing banking occupation: 21% projected growth (BLS).
- **Financial clerks** in structural decline: −5% projected, but 102,200 annual replacement hires needed (BLS).

### D. Capacity constraint is measurable

- **SAR filings up 18.5%**, reaching 4.7M/year (FinCEN/ABA).
- **Credit card delinquencies exceeded GFC levels**: 3.07% → 4.25% (Fed).
- **RTP +38% YoY; FedNow +459% YoY** — each new payment rail adds reconciliation/exception/fraud volume.
- **15% of reconciliations** require manual intervention; finance teams spend 30–40% of time on exceptions.
- **18-month average vacancy** for senior compliance roles; 43% of banks report regulatory work undone `[unverified]`.

### E. Attrition compounds the problem

- **Indian private banks**: 22–33% attrition; RBI flags as operational risk.
- **41% of senior compliance officers retired 2024–2025** `[unverified]`.
- **Operations roles pay $48K–$86K**, lagging tech-sector alternatives.
