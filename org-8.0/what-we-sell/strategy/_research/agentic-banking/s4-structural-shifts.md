## Shift 2 — Experienced Banking Staff Becoming Scarce and Expensive

**Thesis:** Specialist banking staff (dispute investigators, compliance officers, relationship managers) are aging out faster than replaced. Knowledge is undocumented. AI agents become necessary not as a choice but as the only viable staffing model.

### Evidence Table

| # | Claim | Value | Source | URL | Verified |
|---|-------|-------|--------|-----|----------|
| 1 | BLS projects banking teller employment to decline 13% from 2024–2034, losing ~44,900 jobs; all annual openings (~29,800) are replacements, not growth | −13% over decade | Bureau of Labor Statistics, Occupational Outlook Handbook | https://www.bls.gov/ooh/office-and-administrative-support/tellers.htm | Yes |
| 2 | BSA/AML compliance: SAR volumes surged 18.5% between Jul 2023 and Dec 2024; 75% of member organizations report higher volumes in Q4 2024, while compliance teams are shrinking | +18.5% SAR volume | ABA / Auriemma BSA/AML White Paper, Jul 2025 | https://www.aba.com/-/media/documents/industry-insights/auriemma-bsa-aml-compliance-in-transition.pdf | Yes |
| 3 | Wealth management industry facing significant talent shortage; advisers quitting the sector, creating a "collision course" with workforce gaps | Talent crisis | ABA Banking Journal, Jul 2025 | https://bankingjournal.aba.com/2025/07/survey-wealth-management-industry-facing-talent-shortage/ | Yes |
| 4 | Median teller wage $39,340 (May 2024); specialized compliance and dispute roles command significantly higher compensation with longer time-to-fill | $39,340 median | BLS OES, May 2024 | https://www.bls.gov/ooh/office-and-administrative-support/tellers.htm | Yes |
| 5 | Top-performing institutions resolve disputes in ~11 days vs. 30-day industry average; 73% of customers say loyalty is heavily influenced by dispute handling | 11 vs. 30 days | ABA / Quavo 2025 Dispute Management Report | https://www.aba.com/news-research/analysis-guides/the-2025-state-of-dispute-management-performance-report | Yes |

### Bank-Tier Analysis

| Tier | Impact |
|------|--------|
| **Tier 1** | Can absorb cost increases but face knowledge-loss risk as senior specialists retire. Investing in copilot tools to capture and encode expertise (Morgan Stanley: 98% advisor adoption of AI assistant). |
| **Tier 2** | Acute pressure. Cannot match Tier 1 compensation for specialized roles. Dispute resolution and compliance are hardest-hit functions. Must automate or outsource. |
| **Tier 3** | Existential. Community banks and credit unions competing for compliance officers against institutions paying 2–3× more. Vendor-packaged AI is the only viable staffing model for 24/7 operations. |

### Geographic Variation

| Region | Status |
|--------|--------|
| **USA** | BLS data confirms structural decline. ABA reports workforce pressure across compliance, disputes, wealth management. |
| **UK/EU** | Similar aging-out dynamics. FCA Consumer Duty requirements increase demand for skilled compliance staff while supply contracts. |
| **India** | Different dynamic — labor is more available but specialized knowledge (dispute investigation, complex compliance) remains scarce. SBI deploying agentic AI partly to scale expertise. |

**Evidence quality: Strong** — BLS data, ABA industry surveys, dispute management benchmarks, wealth management talent surveys.

## Shift 3 — Project-by-Project AI Failing to Scale Beyond the Pilot

**Thesis:** Most banks have isolated AI experiments. The 50th project costs as much as the 1st — nothing compounds. Banks are stuck in "pilot purgatory."

### Evidence Table

| # | Claim | Value | Source | URL | Verified |
|---|-------|-------|--------|-----|----------|
| 1 | Only 5% of enterprise AI pilots reach production; 95% fail to scale | 5% success rate | Analysis, citing industry data | https://januszczak.org/articles/pilot-purgatory/ | Yes |
| 2 | BCG: 75% of banks remain stuck in siloed pilots and POCs; only 25% have woven AI into their strategic playbook | 75% stuck | BCG, "For Banks, the AI Reckoning Has Arrived," 2025 | https://www.bcg.com/publications/2025/for-banks-the-ai-reckoning-has-arrived | Yes |
| 3 | McKinsey State of AI: nearly two-thirds of organizations have not begun scaling AI across the enterprise; only 39% report any EBIT impact; only 6% qualify as "high performers" (>5% EBIT from AI) | 6% high performers | McKinsey / CX Today, 2025 | https://www.cxtoday.com/ai-automation-in-cx/mckinseys-state-of-ai-the-scaling-gap-is-now-cxs-problem/ | Yes |
| 4 | BCG: 70% of AI failures are organizational (people, process, change management), 20% technological, 10% algorithmic | 70% organizational | Applied AI / BCG data, 2025 | https://www.applied-ai.com/newsletter/issue-01-2025-06-26/ | Yes |
| 5 | Fewer than 10% of banks have measurable GenAI use cases in operation despite extensive experimentation | <10% measurable | BCG, "Faster Path to Scaling GenAI in Banking Compliance," 2025 | https://www.bcg.com/publications/2025/a-faster-path-to-scaling-genai-in-banking-compliance | Yes |

### Bank-Tier Analysis

| Tier | Posture |
|------|---------|
| **Tier 1** | Best positioned to escape pilot purgatory through platform investment (JPM OmniAI, BofA). But even Tier 1 banks face organizational resistance. JPM CEO Dimon acknowledges AI ROI is "uniquely hard to measure." |
| **Tier 2** | Most exposed. Running 10–50 AI experiments without a unifying platform. Each pilot requires its own data pipeline, governance review, and integration work. |
| **Tier 3** | Least exposed paradoxically — have fewer pilots to manage but lack the capability to build platforms. Best path: adopt vendor platforms that provide compounding infrastructure. |

### Geographic Variation

| Region | Status |
|--------|--------|
| **USA** | Most experiments, most pilot purgatory. BCG data primarily reflects US/European banks. |
| **UK/EU** | EU AI Act compliance requirements add friction to scaling — each use case requires separate risk assessment and documentation. |
| **India** | Fewer pilots but moving faster in some cases (SBI agentic AI, HDFC). Less legacy infrastructure baggage in some digital-native banks. |

**Evidence quality: Strong** — BCG and McKinsey survey data, multiple independent analyses confirming the pattern.

## Shift 4 — Consumer Protection Regulators Forcing Governed AI Deployment

**Thesis:** CFPB, EU AI Act, and FCA Consumer Duty are converging to create a regulatory environment where ungoverned AI in banking is legally untenable. Banks must deploy AI with embedded governance or not deploy at all.

|---|-------|-------|--------|-----|----------|

| 2 | EU AI Act: AI systems for creditworthiness/credit scoring classified as high-risk. High-risk requirements enforceable from Aug 2, 2025. ### Regulatory Convergence Map

| Jurisdiction | Regime | Key Requirement | Timeline |
|--------------|--------|----------------|----------|
| **USA (CFPB)** | Existing consumer protection law + CFPB enforcement | AI outputs must meet same standards as human outputs; no safe harbor for chatbot errors | Active enforcement |
| **EU** | EU AI Act + EBA guidance | High-risk classification for credit scoring AI; mandatory risk management, data governance, transparency, human oversight | Aug 2025 (high-risk rules active), Aug 2026 (full Annex III) |
| **UK (FCA)** | Consumer Duty + SM&CR | Firms must evidence AI produces good customer outcomes; accountability to named senior managers | Active since Jul 2023; ongoing AI review |
| **India (RBI)** | Forthcoming framework | RBI guidelines on AI expected; current rules require manual oversight of customer-facing systems | Anticipated 2026–2027 |

### Bank-Tier Analysis

| Tier | Impact |
|------|--------|
| **Tier 1** | Best equipped — large compliance teams, model risk management functions. EU AI Act high-risk requirements manageable but costly. Risk: over-compliance stalls deployment. |
| **Tier 2** | Significant burden. Must build or buy governance infrastructure. EU banks face compressed timeline (Aug 2026 full compliance). |
| **Tier 3** | Rely on vendor-embedded governance. If vendors do not provide compliant-by-design AI, Tier 3 banks cannot deploy. This creates a vendor selection filter. |

**Evidence quality: Strong** — Direct regulatory text, enforcement actions, analyst assessments.

## Shift 6 — Domain Knowledge Becoming the Bottleneck — Not AI Capability

**Thesis:** Foundation model capability has outpaced deployment infrastructure. The constraint is whether the bank has an explicit model of how disputes work, what evidence is needed, what policies apply. The "last mile" is domain encoding, not AI capability.

### Evidence Table

| # | Claim | Value | Source | URL | Verified |
|---|-------|-------|--------|-----|----------|
| 1 | Only 38% of AI projects in finance meet ROI expectations; over 60% experience significant implementation delays; core issue is "hiring the wrong people" without deep banking domain + AI knowledge | 38% meet ROI | Caspian One AI in Financial Services Report, 2025 | https://caspianone.com/ai-in-financial-services-report | Yes |
| 2 | 51% of financial institutions cite regulatory and compliance risks as top AI adoption challenge — a domain-knowledge problem, not a technology problem | 51% cite compliance risk | Turing / AI Implementation in Banking | https://www.turing.com/blog/ai-implementation-in-banking | Yes |
| 3 | Finastra: "The last mile to autonomous banks" — activation and integration of agentic AI requires banking-specific domain knowledge woven into AI deployment | Last mile = domain | Finastra White Paper | https://www.finastra.com/viewpoints/white-paper/last-mile-autonomous-banks-activation-and-integration-agentic-ai-banking | Yes |
| 4 | Banks' cautious approval processes stretch 12+ months while AI advances every few months, creating deployment misalignment. Organizational agility — not technology — is the bottleneck. | 12+ month cycles | The AI Journal | https://aijourn.com/ai-in-banking-the-technology-isnt-the-bottleneck/ | Yes |
| 5 | Quavo's dispute platform eliminates 80%+ of manual work but requires explicit codification of Reg E, Reg Z, Nacha, and card network rules — the domain model, not the AI, is the asset | 80% automation via domain codification | Quavo / ABA Report | https://www.quavo.com/news/quavo-fraud-disputes-releases-landmark-2025-state-of-dispute-management-performance-report/ | Yes |

### Bank-Tier Analysis

| Tier | Impact |
|------|--------|
| **Tier 1** | Have domain expertise but it's trapped in people's heads and scattered across policy documents. JPM and Morgan Stanley investing in knowledge platforms, but encoding decades of institutional knowledge is multi-year work. |
| **Tier 2** | Domain knowledge exists but is thinner. Less documentation, fewer dedicated policy teams. Greatest risk: deploying AI without adequate domain grounding, leading to compliance failures. |
| **Tier 3** | Rely on vendor-codified domain knowledge. Banks using Quavo, Pega, or NICE benefit from domain models built across the vendor's customer base. This is a structural advantage for banks that choose domain-aware vendors. |

### Geographic Variation

| Region | Status |
|--------|--------|
| **USA** | Most complex domain landscape (Reg E, Reg Z, Nacha, card network rules, CFPB requirements, state-level variation). Domain encoding is the hardest problem. |
| **UK/EU** | FCA Consumer Duty and EU AI Act require explainability, creating demand for explicit domain models that can be audited. |
| **India** | RBI guidelines are simpler but evolving. Domain knowledge for Indian banking products (UPI disputes, NACH mandates) is less codified than US equivalents. |

**Evidence quality: Strong** — Multiple independent sources confirming the pattern across industry reports, vendor analyses, and analyst commentary.

## Shift 7 — Multi-Channel Continuity Becoming Table Stakes

**Thesis:** Customers interact across mobile, web, chat, voice, branch, API — often within a single issue. AI that cannot maintain context across channels produces worse outcomes. Banks must move from "omnichannel" (multiple channels) to "integrated channels" (one continuous journey).

### Evidence Table

| # | Claim | Value | Source | URL | Verified |
|---|-------|-------|--------|-----|----------|
| 1 | McKinsey: Banks implementing "integrated channels" (mobile as orchestrator) doubled digital sales, tripled cross-sell, raised customer activity 40%, cut cost-to-serve by up to two-thirds | 2× sales, 3× cross-sell | McKinsey, "Integrated Channels: The Next Frontier" | https://www.mckinsey.com/industries/financial-services/our-insights/integrated-channels-the-next-frontier-beyond-omnichannel-distribution | Yes |
| 2 | Distribution costs represent 20–25% of banking operating costs, making channel integration a high-value target | 20–25% of opex | McKinsey, same article | https://www.mckinsey.com/industries/financial-services/our-insights/integrated-channels-the-next-frontier-beyond-omnichannel-distribution | Yes |
| 3 | NatWest agentic financial assistant built on OpenAI — includes voice-to-voice (planned mid-2026) and cross-channel context; AI-generated summaries increased adviser client time by 30% | +30% adviser time | ResultSense / NatWest AI Deployment, Feb 2026 | https://www.resultsense.com/news/2026-02-17-natwest-deploys-ai-across-customer-service-wealth-and-engineering | Yes |
| 4 | Eltropy: ABNB Federal Credit Union consolidating 7–8 vendors into one unified conversation platform across text, voice, chat, video; expects to automate 50–60% of contact center interactions | 50–60% automation | Finopotamus / Eltropy, Mar 2025 | https://www.finopotamus.com/post/abnb-federal-credit-union-chooses-eltropy-s-ai-powered-unified-conversations-platform-to-modernize-m | Yes |

### Bank-Tier Analysis

| Tier | Posture |
|------|---------|
| **Tier 1** | Building proprietary integrated-channel experiences. BofA Erica spans mobile, Merrill, CashPro. JPM investing in unified digital experience. Wells Fargo deploying Google Agentspace across all employee touchpoints. |
| **Tier 2** | NatWest leading with cross-channel agentic assistant (chat, voice-to-voice planned). DBS Joy deployed across IDEAL corporate platform. Most Tier 2 banks still have siloed channel experiences. |
| **Tier 3** | Dependent on vendor platforms for channel unification. Eltropy and Glia offering multi-channel packages tailored to credit unions and community banks. |

### Geographic Variation

| Region | Status |
|--------|--------|
| **USA** | Most advanced in mobile banking but channel integration remains fragmented at most institutions. Branch-to-digital handoff is the weakest link. |
| **UK/EU** | NatWest and Barclays investing in cross-channel AI. Open Banking regulations (PSD2/PSD3) create data-sharing infrastructure that could enable better channel continuity. |
| **India** | UPI has created a unified payment layer but customer service across channels remains fragmented. SBI's agentic AI aims to unify internal service channels first. |

**Evidence quality: Moderate** — McKinsey integrated-channels framework is well-evidenced; bank-specific cross-channel AI deployment data is thinner. Customer expectation survey data on channel switching frustration was not independently verified with primary survey sources.

**Thesis:** Multi-member accounts, delegated authority, generational financial interactions create a distinct AI domain that neither consumer nor corporate banking AI is designed for. Fintechs are moving faster than banks.

| 2 | Greenlight: 6.5M+ parents and kids, $2B+ assets managed, $339M saved, 73M chores completed in 2025 | 6.5M users | Greenlight / BusinessWire, Dec 2025 | https://www.businesswire.com/news/home/20251204763975/en/Greenlights-Annual-Family-Trends-Report-Uncovers-Bright-Spot-in-Consumer-Finance-Kids-are-Building-Wealth-Through-Investing | Yes |

### Bank-Tier Analysis

| Tier | Posture |
|------|---------|
| **Tier 1** | Have family/household banking products (joint accounts, custodial accounts, family trusts) but these are treated as variants of individual products, not a distinct domain. No bank has deployed AI specifically designed for household-level financial management. |
| **Tier 2** | Regional banks have strong family/household relationships (generational banking) but no technology layer to support it. Vulnerable to fintech disintermediation of younger family members. |
| **Tier 3** | Community banks and credit unions have the deepest household relationships but the least digital capability. Family-oriented value proposition is cultural, not technological. |

### Geographic Variation

| Region | Status |
|--------|--------|
| **USA** | Fintech-led. Greenlight, GoHenry/Acorns Early, FamZoo, Current, Step dominating. Banks have not responded with competitive products. North America leads the family banking apps market. |
| **UK/EU** | Revolut Junior, GoHenry (now Acorns Early). Less bank engagement. |
| **India** | Limited family-specific fintech activity. Joint accounts are common but not digitally differentiated. Cultural emphasis on family financial decision-making could create demand for household-level AI. Asia-Pacific identified as fastest-growing region for family banking apps. |

**Evidence quality: Moderate** — Market size data from a single market research firm (Dataintelo); Greenlight data is robust but is one company. Bank-side evidence for household banking as an AI domain is thin — this is largely a hypothesis supported by demographic and market data. Traditional bank investment in this space is unverified.

<a id="key-findings"></a>
## Key Findings

### The Three-Generation Transition Is Real but Uneven
- **Gen 1 (FAQ chatbots)** is mature and ubiquitous — all top-10 US banks use them, BofA Erica has 3.2B interactions.
- **Gen 2 (copilots)** is deploying rapidly across Tier 1 and Tier 2 — Morgan Stanley (98% adoption), Barclays (8M calls summarized), U.S. Bank (Pega Voice AI), DBS (CSO Assistant).
- **Gen 3 (autonomous resolution)** is emerging in vendor products (Salesforce Agentforce, Pega Smart Investigate, NICE Autopilot) but no bank has deployed Gen 3 at scale for customer-facing resolution. NatWest's agentic assistant (25K customers) is the closest publicly disclosed pilot.

### The Scaling Problem Is Organizational, Not Technological
- 95% of AI pilots fail to reach production. 75% of banks stuck in siloed experiments. 70% of failures are people/process, not technology.
- Banks that have escaped pilot purgatory (JPM, BofA, Morgan Stanley) invested in platforms (OmniAI), not projects.
- McKinsey estimates agentic AI could lower banking costs 20%+ (9–15% of operating profits) — but only for banks that scale beyond pilots.

### Regulation Is Converging to Require Governed AI
- Three major regimes (CFPB, EU AI Act, FCA Consumer Duty) all reject the notion that AI errors are acceptable.
- EU AI Act classifies credit-scoring AI as high-risk with mandatory governance requirements (Aug 2025 enforceable).
- FCA Consumer Duty requires firms to evidence good outcomes regardless of whether AI is used.
- Banks that deploy ungoverned AI face enforcement risk; banks that don't deploy AI face competitive risk. The only viable path is governed AI deployment.

### SME Banking Is the Largest Underserved Opportunity
- Half of UK SMEs now use fintechs. 30% open to switching. 50% dissatisfied with proactive advice from banks.
- DBS is the only major bank deploying GenAI specifically for SME clients at scale (DBS Joy, 120K+ monthly chats, 23% CSAT improvement).
- McKinsey estimates 3–15% revenue uplift per RM from agentic AI — highest potential where RM time is most constrained (i.e., SME banking).

### Domain Knowledge — Not AI Capability — Is the Binding Constraint
- Only 38% of banking AI projects meet ROI expectations. The bottleneck is domain encoding (dispute rules, compliance policies, product logic), not model capability.
- Banks with 12+ month approval cycles face structural misalignment with AI capability advancement cycles (months).
- Vendors that embed domain models (Quavo for disputes, Pega for payments, NICE for customer service) create compounding advantage.

### The Labor Market Is Forcing the Issue
- Banking teller jobs declining 13% over the decade. Compliance SAR volumes up 18.5%. Wealth management facing talent crisis.
- The staffing arithmetic makes AI deployment a necessity, not a strategic option — particularly for Tier 2/3 banks that cannot match Tier 1 compensation.

### Family Banking Is a Nascent but Growing Domain
- $2.1B market (2024), projected 17.8% CAGR to $8.1B by 2033. Greenlight has 6.5M users.
- No bank has deployed AI specifically for household-level financial management. This remains fintech-dominated.
- Represents a future AI domain but is not yet a structural shift driving bank modernization decisions.

## Gaps and Unresolved Questions

### Data Gaps
1. **Gartner's original "25% by 2027" prediction** referenced in the brief was not found. Gartner's actual predictions are "40% by third-party GenAI by 2027" and "80% by agentic AI by 2029." The 25% figure may be from an earlier or different Gartner report not located.
2. **Capital One Eno** — no 2025/2026 data found on Eno's evolution toward agentic capabilities. Capital One ranks highest in J.D. Power small business banking satisfaction but Eno-specific AI agent data was not available.
3. **Standard Chartered** — no specific 2025/2026 AI agent deployment announcements found.
4. **PNC, TD Bank** — no specific AI agent deployment data found in this research cycle.
5. **McKinsey Global Banking Annual Review** — the 2025 edition was not directly accessible; data cited from adjacent McKinsey publications.
6. **Gartner Hype Cycle for AI in Banking** — the full report is paywalled; data cited from Gartner press releases.
7. **RBI India AI guidelines** — anticipated but not yet published as of March 2026. Regulatory landscape for Indian banking AI remains informal.

### Unresolved Questions
1. **What is the actual Gen 3 (autonomous resolution) deployment rate across Tier 1 banks?** Public announcements cover copilots and chatbots; autonomous customer-facing resolution at scale is not well documented.
2. **What is the dispute-specific AI deployment landscape?** Quavo/ABA data covers platform performance but not how many banks are deploying AI for dispute resolution specifically.
3. **How are banks measuring AI customer outcomes for regulatory compliance?** EU AI Act and FCA Consumer Duty require evidenced good outcomes — what measurement frameworks exist?
4. **What is the cost-to-deploy for domain-specific AI in banking?** Anecdotal data (12+ month cycles, 38% ROI achievement) but no rigorous cost-per-use-case benchmarks.
5. **How do household/family banking AI requirements differ architecturally from individual banking AI?** No academic or industry research found on this specific question.
6. **What is the cross-channel context-loss rate in current bank AI deployments?** McKinsey's integrated-channels framework describes the opportunity but not the current failure rate.
7. **How will the CFPB's regulatory posture evolve under the current US administration?** CFPB enforcement activity may shift with political changes, potentially altering the regulatory forcing function.
