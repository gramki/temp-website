### 2.1 Transaction Volume vs. Vendor-Addressable Revenue — The 50–100× Gap

- **Bain (2022)** is the clearest source distinguishing the two: USD 2.6 T in US embedded finance *transaction volume* in 2021 generated only USD 22 B in platform + enabler *revenue* — an implied aggregate take rate of ~0.85 %.
- By 2026 Bain projects USD 7 T in transaction volume yielding USD 51 B in revenue (~0.73 % take rate, declining due to margin compression).
- Analyst reports from Grand View, Mordor, MarketsandMarkets, etc. often measure different things: some size "BaaS platform revenue" (USD 19–30 B range for 2025), others size "total embedded finance market" (USD 77–185 B), and many blur transaction volume with revenue. **Every figure in an opportunity analysis must be tagged with what it measures.**

### 2.2 Bain Sub-Segment Revenue Breakdown (US, 2021 → 2026F)

| Sub-segment | 2021 Revenue | 2026F Revenue | 2021 Txn Volume | 2026F Txn Volume |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Consumer payments | USD 12 B | USD 21 B | USD 1.7 T | USD 3.5 T |
| B2B payments | USD 1.9 B | USD 6.7 B | USD 0.7 T | USD 2.6 T |
| BNPL | USD 0.9 B | USD 4.0 B | USD 49 B | USD 265 B |
| PoS lending (installment) | USD 4.2 B | USD 7.5 B | USD 43 B | USD 84 B |
| B2B lending | [see report] | [see report] | USD 12 B | — |
| Banking + cards | USD 2 B | USD 10 B | — | — |
| **Total** | **USD ~22 B** | **USD ~51 B** | **USD ~2.6 T** | **USD ~7 T** |

### 2.3 BCG/Adyen TAM (N. America + Europe, 2024)

- Four product pillars: **payments, capital solutions (lending), accounts, card issuing**
- Total TAM: USD 185 B; current penetration ~USD 32 B
- Payments is most mature; capital solutions / accounts / card issuing represent 80+ % of the untapped opportunity
- SaaS platforms with integrated payments already capture 36 % of SME acquiring revenue → 45 % by 2028

### 2.4 BaaS Market Sizing (Vendor Revenue)

Multiple analyst houses size the global BaaS platform/vendor revenue market:

| Source | Year | Market Size | Forecast Year | Forecast Size | CAGR |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Grand View Research | 2021 | USD 19.65 B | 2030 | USD 74.55 B | 16.2 % |
| Mordor Intelligence | 2026 | USD 28.96 B | 2031 | USD 65.78 B | 17.83 % |
| Research and Markets | 2024 | USD 26.91 B | 2030 | USD 55.41 B | 12.78 % |
| MarketsandData | 2023 | USD 4.39 B | 2031 | USD 16.71 B | 18.20 % |

**Note:** The MarketsandData figures are dramatically lower than peers, likely due to a narrower definition (pure BaaS middleware only, excluding broader platform revenue). The variation across sources underscores definitional fragility.

### 2.5 Geography

- **North America** dominates at ~35 % of global BaaS market share
- **Asia-Pacific** is the fastest-growing region (21 % CAGR, Mordor)
- **India BaaS** (vendor revenue): USD 321 M (FY2024) → USD 1.43 B (FY2032) at 20.6 % CAGR; small base but high growth
- **UK BaaS platforms**: USD 2.1 B (2025) → USD 10 B (2035) at 16.8 % CAGR
- **Europe embedded finance revenues**: EUR 20–30 B (2023) → EUR 100 B+ by end of decade (McKinsey)

### 2.6 Bank Tier Dynamics

- **Under-USD-10B banks (Durbin-exempt):** structural interchange advantage makes them preferred BaaS sponsor banks — they can charge higher debit interchange rates and share more with fintech partners (McKinsey).
- **139 sponsor banks** active in the US (Q1 2024, FedFis data), supporting 634 fintech brands. Median bank has only 2 fintech partners; mean is 6 — heavy concentration among a handful of banks.
- **34 %** of US BaaS sponsor banks are publicly traded (48 banks).
- **Tier 1 / large bank BaaS participation** is limited; most activity is community/regional banks. Notable exceptions: Goldman Sachs (pulling back from Marcus/BaaS), Cross River (USD 675 M revenue), Green Dot (USD 1.72 B revenue with large BaaS segment).

### 2.7 BaaS Revenue Streams for Banks

Per Synctera and industry analysis:
- **Per-account fees:** USD 1–5 per active account per month
- **Interchange revenue share:** 10–30 % of interchange earned
- **Net interest income share:** percentage of NII on deposits or loans
- **Marketing incentives for deposits:** quoted as "X % of Effective Fed Funds Rate"
- A community bank with USD 500 M in assets can support a fintech with 2 M accounts, generating substantial deposit volume and fee income without branch investment.
- Cross River Bank: NII of USD 477 M represents >70 % of total revenue — deposits from fintech partnerships are the engine.

### 2.8 Card Issuing Platforms

- Modern card issuing platforms (Marqeta, Lithic, Highnote, Stripe, etc.): USD 1.8 B in 2025 → USD 4.2 B by 2030 (Juniper Research)
- Modern processors projected to grow from USD 1.5 B to USD 7.5 B by 2034 (5× growth), while traditional processors grow modestly from USD 6.5 B to USD 7.5 B — converging to an even market split.
- Marqeta (market leader): USD 625 M net revenue in FY2025 on USD 383 B TPV.

### 2.9 India-Specific Notes

- India's BaaS market is nascent but growing rapidly: USD 321 M (FY2024) → USD 1.43 B (FY2032)
- Key BaaS infrastructure providers: M2P Fintech (800+ fintech engagements, 200+ banks), Decentro, Setu
- PPI (prepaid payment instruments) market: USD 62.65 B in 2025, but declining wallet volumes (-20 % in 2024) partly due to Paytm Payments Bank regulatory action
- RBI approved 11 non-bank PPI issuers during FY25
- India digital lending expected to contribute 53 % of fintech revenue, reaching USD 190–250 B by 2030 (transaction volume, not vendor revenue)

### 2.10 Regulatory Headwinds Shaping the Market

- **Synapse collapse (Apr 2024):** 100K+ customers, ~USD 265 M in funds affected, USD 85 M unaccounted for → triggered regulatory crackdown
- **Fed cease-and-desist on Evolve Bank** (Jun 2024): restricts new fintech partnerships
- **FDIC/OCC/Fed joint statement** (Jul 2024): banks must reconcile all accounts regardless of third-party involvement
- Market is consolidating around infrastructure providers with strong compliance capabilities
- BaaS providers (Synctera, Unit, Treasury Prime) increasingly positioning as compliance-first middleware

## 3. Gaps and Unresolved Questions

1. **No clean product-level breakdown of the BCG/Adyen USD 185 B TAM** across payments vs. capital solutions vs. accounts vs. card issuing. The full report (gated) likely contains this; public press releases do not.

2. **Bank-side spend on BaaS-enabling technology** (API platforms, multi-tenant core, partner onboarding, compliance/monitoring) is not separately sized by any source found. The Celent "37 % of NA banks prioritize BaaS" data point does not translate to a dollar figure. The core banking modernization market (USD 1.9 B in 2025) is a partial proxy but includes non-BaaS modernization.

3. **India BaaS program count** — number of active bank-fintech BaaS partnerships — is not tracked by any publicly available equivalent of Bobby Button / FedFis. M2P's "200+ bank" figure is the best available.

4. **Bain sub-segment breakdown for banking + cards** in their 2021 report shows USD 2 B in 2021 revenue growing to USD 10 B by 2026, but the split between account issuing and card issuing revenue is not separately provided in the public version of the report.

5. **Gartner BaaS-specific market sizing** was not found. Gartner publishes core banking and digital banking platform sizing but does not appear to separately size "BaaS vendor revenue" as a market.

6. **BCG Global Payments Model** — the 2024 and 2025 editions were published but specific embedded finance / BaaS revenue pool data from the model was not accessible from public URLs. The 2025 report PDF is available at `https://web-assets.bcg.com/25/91/2269153c468ca43668442f055cb0/2025-global-payments-report-sep-2025.pdf` but was not retrievable during this research session.

7. **Vendor take-rate benchmarks by sub-segment** need further refinement:
   - Consumer payments: ~75 bps aggregate (Bain 2022 data; declining)
   - BNPL: 150–180 bps (2021) → 130–150 bps (2026F)
   - PoS lending: 9–11 % of credit value (2021) → 8–9 % (2026F)
   - Card issuing: not separately quantified
   - Deposit/account platform fees: USD 1–5/account/month + NII share

8. **Tier 1 bank BaaS economics** — large banks entering as BaaS providers (e.g., JP Morgan, BBVA in Spain, NatWest Boxed in UK) are not well tracked in public data. Most public data focuses on community/regional sponsor banks.

9. **India digital lending vendor revenue** (distinct from loan origination volume) is not separately sized.

10. **McKinsey 2025 Global Payments Report** embedded finance data was not extractable from the public version. The report notes USD 2.5 T in global payments revenue from USD 2.0 quadrillion in value flows, but embedded finance sub-sections are behind the paywall.

## 4. Raw Notes and Excerpts

### Bain Capital / Bain & Company (2022) — "Embedded Finance: What It Takes to Prosper in the New Value Chain"

> "We estimate the 2021 US market for platforms and enablers at $22 billion in total revenue across payments, lending, banking, and cards. We expect this market to more than double to $51 billion by 2026."

> "Embedded finance already accounted for $2.6 trillion, or nearly 5% of total US financial transactions, in 2021, and by 2026 it will exceed $7 trillion, or over 10% of total US transaction value."

> "Consumer payments account for more than 60% of all embedded finance transactions. In 2021, US customers spent $1.7 trillion via embedded payments, generating $12 billion in net revenue, based on an aggregate take rate of around 75 basis points."

> Revenue model breakdown: Thin stack (front-end integration only) = 0–30 bps revenue; Hybrid = 10–80 bps; Full stack = 60–100 bps.

### BCG / Adyen (2024) — Embedded Finance Report

> "In North America and Europe, we estimate the total addressable market (TAM) for embedded finance is about $185 billion across four core products—payments, capital solutions, accounts, and card issuing. That compares to current penetration of around $32 billion."

> "SME adoption of vertical software reached 59% in the US in 2024, compared with 50% just two years earlier."

> "SaaS providers offering integrated payments solutions accounted for 36% of SME acquiring revenues [in 2024]. By 2028, we expect they will expand their share to 45%."

### McKinsey — "Embedded Finance: The Choices and Trade-offs for US Banks"

> "Banks with less than $10 billion in assets (Durbin-exempt banks) have a structural advantage—the Durbin Amendment allows them to charge higher debit interchange rates, helping them outbid larger banks for embedded finance distribution partnerships."

> US embedded finance market valued at USD 20 B as of 2021, projected to double within 3–5 years.

### McKinsey — "Embedded Finance: How Banks and Customer Platforms Are Converging"

> "In Europe, embedded finance revenues are projected to surpass €100 billion by the end of the decade, growing from €20-30 billion in 2023."

> "Embedded finance channels expected to account for 20-25% of retail banking sales and lending by 2030, up from 5-10% today."

### Bobby Button / FedFis Analytics (Q1 2024)

> 139 BaaS sponsor banks in the US, supporting 634 fintech brands. Average: 6 fintech brands per bank; median: 2. Heavy concentration — a handful of banks support the majority of partnerships.

> Regional distribution: Midwest (41), South (36), West (33), North (29).

### Celent (2024)

> "37% of North American banks have prioritized investing in BaaS and embedded finance" (Celent Dimensions Survey 2024, 63 respondents).

> IT budgets grew 5% in 2024 with expectations for greater increases in 2025.

### Synctera — Revenue Projection Guide for Embedded Banking Products

> Sponsor bank per-account fees: USD 1–5/month
> Interchange revenue share: 10–30 % of interchange earned
> NII share: percentage of net interest income on deposits/loans
> Marketing incentives for deposits: "X % of Effective Fed Funds Rate" or "EFFR minus X bps"

### Synapse Collapse — Regulatory Fallout

> Synapse Financial Technologies collapsed April 2024; 100K+ customers affected; ~USD 265 M in funds, USD 85 M unaccounted for.
> Fed issued cease-and-desist against Evolve Bank & Trust (June 2024).
> FDIC/OCC/Fed joint statement (July 2024): banks must reconcile all accounts regardless of third-party involvement.

### Card Issuing — CardsFTW Analysis

> US issuer processor market: USD 8 B (2025) → USD 15 B (2034). Modern processors (Stripe, Marqeta, Lithic, Highnote, etc.) at USD 1.5 B growing to USD 7.5 B; traditional processors at USD 6.5 B growing to USD 7.5 B → market parity by 2034.
