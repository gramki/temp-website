### Fleet Card as Opportunity Assessment

**Should a general-purpose commercial card platform pursue fleet cards?**

**Arguments for caution:**
- Fleet cards are highly vertically integrated — WEX and Corpay dominate with deep fuel-network relationships, proprietary closed-loop networks (80% of market), and EV charging infrastructure partnerships
- Specialized requirements (RFID at pump, telematics integration, fuel-specific fraud controls, gallon-level data, driver/vehicle ID matching) create high barriers to entry
- Fleet cards represent only ~4% of US commercial card spending, limiting total addressable market relative to AP automation or T&E
- Incumbents are rapidly modernizing: WEX launched unified EV+fuel card (Jan 2026); Corpay integrates telematics fraud prevention; new entrants like Car IQ are eliminating physical cards entirely
- The over-the-road trucking segment faces structural softness (WEX Q4 Mobility flat YoY)

**Arguments for selective entry:**
- 38% of fleets still don't use fuel cards; 25% track expenses manually — digitization runway exists
- Mixed fleets (ICE + EV) create complexity that favors general-purpose platforms with multi-payment-type capability
- The Corpay One model (fuel + general expenses + vendor payments on single platform) suggests convergence between fleet and general commercial cards
- GSA SmartPay fleet contracts demonstrate government opportunity (300,000+ cards via WEX/Citi)
- Fleet card market growing at 10–16.5% CAGR depending on measure

**Assessment:** Fleet cards are better suited as a **partnership or white-label opportunity** than an organic build for a general-purpose platform. The deep vertical integration (fuel networks, RFID infrastructure, telematics partnerships) required for competitive positioning makes organic entry capital-intensive and slow. A platform that can serve as issuer-processor infrastructure to fleet-focused fintechs or provide white-label virtual card rails for fleet platforms would capture value without bearing the specialized distribution burden.

## Key Findings

- **Virtual cards for AP automation are the highest-growth segment in commercial cards.** B2B virtual card transaction value is projected to grow from $3.95T (2025) to $14.6T (2029) per Juniper Research. WEX reported 25% YoY growth in AP automation volumes. Mastercard estimates only $3T of an $80T addressable B2B AP/AR market currently runs on card rails — 96% whitespace remains.

- **The check-to-digital transition is accelerating but virtual cards still face an adoption gap.** Only 33% of companies use checks for B2B payments (down from 42% in 2019), and 25% plan full elimination by 2026. However, while 88% of financial leaders consider virtual cards, only 48% have implemented them — supplier enablement remains the primary bottleneck.

- **Virtual card economics are compelling for buyers but create friction for suppliers.** Buyers earn 0.5–1.5% rebates that convert AP from cost center to profit center, saving ~$25/transaction vs. checks. Suppliers bear ~1.65% interchange fees. Mastercard's 2025 data shows 89% of suppliers struggle to balance card acceptance with business needs — a friction that embedded platform-level acceptance (SAP, Coupa) is designed to solve.

- **GSA SmartPay is a massive anchor program for purchase cards.** $39.7B in FY2024 spend across ~13M accounts, generating $506M in refunds. Only Citibank and US Bank hold contracts (through 2031). The program represents ~$700B in total expected spend over its life — a concentrated government procurement opportunity.

- **Fleet cards are a specialized, vertically integrated market dominated by WEX and Corpay.** WEX ($2.66B total revenue; ~$1.44B Mobility) and Corpay ($4.5B total revenue) control the market through proprietary closed-loop networks, fuel partnerships, and increasingly EV/telematics integration. Fleet cards represent only ~4% of US commercial card spending but are growing at 10–16.5% CAGR.

- **Cross-border virtual cards are emerging as a competitive wedge.** Corpay's cross-border business is valued at $13B (Mastercard invested $300M in 2025). Fintech multi-currency virtual cards offer 80% FX cost reduction vs. traditional bank cards. 60% of enterprises cite FX management as critical.

- **EV charging integration is the next fleet card battleground.** WEX launched the first unified fuel+EV fleet card (Jan 2026) with RFID tap-to-charge at 175,000+ ports. Cardless vehicle-payment solutions (Car IQ/BlueArrow) are emerging. Mixed fleet complexity creates opportunity for platforms that unify ICE and EV payment flows.

## Gaps and Unresolved Questions

- **Mercator Advisory Group data:** Could not access Mercator's proprietary commercial card, purchase card, or fleet card reports. These would provide more granular US market sizing and segmentation. `[unverified — needs manual confirmation]`

- **Precise virtual card percentage of total B2B payments:** The AFP survey tracks check vs. ACH vs. electronic broadly but does not publish granular virtual card share as a percentage of total B2B payment volume. This metric requires Mercator or proprietary issuer data.

- **WEX Corporate Payments segment standalone revenue:** WEX reports Mobility and Benefits segment revenue but detailed Corporate Payments segment revenue breakdown was not available in publicly disclosed summaries. Full 10-K review needed.

- **Corpay Vehicle Payments segment FY2025 full-year revenue:** Only Q3 2024 quarterly figure ($506.8M) was found in public earnings summaries. FY2025 segment-level revenue requires full 10-K filing review.

- **Boost Payment Solutions private company financials:** Boost is privately held; $23B+ processed and 64 Fortune 100 clients are self-reported. Independent verification of these figures is not available.

- **NAPCP proprietary data:** NAPCP P-Card benchmarking surveys and value proposition reports are paywalled. The 70% US P-card adoption figure and spending concentration data are sourced from their summaries but could not be independently verified against raw data.

- **Virtual card adoption by company size:** Data on virtual card penetration by revenue band (mid-market vs. enterprise vs. SMB) is sparse in public sources. This would clarify the go-to-market priority for a platform entrant.

- **Specific cross-border corridors where virtual cards are gaining share vs. wire/ACH:** The 45% US→Europe and 28% US→Asia-Pacific figures describe total cross-border B2B flow, not virtual card specifically. Corridor-level virtual card adoption data is not publicly available.

- **Fleet card market: US-specific sizing vs. global:** The $1T global fleet card figure (Allied Market Research) is hard to reconcile with the $89.9B US fleet card spend figure (Javelin/PaymentsJournal, 2021). Methodological differences (transaction value vs. revenue) may explain the gap, but clarification is needed.
