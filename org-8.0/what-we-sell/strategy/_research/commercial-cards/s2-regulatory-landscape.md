## Key Findings

### Cross-Cutting Infrastructure Demands

- **Real-time transaction data enrichment is the foundational capability** across all three markets. IRS accountable plan rules (USA), GST ITC reconciliation (India), and VAT reclaim automation (EU) all converge on requiring structured, line-item transaction data captured at point of sale and flowing through to compliance/tax systems.

- **MCC-based transaction controls are a universal regulatory lever.** The USA mandates MCC filtering for HSA/FSA/HRA cards (IIAS enforcement). India's RBI uses merchant-restricted acceptance for Small PPIs. The EU uses card categorization (commercial vs. consumer) at the BIN/MCC level for interchange differentiation. Any commercial card platform must treat MCC infrastructure as a first-class compliance capability.

- **Commercial cards occupy a regulatory "exemption zone" that creates strategic economics.** In both the EU (IFR 2015/751) and the UK (SI 2015/1911), commercial cards are exempt from consumer interchange fee caps. In the USA, business-purpose cards are exempt from most TILA/CARD Act consumer protections. This exemption zone makes commercial card programs economically distinct from consumer cards — higher interchange revenue for issuers, but with the trade-off that merchants can decline commercial cards selectively.

- **The CFPB/Regulation Z exemption creates a dual-compliance architecture requirement.** Card issuers must maintain parallel compliance tracks: full consumer protection for consumer-purpose cards and limited protections (issuance + unauthorized use only) for business-purpose cards. Purpose classification at issuance is the critical decision point.

- **India's corporate benefits card landscape is uniquely fragmented by tax regulation.** Separate tax rules govern meal cards (₹50/meal cap), fuel cards (engine-capacity slabs), gift cards (₹5,000 annual cap), and health benefits — each with distinct compliance logic. A unified benefits card platform in India must implement five or more separate regulatory rule engines within a single card program.

- **Government purchase card programs (GSA SmartPay) are undergoing contraction and consolidation.** The February 2025 Executive Order freeze and subsequent consolidation mandate signal reduced card volumes but increased technology requirements per card (monthly reporting, 16-element data feeds, stricter internal controls).

### Regulatory Asymmetries Creating Market Opportunity

1. **India has no equivalent of the EU commercial card interchange exemption.** RBI regulates interchange holistically rather than carving out commercial cards, which means Indian commercial card programs do not enjoy the same interchange premium that EU/UK programs do. This shifts the value proposition toward tax compliance and benefits delivery rather than interchange economics.

2. **India's GST ITC reconciliation burden is unique globally.** The requirement to match every corporate expense against supplier-filed GSTR-2B data — with provisional claims now blocked — creates a massive compliance automation opportunity that has no direct parallel in the USA or EU.

3. **EU CSRD creates a net-new spend categorization requirement that USA and India lack.** ESG-tagged corporate spend reporting is a European regulatory mandate with no equivalent in US or Indian regulation as of March 2026, though ESG disclosure pressure is increasing in all markets.
