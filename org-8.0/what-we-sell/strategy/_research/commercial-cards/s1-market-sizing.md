## Key Findings

### Total Market and Penetration

- **Global commercial card spend crossed $4 trillion in 2023** for the first time, growing 8% YoY, and is projected to exceed **$6 trillion by 2029** (Datos Insights). Visa/Forrester project global commercial credit card spend reaching **$5.07 trillion by 2028**.
- **Commercial cards represent only ~9% of global card purchase volume** and roughly **3% of total business expenditures**, compared to ~35% for consumer cards (Federal Reserve). This massive under-penetration indicates significant runway.
- **Mastercard sizes the commercial payments serviceable addressable market at $80 trillion**, of which only $3 trillion currently flows through cards — implying <4% card penetration in B2B payments.

### Segment Dynamics

- **T&E cards are the fastest-growing program type** (+16% in 2023), driven by rising travel costs and post-pandemic recovery. India saw T&E spending more than double.
- **Virtual cards are the transformational sub-segment**: the market (revenue basis) grows from ~$19B (2024) to $60B+ (2030) at 21.2% CAGR. Transaction value through B2B virtual cards will reach **$14.6 trillion by 2029**, representing 83% of the total virtual card market (Juniper Research).
- **Fleet cards constitute a $1 trillion global market** (2024) with projected 16.5% CAGR to $4.8T by 2034; the US fuel card segment alone is $88B growing at 9.4% CAGR.
- **Purchase cards (P-cards)** hold ~30-36% of commercial card market share, growing at ~7.3-7.5% CAGR, increasingly embedded in procurement automation.

### Vendor-Addressable Technology TAM

- The **technology infrastructure layer** (not card spend) has three addressable tiers:
  - **Card issuance platforms (Card-as-a-Service)**: $1.8B → $4.2B by 2030 (Juniper Research). Leaders: Marqeta, G+D, FIS, IDEMIA, i2c.
  - **Corporate card platform market**: $21.4B (2024) → $51.3B (2033) at 10.2% CAGR. Includes card program management, spend controls, and analytics.
  - **SaaS expense management**: $5.5B → $21.9B by 2034 at 15% CAGR. Cloud deployments represent 73-74% of market.
- Combined, the **vendor-addressable commercial card technology TAM is approximately $28-30B in 2024**, growing to $75-90B by 2033-2034 across issuance, platform, and expense management layers.

### Modern Commercial Card Economics (Fintech Benchmarks)

- **Ramp**: $1B annualized revenue, $100B annualized purchase volume, $32B valuation (Nov 2025). 45,000 customers; 100% revenue growth YoY.
- **BILL Holdings**: $1.5B revenue (FY2025), $329.8B TPV. 493,800 businesses on platform.
- **Marqeta**: $625M net revenue, $383B total processing volume (FY2025). 31% volume growth YoY.
- **Zaggle (India)**: ₹432 crore quarterly revenue (Q2 FY26), 42% YoY growth. 3,674 customers, 3.5M platform users. Employee benefits and spend management SaaS.

### Geographic Insights

- **USA** dominates: ~45% of global commercial card market share, ~75% of global B2B card spending. US commercial card volume was $2.23T in 2024.
- **India** is the fastest-growth story: prepaid card market growing at 28-30% CAGR; credit card spending growing 14-19% YoY; corporate vertical is 36% of prepaid market. Regulatory tailwinds from RBI PPI guidelines and new Income Tax meal card exemptions (up to ₹1.05L).
- **UK/Europe**: stable, compliance-driven growth. PSD2 standardization accelerating card digitization. NatWest, HSBC, Barclays all have active virtual card programs.

### Data Gaps

- **Visa commercial card volume (global, non-US)**: Visa's reported US commercial card volume is part of the Nilson aggregate ($2.23T across all brands). Visa-only global commercial volume broken out from consumer is not publicly available outside paywalled Nilson/Visa investor data. Visa FY2024 annual report reports $13.2T payments volume but does not publicly disaggregate commercial vs. consumer at global level in an accessible format. `[unverified — needs manual confirmation from Visa 10-K or operational supplements]`

- **Mastercard commercial card volume (absolute $)**: Mastercard reports commercial as "~13% of GDV" but does not disclose absolute commercial dollar volume in a publicly accessible free source. Full breakout would require Mastercard 10-K cross-referencing. `[unverified — needs manual confirmation]`

- **Nilson Report granular data (by program type, by region)**: The Nilson Report (Issue 1298, Dec 2025) contains "Commercial Card Products (US Visa & Mastercard Purchase Volume 2024)" tables, but these are behind a paywall. Only the aggregate $2.23T figure and 20.70% share are publicly visible. `Nilson Report, Issue 1298, December 2025, "US Commercial Cards — Amex, Mastercard and Visa — in 2024"`

- **Mercator Advisory Group reports**: No publicly accessible data found from Mercator on commercial card or fleet card volumes. Mercator reports are behind paywall/client subscriptions.

- **Brex financial data**: Brex is private and has not filed S-1 or 10-K. No public revenue or card spend data is available. `[unverified — private company]`

- **UK/Europe commercial card market size (absolute)**: No standalone market sizing for UK or European commercial card technology TAM was found in public sources. The BRI report notes Europe has "stable growth" driven by PSD2 compliance, but does not provide an absolute number.

- **India benefits card TAM (standalone, ex-prepaid)**: The employee benefits sub-segment (meal, fuel, health cards) within India's corporate prepaid market is not separately sized in accessible sources. The overall corporate/organizational segment of India prepaid cards is 36% of the total market, but a benefits-only breakout is not available. Zaggle and Pluxee/Sodexo financials provide proxy data.

- **Virtual card adoption as % of total B2B payments**: Juniper Research provides transaction value ($14.6T by 2029) and Mastercard's $80T SAM implies <4% card penetration in B2B. However, a direct "virtual cards as % of total B2B payment methods" figure was not found in public sources.

### Methodological Notes

- **Market size figures vary dramatically** depending on whether reports measure transaction volume (spend flowing through cards = trillions) vs. platform/technology revenue (what banks and corporates pay for software = billions). The vendor-addressable TAM for technology platforms is orders of magnitude smaller than card transaction volumes.
- **Fleet card market figures ($1T)** from Allied Market Research appear to measure transaction value, not technology platform revenue. The US fuel card market ($88B from GVR) also appears to be transaction-based. Actual fleet card technology platform TAM would be significantly smaller.
- **India prepaid card projections (28-30% CAGR to $2.7T)** include both consumer and corporate segments, and likely measure transaction value rather than platform revenue. The corporate-only share is ~36%.
