# 01 — The Enterprise Solutions Playbook

*A strategic guide for executives building enterprise solutions businesses — and a corrective lens for those applying the wrong playbook.*

---

## Why This Book Exists

Every executive carries an invisible playbook — instincts formed by their formative career experience. When those instincts were shaped by consumer products or B2B SaaS, applying them to an enterprise solutions business produces systematic misalignment: in how opportunities are discovered, deals are shaped, value is delivered, success is measured, and organizations are built.

This book makes the misalignment visible. It covers the full lifecycle of an enterprise solutions business, from problem discovery to strategic positioning, and contrasts how each strategic question is answered differently across four business archetypes: Consumer Product, B2B SaaS, Enterprise Solutions, and SI/Consulting.

The frameworks are industry-agnostic. The examples draw primarily from banking technology, with additional references from healthcare IT, industrial platforms, and government technology.

## How to Read This Book

**For the impatient executive**: Read the Prologue, then Chapters 2, 6, and 9. These three chapters address the most damaging misapplications: how opportunities are discovered (not built), how enterprises buy (not adopt), and why solutions economics differ from SaaS economics.

**For the product leader**: Read the Prologue, then Chapters 2, 6, 8, and 11. These cover the full arc from opportunity to delivery to platformization — the journey that product leaders in enterprise solutions must understand differently than their consumer or SaaS counterparts.

**For the go-to-market leader**: Read the Prologue, then Chapters 4, 6, 7, and 10. These cover where enterprise money sits, how enterprises buy, how deals are shaped, and how solutions businesses scale.

**For the finance and strategy leader**: Read the Prologue, then Chapters 3, 9, 11, and 12. These cover opportunity sizing, solutions economics, platformization dynamics, and strategic positioning — the questions that determine how the business is valued.

**For the complete picture**: Read sequentially from Prologue through Epilogue. The chapters build on each other, though each can stand alone.

## The Recurring Device: Four-Archetype Contrast Tables

Every chapter includes a contrast table showing how the chapter's core question is answered differently across four business archetypes:

| Archetype | Defining Characteristic |
|-----------|------------------------|
| **Consumer Product** | The user and the buyer are the same person. The product sells itself through experience. |
| **B2B SaaS** | The product is standardized. Growth is driven by the product's ability to spread with minimal customization. |
| **Enterprise Solutions** | The problem is discovered before the product exists. Value is realized through delivery, not adoption. |
| **SI / Consulting** | Revenue scales with headcount. Nothing technological compounds from one engagement to the next. |

These tables are the book's signature pedagogical device — designed to make the invisible playbook visible.

## What This Book Does Not Cover

This book addresses the strategic and business model dimensions of enterprise solutions businesses. It does not cover:

- **Product architecture and technology decisions** — How to design the technology platform itself
- **Financial modeling** — Detailed unit economics, pricing models, or financial projections
- **Organizational design** — Detailed org structures, role definitions, or operating models
- **Industry-specific regulatory guidance** — Banking regulations, healthcare compliance, etc.
- **Company-specific application** — How any specific company should apply these frameworks to its own situation

Each of these warrants its own treatment.
