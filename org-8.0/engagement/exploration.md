# Exploration — Before an Engagement Exists

[← Back to Guide](README.md)

---

## Triggers

- **Sales (new customers):** Prospect outreach, initial qualification, relationship building
- **Account Management (farming/expansion):** Existing customer expansion, new product needs, upsell/cross-sell opportunities

---

## ERC Provides Ingredients of Success

The **Engagement Readiness Council (ERC)** provides the ingredients required for a successful Exploration:

- **Exploration Lead** — a named person from engineering who drives the Exploration to its outcome
- **EA** — assigned for technical feasibility assessment and preliminary architecture
- All supporting resources, frameworks, and archetype references needed to evaluate fit

---

## Exploration Lead

- A named individual accountable for driving the Exploration from initiation to outcome (qualify or disqualify)
- Drives discovery, needs assessment, feasibility, POC, and technical evaluation
- Works with EA on technical feasibility and with Sales/AM on commercial shaping
- **Continuity principle:** The Exploration Lead is the preferred candidate to continue as the CP Squad Engineering Lead when the Exploration converts to an Engagement, preserving context and relationships

---

## Sales Role During Exploration

- Nurture and review the prospect relationship
- Assemble ingredients of success from the prospect side (requirements, decision-makers, budget, timeline)
- Collaborate with the Exploration Lead and EA on commercial shaping

---

## Activities

- Customer discovery and needs assessment
- Work Model discovery (first cut) and induction feasibility assessment (EA-led)
- Technical feasibility evaluation
- POC design and execution (when warranted)
- Solution fit assessment against available archetypes
- Preliminary architecture (EA-led)
- Commercial term shaping
- Operating model selection (Fully Managed, Co-Managed, Customer-Operated)

---

## Qualification Gate

An Exploration converts to an Engagement when:

- Customer commitment is confirmed (contract, SOW, or equivalent)
- Scope is sufficiently defined
- Archetype fit is assessed
- Operating model is selected
- Commercial terms are agreed

---

## Disqualification

When an Exploration does not qualify as an Engagement:

- Exploration Lead communicates the outcome to Sales/Account Management and ERC
- Exploration artifacts (scope outline, preliminary architecture, archetype assessment, commercial analysis) are retained for future reference — a disqualified Exploration may be revisited if circumstances change
- Exploration Lead documents key reasons for disqualification and any learnings
- ERC releases any allocated resources (EA time, framework support)

---

## Artifacts Produced

- Scope outline
- Work Model v1 (first cut of the domain's work: commitments, disciplines, scenarios, who resolves what)
- Induction feasibility assessment
- Archetype fit assessment
- Preliminary architecture
- Commercial terms (or draft)
- Operating model choice

---

## Boundary: Exploration → Engagement

An Exploration becomes an Engagement when a **customer commitment** triggers the delivery lifecycle. Until that commitment, the work is an Exploration — not an Engagement. The Initiate phase begins at commitment. Exploration artifacts carry forward into the Engagement — the Work Model v1 discovered here becomes the starting point for Discover; nothing is discarded.
