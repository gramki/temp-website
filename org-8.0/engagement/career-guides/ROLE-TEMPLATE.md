# Role Coaching Template

Use this structure for each role coaching doc. All sections must be grounded in the Engagement Operating Model — no generic advice.

---

## Section Structure

### 1. Success in the Role

- **First 90 days (or first increment):** What to learn, who to align with, what to deliver first. Reference the lifecycle phase you enter (typically Initiate or Discover) and the formation stage that assigns you.
- **What "good" looks like:** Behaviours and outcomes specific to this role in this model. Reference artifacts, governance, and lifecycle.
- **Key relationships:** Name the roles you interact with most and what you need from each.
- **Performance input and the dual axis:** How your execution-axis leader (EO/EPM/Client Partner) provides input to your functional-axis leader. What to document and when to ask for feedback.

### 2. Expected Interactions, Tensions, and How to Navigate

- **Key interactions:** With whom, for what, at which lifecycle phases. Use role names and governance bodies.
- **Normal tensions:** Tensions that are part of the model, not failure. Name both sides' legitimate interests (e.g. "EPM needs predictability; EL needs flexibility within the squad").
- **Guidance to navigate:** How to handle those tensions well — when to align early, when to document trade-offs, when to use existing governance (EPM as first escalation, EO as final, ERC for CP ↔ EO).

### 3. Escalation: Dos and Don'ts

**Do:**

- Escalate to the right place per the [escalation model](../governance.md#escalation-model).
- Before escalating: try the documented first point of contact, document the issue and what was tried, bring proposed options.
- When escalating: give context, impact, and what you need (decision, input, or awareness).

**Don't:**

- Skip the intended step (e.g. don't go straight to EO when EPM should mediate).
- Escalate the same issue on both axes at once without clarity.
- Go around the model (e.g. Client Partner doesn't override AVA; EO doesn't override AVA without a documented governance event).

Add role-specific escalation guidance (e.g. for EA: "EA vs AVA on architecture → EPM mediates; EA vs AVA on verification/release → AVA prevails, EPM mediates, then EO").

### 4. Further Learning

**Internal references:**

- Link to [roles.md](../roles.md), [career-paths.md](../career-paths.md), [governance.md](../governance.md), [engagement-formation.md](../engagement-formation.md), and other guide docs relevant to this role.

**External references:**

- 1-3 curated entries with title, stable link, and one-line "why relevant to this role in the Engagement Operating Model."
- Only include references that suit this model. Do not add generic titles.

**For EA and AVA only:** Add a short **Architecture vs technical architecture** subsection distinguishing solution/enterprise architecture (stakeholders, outcomes, multiple domains) from technical/software architecture (structure, behaviour, components).

---

## Checklist Before Publishing

- [ ] Every section references at least one concrete construct from the guide.
- [ ] Business context is reflected where it explains why the role exists or why a behaviour matters.
- [ ] No standalone platitudes; every piece of advice tied to a specific interaction, decision, or artifact.
- [ ] Escalation guidance names actual roles and governance and points to [governance.md](../governance.md).
- [ ] Interactions/tensions name both sides' legitimate interests and reference specific lifecycle phases or decisions.
- [ ] Further learning entries have stable links and one-line "why relevant" notes tied to the model.
