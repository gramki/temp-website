# Role Coaching Template

Use this structure for each role coaching doc. All sections must be grounded in the Engagement Operating Model — no generic advice.

---

## Document Structure

### Header and Navigation

```
# {Role Name} — Role Coaching Guide

[← Back to Career Guides](../README.md) | [Role Definition](../../roles.md#{anchor})
```

### Introduction

3-5 lines covering:

- **Purpose:** One sentence defining what the role does, drawn from [roles.md](../roles.md).
- **Scope:** Per-client, per-Engagement, or per-squad.
- **Position:** Where the role sits in the execution-axis hierarchy (who it reports to, who reports to it).
- **Function:** Which functional track the role belongs to on the career axis.

The Introduction makes the document nearly self-sufficient — a reader should not need to leave the doc to understand what the role is.

**For EA and AVA only:** Add a conceptual preamble after the Introduction and before section 1. EA: "Architecture vs. Technical Architecture." AVA: "The AVA Leads the Verification Squad."

### 1. Empowerment

What authority this role carries. What decisions it can make unilaterally. What mechanisms or artefacts it owns. What it *cannot* override. Drawn from [roles.md](../roles.md) authority definitions and the operating model.

### 2. Assignment

How the role is assigned (ERC / EO / PPM), at which formation stage, from which function, and typical scope. Reference [engagement-formation.md](../engagement-formation.md) for the three-stage assignment model.

### 3. Ambition

What "great" looks like — beyond the baseline. First 90 days (or first increment) entry point: what to learn, who to align with, what to deliver first. Career trajectory and progression within the function. The aspiration, not just the minimum.

### 4. Commitments

SLA-based delivery commitments across the applicable dimensions:

- **Quality** — correctness, standards, verification, certification
- **Predictability** — ability to forecast, deliver on schedule, no surprises
- **Velocity** — speed of delivery, throughput
- **Fitment** — how well the solution fits the customer need, platform fit, archetype alignment
- **Cost** — delivery cost, resource consumption, commercial alignment

Each role covers only the dimensions it is directly accountable for. Commitments are tiered by contractual connection:

- **Tier 1 — Direct contractual:** Role interfaces with the contract, the client, or Account Management.
- **Tier 2 — Contractual mechanism:** Role's output serves as evidence or precondition for contractual obligations.
- **Tier 3 — Roll-up:** Role's SLAs aggregate upward to Tier 1/2 roles.

Connect to the [accountability framework](../../people-and-culture/accountability.md) to distinguish outcome-based vs. commitment-based accountability where appropriate.

### 5. Collaboration

Key relationships table (role, what you need from them, what they need from you) plus interaction tensions with navigation guidance. Name both sides' legitimate interests (e.g. "EPM needs predictability; EL needs squad autonomy"). Reference specific lifecycle phases, governance bodies, and decisions.

### 6. Management

Dual-axis reporting. Who the role reports to on each axis:

- **Execution axis:** Per-Engagement; who evaluates Engagement-level effectiveness.
- **Functional/career axis:** Permanent; who evaluates multi-Engagement career growth.

How performance input flows. What to document and when to request feedback. Reference the [dual-axis reporting model](../roles.md#dual-axis-reporting-model).

### 7. Accountability

Escalation model: Dos and Don'ts. When and to whom to escalate, per the [escalation model](../governance.md#escalation-model). What to do before escalating (try the documented first point of contact, document the issue, bring options). What not to do (skip steps, escalate on both axes simultaneously, go around the model).

What happens when commitments are missed — connect to the [accountability framework](../../people-and-culture/accountability.md) (learning loop for outcome-based gaps, reliability loop for commitment-based gaps). Governance events (e.g. AVA release-block override).

Add role-specific escalation guidance (e.g. for EA: "EA vs AVA on architecture → EPM mediates; EA vs AVA on verification/release → AVA prevails, EPM mediates, then EO").

### 8. Objectives in Perspective

How this role's objectives fit within the broader Engagement, client, and organisational context. What the role should care about beyond its immediate scope. How the role's SLAs connect to the overall Engagement success, client satisfaction, and platform evolution.

### Further Learning

**Internal references:**

- Link to [roles.md](../roles.md), [career-paths.md](../career-paths.md), [governance.md](../governance.md), [engagement-formation.md](../engagement-formation.md), and other guide docs relevant to this role.

**External references:**

- 1-3 curated entries with title, stable link, and one-line "why relevant to this role in the Engagement Operating Model."
- Only include references that suit this model. Do not add generic titles.

## Checklist Before Publishing

- [ ] Introduction covers Purpose, Scope, Position, Function — reader can understand the role without leaving the doc.
- [ ] Every section references at least one concrete construct from the guide (lifecycle phase, governance body, artefact, escalation path).
- [ ] Commitments section names specific SLA dimensions with contractual tier.
- [ ] Business context is reflected where it explains why the role exists or why a behaviour matters.
- [ ] No standalone platitudes; every piece of advice tied to a specific interaction, decision, or artefact.
- [ ] Escalation guidance names actual roles and governance and points to [governance.md](../governance.md).
- [ ] Collaboration tensions name both sides' legitimate interests and reference specific lifecycle phases or decisions.
- [ ] Further learning entries have stable links and one-line "why relevant" notes tied to the model.
