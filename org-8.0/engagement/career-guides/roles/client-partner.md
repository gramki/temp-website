## 1. Success in the Role

### First 90 Days (or First Engagement with a New Client)

- **Learn the client's organisation:** Map every stakeholder who has influence over Engagement outcomes — decision-makers, budget holders, technical counterparts, change sponsors. Understand the client's internal politics and governance cadence.
- **Align with EO(s):** Meet each Engagement Owner within your client scope. Establish the authority boundary early — you own client-facing decisions (stakeholder management, commercial alignment, relationship strategy); EO retains full internal delivery authority. Agree on a communication protocol: what the client hears from you directly, what they hear from EPM, and how you stay in sync.
- **Brief your CPA(s):** Set expectations for governance preparation, stakeholder tracking, and cross-Engagement coordination. The CPA is your operational backbone — invest in the relationship.
- **Understand the commercial context:** Review the contract terms, scope, and any existing delivery-commercial misalignment. Meet with Account Management leadership to understand the broader commercial relationship.

### What "Good" Looks Like

- The client has a single, trusted point of strategic contact who speaks with authority about the relationship, commercial alignment, and Engagement direction.
- EOs can focus on internal delivery because they trust the Client Partner to manage client expectations, governance, and escalation.
- Cross-Engagement coordination (for multi-Engagement clients) happens through you and your CPA, not through ad-hoc EO-to-EO conversations.
- Governance meetings are well-prepared (CPA prepares), well-run (you lead), and result in clear actions (you follow up).
- Commercial and delivery are in sync — no surprises for Account Management or the client.

### Key Relationships

| Role | What You Need from Them | What They Need from You |
|------|------------------------|------------------------|
| EO | Honest delivery assessment, early warning of risks, recommended options for client-impacting decisions | Client context, stakeholder priorities, commercial constraints, timely escalation of client concerns |
| CPA | Governance preparation, stakeholder tracking, cross-Engagement status consolidation | Clear delegation, feedback on quality, career development investment |
| EPM | Customer-facing delivery communication, Engagement Success progress | Aligned messaging to client, access to client stakeholders for adoption/enablement |
| Account Management | Commercial strategy, contract terms, renewal/expansion context | Client relationship health, early warning of commercial misalignment |
| ERC | Role assignment, capacity, escalation resolution for CP ↔ EO disputes | Client portfolio view, risk signals, formation feedback |

### Performance Input and the Dual Axis

Your functional home is **Account Management**. AM leadership evaluates your career growth across client assignments — client relationship depth, commercial alignment quality, multi-Engagement coordination. EO(s) and EPM(s) provide execution-axis input on your effectiveness within specific Engagements. Request explicit feedback from EOs after each Engagement milestone (go-live, Transfer, Complete).

## 2. Expected Interactions, Tensions, and How to Navigate

### Client Partner ↔ EO

**Tension:** You want to maximise client satisfaction and say "yes" to client requests. EO owns delivery feasibility and may say "not possible in this timeline" or "not ready for release." Both positions are legitimate — the client relationship matters and engineering integrity matters.

**How to navigate:**
- When delivery decisions affect client commitments → EO recommends; you decide. Ensure you understand the technical rationale before overriding.
- When client demands affect delivery feasibility → bring to EO; EO assesses. Do not commit to the client before the assessment.
- Never override AVA release-block authority, even under extreme client pressure. AVA's certification is independent. If you disagree with a release block, escalate through EPM → EO. An EO override is a governance event.

### Client Partner ↔ EPM

**Tension:** Both of you are customer-facing, but with different scopes. EPM owns delivery communication and the "what's happening" narrative. You own relationship strategy and the "what it means for our partnership" narrative. Overlap is inevitable.

**How to navigate:**
- Agree early on the communication split: EPM leads delivery status; you lead strategic and commercial conversations. Both attend client governance, but with defined roles.
- If a client escalation arrives to both of you, coordinate before responding. Contradictory messages destroy client trust faster than a delayed response.

### Client Partner ↔ Account Management

**Tension:** Account Management owns the commercial relationship and contract. You own the client relationship at the Engagement level. Scope changes, contract amendments, and renewal conversations can create friction if you and AM are not aligned.

**How to navigate:**
- You surface commercial misalignment; AM negotiates. Do not negotiate contract terms directly with the client — flag to AM and coordinate.
- Ensure AM has visibility into delivery progress that affects commercials. Surprises at renewal are failures of coordination, not of delivery.

## 3. Escalation: Dos and Don'ts

**Do:**

- Escalate CP ↔ EO disagreements to **ERC** — ERC assigned both roles and is the designated escalation endpoint ([governance.md](../../governance.md#escalation-model)).
- Before escalating: document the issue, what was tried (direct conversation with EO), options, and your recommended path.
- Escalate client-side risks to EO and EPM early — giving them time to assess and recommend, rather than presenting a fait accompli.

**Don't:**

- Go directly to Engineering leadership, Architecture leadership, or PAC to override EO's delivery decisions. The execution axis goes through EO, not around EO.
- Override AVA release-block authority. This is non-negotiable. If you believe a release block is wrong, the path is: EPM mediates → EO decides. An EO override is a documented governance event.
- Commit delivery timelines to the client before EPM and EO have confirmed feasibility. Saying "yes" to the client and then asking the delivery team to make it work is a trust-destroying pattern.
- Escalate the same issue to AM leadership and ERC simultaneously without clarity on what each body should decide.
