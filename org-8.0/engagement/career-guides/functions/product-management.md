# Product Management — Function Coaching Guide

[← Back to Career Guides](../README.md) | [Career Paths — Product Management](../../career-paths.md#3c-product-management)

---

## Purpose

This guide helps product managers at every level understand what the Engagement Operating Model expects of them and how to grow within the Product Management function. Product managers in this model work at the intersection of customer needs and platform capability — the fundamental question is *"what does the customer need beyond what the Product Line already provides, and how do we close that gap?"* The Product function supplies the **EPO** and **Squad PM** roles.

---

## Associate Product Manager

### Required Craft, Skills, Competencies

- **Craft:** Decompose EPO-level requirements into squad-executable work items with clear acceptance criteria. Understand the Product Line's existing capability well enough to distinguish "already in the box" from "needs Engagement-specific work." Write user stories and acceptance criteria that engineers can implement without ambiguity.
- **Skills:** Manage a squad backlog in coordination with the EL — prioritize across EPO requirements, EA inner source work, AVA verification requirements, and tech debt. Communicate progress and blockers to EPM in the format the integrated progress view needs (scope, timeline, risk, dependencies).
- **Competencies:** Distinguish product decisions (what to build, in what order) from process decisions (how to run the sprint) — the Squad PM owns the former; the Scrum Master owns the latter. Resist the temptation to manage process; focus on what the squad delivers, not how it runs.

### Roles Expected to Play and Challenges

- **Squad PM** on a CP, Studio, or PL Squad.
- **Challenge:** Prioritising across competing inputs. EPO requirements, EA inner source contributions, AVA verification requirements, and squad tech debt all compete for sprint capacity. The Squad PM must make the trade-off transparent to the EL and EPM — hidden prioritization creates surprises.
- **Challenge:** Joining a temporary squad with unfamiliar team dynamics. Build cadence quickly by understanding the squad's context (CP vs. Studio vs. PL), the archetype in use, and the EA's gap analysis.

### How to Learn

- **On the job:** Serve as Squad PM in at least two different squad contexts (e.g. CP Squad, then Studio Squad) to understand how requirements flow differs. In a CP Squad, requirements come from EPO's customer discovery. In a Studio Squad, requirements come from the EA's integration design.
- **Feedback:** EL provides execution-axis input on your backlog management and prioritisation quality. Product leadership evaluates your growth across Engagements.
- **Knowledge sharing:** Participate in EPO-led requirements review sessions. Study how the EPO translates customer needs into actionable requirements.
- **External reading:** *Inspired* (Cagan) — [svpg.com/inspired](https://www.svpg.com/inspired-how-to-create-tech-products-customers-love/) — product discovery and delivery, applicable to the customer-facing product work in the EPO/Squad PM flow.

## Product Manager

### Required Craft, Skills, Competencies

- **Craft:** Run requirements decomposition sessions — take EPO's Engagement-level requirements and break them into squad-level backlogs for multiple squads. Understand the architecture well enough to know when a requirement needs EA review before it can be decomposed. Identify requirements that affect the operating model choice (Fully Managed vs. Co-Managed vs. Customer-Operated) and flag them to EPO and EPM.
- **Skills:** Manage cross-squad requirements dependencies — when one squad's work blocks another's, surface the dependency to EPM early with impact and options. Provide input to EPO on customer-side gaps discovered during Build (requirements change as the customer sees the product taking shape). Coordinate with EA on inner source requirements — understand which inner source contributions compete with Engagement delivery and help EA and EL find realistic timelines.
- **Competencies:** Navigate the EPO-Squad PM boundary — EPO determines what the customer needs; Squad PM determines how to prioritize and execute. Push back on EPO when requirements are too vague to decompose, and push back on EL when delivery pressure causes requirements to be skipped rather than deferred.

### Roles Expected to Play and Challenges

- **Squad PM** on complex squads. May serve as **EPO** on smaller Engagements (Senior PM level or above).
- **Challenge:** Requirements change management — customers refine their needs as they see the product. Managing this without creating chaos in squad backlogs requires explicit change agreement with EPO and EPM, not silent backlog updates.
- **Challenge:** Inner source requirements competing for backlog space. EA prioritises inner source; the squad has delivery commitments. The PM must make the trade-off visible, not resolve it silently.

### How to Learn

- **On the job:** Take a multi-squad EPO assignment on a medium Engagement to develop cross-squad requirements thinking. Shadow an experienced EPO during Discover to understand customer discovery depth.
- **Feedback:** Dual-axis — EL rates backlog quality; Product leadership evaluates requirements craft and customer discovery contributions across Engagements.
- **Knowledge sharing:** Contribute requirements patterns (how to decompose a particular type of customer need) to PAC Practice Mode.
- **External reading:** *Continuous Discovery Habits* (Torres) — [producttalk.org](https://www.producttalk.org/continuous-discovery-habits/) — structured customer discovery that applies to the EPO's work and prepares PMs for the EPO role.

## Senior Product Manager

### Required Craft, Skills, Competencies

- **Craft:** Own customer discovery for an Engagement — work directly with customers to understand needs, pain points, and desired outcomes. Translate customer language into engineering requirements without losing the "why." Own customer training and enablement at Transfer — ensure customers understand and can use the delivered product (critical for Engagement Success).
- **Skills:** Work with EA to translate requirements into architectural decisions — the requirements-architecture boundary is where most Engagement scope issues originate. Know when a requirement implies a platform gap (inner source needed), a configuration change, or a custom build. Lead requirements prioritisation across multiple Squad PMs — set priorities at the Engagement level and let Squad PMs manage squad-level sequencing.
- **Competencies:** Drive Engagement Success outcomes (adoption, customer value) alongside EPM — EPM owns the function, but EPO drives the training and enablement that make adoption possible. Manage the tension between discovery depth and delivery velocity — too much discovery delays Build; too little means rework.

### Roles Expected to Play and Challenges

- **EPO** on complex Engagements. **Squad PM** on the highest-complexity squads in very large Engagements.
- **Challenge:** Bridging customer language with engineering requirements. Customers describe problems and outcomes; engineers need specifications and acceptance criteria. The EPO must translate without oversimplifying or gold-plating.
- **Challenge:** Customer training and enablement is deprioritised under delivery pressure. The EPO must advocate for it — without adoption, delivery is incomplete.
- **Challenge:** Requirements flow to multiple Squad PMs — if every requirement must go through EPO, the EPO becomes a bottleneck. Design the handoff so Squad PMs can decompose independently within a well-defined scope.

### How to Learn

- **On the job:** Lead the Discover phase for a complex Engagement. Own the customer training and enablement plan at Transfer.
- **Feedback:** EO and EPM assess customer alignment and requirements quality. Product leadership evaluates discovery depth and multi-Engagement product record.
- **Knowledge sharing:** Lead a case study on customer discovery patterns at PAC Practice Mode. Mentor Associate and mid-level PMs on requirements decomposition.

## Product Leadership (Director → VP)

### Director of Product / Sr. Director / VP

- **Craft:** Own the Product function's career path. Evaluate PMs across Engagements — you see the full multi-Engagement track record that no single EL or EPM sees. Shape product practice and requirements standards.
- **Key model construct:** Resolve cross-Engagement product conflicts (when multiple Engagements compete for the same customer-facing capability or when EPO requirements contradict each other). Participate in ERC to advise on EPO and Squad PM designation-to-complexity matching.
- **Challenge:** Providing meaningful career guidance when your reports' daily work is embedded in Engagements you do not lead. Build relationships with EOs and EPMs to ensure execution-axis feedback flows reliably.
- **Challenge:** The Product function in this model is different from a typical product organisation. The "product" is a Product Line platform; the "customer need" is the gap between what the platform provides and what a specific customer requires. Ensure PMs understand this distinction — they are not building new products from scratch; they are closing gaps in an existing platform for a specific customer.
