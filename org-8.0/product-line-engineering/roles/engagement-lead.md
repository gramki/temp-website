# Engagement Lead

## Purpose

The **Engagement Lead (EL)** owns **delivery accountability** for a Customer Solution under an engagement. The EL coordinates the Win Engineering Team, liaises with the customer, manages scope and timeline, and ensures the engagement transitions cleanly to steady state. The EL does **not** own solution architecture or variability documentation; the Solution Architect does. The EL owns **delivery**.

---

## Responsibilities

- **Team coordination** — Ensure the Win Engineering Team is aligned on scope, timeline, and priorities; resolve day-to-day coordination issues; escalate when team or resource conflicts block delivery.
- **Customer liaison** — Primary point of contact for the customer on delivery: status, scope, timeline, risks, and changes. Represent Zeta on delivery matters; escalate commercial or contractual issues as needed.
- **Scope management** — Own scope discipline: what’s in and out of the engagement; change management when the customer or team requests scope changes; alignment with Solution Architect on architectural scope boundaries.
- **Delivery** — Own delivery outcomes: milestones, go-live, handover. Ensure operational readiness (runbooks, escalation, handover checklist) per the chosen operating model. Ensure knowledge capture (retrospective, decision log) is done; Solution Architect owns content.
- **Staffing and rotation** — Request and coordinate staffing with Domain Team leads and Solution Architecture; document return/rotation dates; support rotation planning so continuity is maintained.

---

## Authority

- **Team direction** — Direct the Win Engineering Team on delivery priorities and timeline within the agreed scope. Does not override Solution Architect on architecture or variability.
- **Escalation** — Escalate to Domain Team leads (e.g. resource or quality issues), Solution Architecture (e.g. scope vs. archetype), or Engineering Leadership (e.g. scope vs. capacity) when local agreement cannot be reached.
- **Scope decisions (within boundaries)** — Approve or reject scope changes within the engagement’s authority (e.g. small changes); escalate significant or commercial scope changes per process.
- **Handover** — Sign off on handover when operational readiness and handover criteria are met per operating model and contract.

---

## Relationship to Solution Architect

- **Solution Architect** owns solution architecture, archetype application, gap analysis, variability documentation, and archetype updates. SA owns **what** we build and **how** it fits the product line.
- **Engagement Lead** owns delivery: **when**, **with whom**, and **how** we deliver. EL owns **outcomes** and **customer relationship** on delivery.
- **Alignment** — EL and SA must align from engagement start: scope, operating model, and handover. Disputes (e.g. scope vs. architecture) are escalated to Council or Engineering Leadership.

---

## Relationship to Domain Teams

- **Requesting resources** — EL (or designated lead) requests loan of Domain Engineers and others from Domain Team leads; Domain Team leads commit capacity and assign individuals. EL does not "own" loaned people; they report to their Domain Team lead for people management.
- **Managing conflicts** — When resource or quality conflicts arise (e.g. PR review delay, capacity pull), EL escalates to Domain Team lead or Council. EL does not unilaterally extend loan or override Domain Team decisions on capacity.
- **Inner source** — EL supports Win Engineering Team in following inner source process (consult, build, PR); EL does not override Domain Maintainer on PR approval. Timeline vs. quality conflicts are handled per [Tech Debt Policy](../governance/tech-debt-policy.md) and [Inner Source Guidelines](../governance/inner-source-guidelines.md).

---

## Success Metrics

- **Delivery velocity** — Milestones and go-live on time (or with agreed changes); handover on time.
- **Scope discipline** — Scope changes are managed (documented, approved, or rejected); no uncontrolled creep.
- **Customer satisfaction** — Customer feedback on delivery (e.g. NPS, satisfaction survey); minimal escalations.
- **Team health** — Win Engineering Team is aligned and productive; rotation and return happen per plan; knowledge capture is done.
- **Handover quality** — Handover complete per operating model; run team or customer has what they need; no critical open items.

---

## Career Path

Engagement Lead is a **delivery management** role. Career progression may include: senior Engagement Lead (larger or more complex engagements), program or portfolio lead (multiple engagements), or move to Solution Architect or Engineering Manager. See [Career Paths](career-paths.md).
