# Engineering Career Paths

## Purpose

This document describes **career paths** for engineers in the PLE model: **Domain depth** (platform expertise), **Win breadth** (solution and customer context), and **hybrid** (rotation between both). It also covers recognition for inner source and archetype contributions so that engagement work and platform work are both valued.

---

## Two Primary Tracks

### Domain Depth Track

**Focus:** Deep expertise in one or more domain platforms (Olympus, Tachyon, Neutrino, Electron, Quark). Engineers on this track spend most of their time in Domain Teams; they may do short rotations to engagements for context but are primarily platform developers or Domain Maintainers.

**Typical progression:**

- Junior Domain Engineer → Senior Domain Engineer → Staff Domain Engineer → Principal Domain Engineer

**What’s valued:**

- Platform quality, reliability, maintainability, and reusability
- Architecture and API design for the platform
- Inner source governance (when acting as Domain Maintainer)
- Mentoring and technical leadership within the Domain Team

**Rotation:** Short or optional engagement rotations to build customer context; primary home is Domain Team.

---

### Win Breadth Track

**Focus:** Broad experience across platforms and engagements. Engineers on this track spend significant time on Win Engineering Teams (configuring, extending, integrating); they build solution and customer context and may progress to Solution Architect or Engagement Lead.

**Typical progression:**

- Engineer → Senior Engineer → Solution Engineer → Solution Architect (or Engagement Lead for delivery-focused path)

**What’s valued:**

- Solution delivery: configuration, extension, integration
- Archetype application and improvement
- Inner source contributions (quality PRs back to domain platforms)
- Cross-platform and cross-engagement knowledge
- Variability documentation and archetype updates (when in SA role)

**Rotation:** Regular rotation through engagements; may return to a Domain Team for a period or move to Solution Architecture. Home may be a Domain Team (with engagement assignments) or a designated Win/pool structure per org.

---

## Hybrid Track

**Focus:** Engineers who alternate between Domain work and engagement work. They build both depth and breadth; they may progress to Engineering Manager (managing both Domain and Win engineers) or Principal (spanning product line concerns).

**Typical progression:**

- Engineer → Senior Engineer → Staff/Principal Engineer or Engineering Manager

**What’s valued:**

- Ability to work in both Domain and Win contexts
- Inner source contributions and platform quality
- Archetype and variability awareness
- Leadership across teams (technical or people)

**Rotation:** Explicit rotation between Domain Team and engagements; duration and cadence are planned so both depth and breadth are developed.

---

## How Rotation Builds Career

- **Domain depth** — Engineers who spend most of their time in Domain Teams build platform expertise; rotation to engagements is optional or short so depth is not diluted.
- **Win breadth** — Engineers who rotate through engagements build solution and customer context; rotation back to Domain Teams (or to Solution Architecture) is planned so knowledge is carried back and breadth is recognized.
- **Recognition** — Rotation is not a "pause" in career; it is a different kind of growth. Performance and career discussions explicitly value engagement experience, inner source contributions, and archetype contributions.

---

## Recognition for Inner Source Contributions

- **Performance** — Quality and impact of inner source PRs (merged to domain platforms) are considered in performance reviews. Win Engineering Team members who contribute back are recognized; Domain Teams acknowledge the contribution.
- **Career** — Inner source contributions demonstrate ability to work across boundaries and improve the product line; they support progression on both Domain depth and Win breadth tracks.
- **Visibility** — Council and Domain Teams may highlight notable inner source contributions (e.g. in Practice Mode or team meetings) so that the behavior is reinforced.

---

## Recognition for Archetype Contributions

- **Solution Architects** — Archetype ownership and archetype update proposals are part of SA performance and career progression. Maintaining blueprints, cookbooks, playbooks, and variability documentation is valued.
- **Win Engineering Teams** — Engagement feedback that leads to archetype improvements is acknowledged; decision logs and retrospective input that feed Council pattern extraction are valued.
- **Council** — Pattern extraction and archetype evolution are part of Council’s Practice Mode; follow-up actions (e.g. archetype updates) are assigned and recognized.

---

## Summary Table

| Track | Home | Progression | Rotation |
|-------|------|-------------|-----------|
| **Domain Depth** | Domain Team | Junior → Senior → Staff → Principal Domain Engineer | Short or optional engagement rotation |
| **Win Breadth** | Domain Team or Win/pool | Engineer → Senior → Solution Engineer → Solution Architect (or EL) | Regular engagement rotation; return or move to SA |
| **Hybrid** | Domain Team or Win/pool | Engineer → Senior → Staff/Principal or Engineering Manager | Alternating Domain and engagement |
