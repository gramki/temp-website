# Customer Product Delivery Lead (Deprecated)

> **This role has been retired.** The CPDL role is replaced by the **Engineering Lead (EL)** — a per-squad role with engineering/tech leadership and delivery accountability. Customer-facing responsibilities previously held by the CPDL are now split across the new Engagement role structure:
>
> - **Engineering Lead (EL)** — per-squad delivery and tech leadership (replaces CPDL's squad delivery accountability)
> - **Engagement Program Manager (EPM)** — primary customer-facing contact, integrated progress view, commercial alignment, Engagement Success
> - **Engagement Architect (EA)** — architecture across the entire Engagement span; variability; inner source priority
> - **Assembly Verification Architect (AVA)** — assembly certification; release authority; directs Verification Squad
> - **Engagement Product Owner (EPO)** — customer discovery, requirements, training
> - **SRE Lead** — operational readiness; monitoring; runbooks
> - **Engagement Owner (EO)** — overall Engagement accountability, final escalation endpoint
>
> See the [Engagement Operating Model Guide](../../engagement/README.md) for the complete role structure and responsibilities.

---

## Historical Context

The CPDL role combined delivery accountability, customer liaison, scope management, and staffing coordination into a single role. The new structure distributes these responsibilities across specialized roles to improve clarity, reduce single-point-of-failure risk, and allow each function to operate with appropriate authority:

| Former CPDL Responsibility | Now Owned By |
|---|---|
| Squad delivery accountability | Engineering Lead (EL) |
| Customer liaison (delivery) | Engagement Program Manager (EPM) |
| Scope management | EPM + EA |
| Commercial alignment | EPM + Account Management |
| Staffing and rotation coordination | EPM (serves ELs) |
| Customer discovery and requirements | Engagement Product Owner (EPO) |
| Overall Engagement accountability | Engagement Owner (EO) |
