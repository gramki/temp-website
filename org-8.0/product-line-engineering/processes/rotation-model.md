# Rotation Model

## Purpose

The **rotation model** defines how engineers move between Domain Teams and Win Engineering Teams: why we rotate, how long assignments last, how return is guaranteed, and how rotation supports career development. The goal is to balance platform quality (Domain Teams retain expertise) with delivery (Win Engineering Teams get capacity) and to preserve knowledge and build breadth.

---

## Why Rotate

- **Knowledge preservation** — When engineers return to Domain Teams (or move to another engagement), they bring back customer and solution context; knowledge is not locked in a single engagement.
- **Breadth development** — Engineers who rotate gain cross-platform and cross-engagement experience; that supports Solution Architect and leadership paths.
- **Prevent burnout** — Long stints on a single engagement without option to rotate can lead to fatigue; rotation offers a path back to platform work or to a different engagement.
- **Platform quality** — Reserving some capacity in Domain Teams and rotating Maintainers (see [Domain Maintainer](../roles/domain-maintainer.md)) keeps platform expertise and governance strong.

Rotation does **not** mean everyone must rotate on a fixed clock; it means we have a **model** for how long people stay on engagements and how they return or move.

---

## Rotation Cadence Within Engagements

- **Not the whole team at once** — Rotation is staggered so that the Win Engineering Team retains continuity. When one or two people rotate out, others stay (or new people rotate in).
- **Typical cadence** — Individual rotation every 6–12 months (or per engagement need). Exact cadence is set per engagement and documented in staffing plan.
- **Key roles** — Engagement Lead and Solution Architect may stay for the full engagement; Domain Engineers, Integration Engineers, QA, and Domain Analysts are more likely to rotate on a cadence.
- **Exception** — Small engagements or short delivery phases may have no rotation; the team stays until transition. Rotation is applied where engagement duration is long (e.g. 1–2 years).

---

## Return Guarantees to Domain Teams

- **Commitment:** When an engineer is loaned to a Win Engineering Team, their **return** to their Domain Team (or to another agreed role) is guaranteed and **documented** (e.g. return date or "return at end of Phase 3" or "return after N months").
- **Documentation:** Staffing plan or engagement charter includes return dates or rotation points for each loaned individual. Domain Team lead and Engagement Lead agree before or early in the engagement.
- **No indefinite loan** — We do not loan people without a defined return or rotation point. If the engagement extends, return/rotation is renegotiated and documented.
- **Domain Team capacity** — Domain Teams reserve capacity so that returning engineers have meaningful platform work. We do not loan so many people that the Domain Team cannot function or that return is pointless.

---

## How Rotation Builds Career

- **Domain depth** — Engineers who spend most of their time in Domain Teams (with limited or no engagement rotation) build deep platform expertise; path to Staff/Principal Domain Engineer. See [Career Paths](../roles/career-paths.md).
- **Win breadth** — Engineers who rotate through engagements build solution and customer context; path to Solution Architect or Engagement Lead. Rotation is recognized as valuable experience.
- **Hybrid** — Engineers who alternate between Domain work and engagement work can pursue Engineering Manager or Principal roles that span both.
- **Recognition** — Inner source contributions and archetype contributions are recognized in performance and career discussions. Rotation is not a "pause" in career; it is a different kind of growth.

---

## Rotation Planning and Visibility

- **Upfront** — When the Win Engineering Team is composed, rotation and return expectations are set and documented. Domain Team leads and Engagement Lead have visibility.
- **Ongoing** — As the engagement progresses, rotation dates are tracked (e.g. in staffing plan or resource tool). Domain Team leads use this for capacity planning; Engagement Lead uses it for continuity planning (e.g. onboarding the next person before someone rotates out).
- **Forecasting** — Engagement pipeline and staffing needs are visible (e.g. to Domain Team leads and Solution Architecture) so that capacity and rotation can be planned ahead.

---

## Exception Handling

- **Early return** — If an engineer needs to return early (e.g. personal reason, platform emergency), Domain Team lead and Engagement Lead agree; Engagement Lead finds substitute or adjusts scope. Early return is not penalized.
- **Extended stay** — If the engagement needs someone to stay longer than originally planned, this is renegotiated with the engineer and Domain Team lead; return date is updated and documented. We avoid indefinite extension without agreement.
- **Multiple engagements** — An engineer may move from one engagement to another (e.g. after rotation or return). That is still a "return" from the first engagement’s perspective; the second engagement is a new assignment. Domain Team lead may agree to a direct move (engagement A → engagement B) if it fits capacity and career goals.
