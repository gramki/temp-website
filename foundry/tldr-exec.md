# Foundry — the platform for agent-driven software delivery

## Why

We believe the way software is built is about to change — not incrementally, but structurally.

Agents can already write code. That's no longer the frontier. The frontier is what happens when you stop treating agents as tools that help individuals, and start treating them as workers who share the line with humans.

When that shift happens — and it will — the organizations that have figured out how to structure work for human-agent teams will move faster, ship better, and outcompete those still running a human-only playbook with AI bolted on.

We're not waiting for that future. We're building the system that makes it possible.

---

## How

We're not building another agent. We're building the infrastructure around agents.

A system where:

- **Work is defined.** Every kind of work a software organization does — from turning a signal into a spec, to verifying a build, to publishing an artifact — is modeled as a Scenario that agents and humans know how to execute.
- **Context flows with the work.** Every Work Order belongs to a graph rooted at Product Intent — what the product is meant to be. That graph carries the context the work needs, so agents and humans aren't starting from scratch.
- **Humans and agents share the line.** Workspaces are stations on the delivery line — Product Specification, UX Design, Development, QA, Release, Governance. Each station houses the agents and humans who work there. The same work flows through; who does what differs.
- **Transitions are governed.** Every handoff, every gate, every artifact that moves between stages is tracked, validated, and auditable. Governance is built into the flow, not bolted on after.

We formalize this in two models — **UPIM** (what the work is and what context it needs) and **ACE** (how the work moves and who does it). Then we implement those models as a platform.

## What

**Foundry** — the engineering platform where AI agents and human engineers collaborate to deliver software product work.

Not as chat sidekicks. As first-class workers acting on structured work, inside specialized workspaces, against a shared product context.

## What you'd be working on

- **Foundry Management** — Workbenches for Products, repositories (as services), teams, agents, knowledge, tenancy
- **Foundry IDE** — builder-facing views on work, tasks, context, progress — workspace-specific
- **Work Order Runtime** — context compilation, agent lifecycle for WO execution, agent delegation, human-task surfacing
- **Foundry Orchestrator** — move Product Intent across workspaces, create Work Orders, invoke Governance Scenarios, enforce gates
- **Scenario Authoring (per Track, Workspace)** — scenario discovery & definition; Skills, Knowledge, and Tools; agent recommendations
- **Release Tools** — CI/CD pipelines with embedded agents, CD integrations, distribution stores
- **Platform Ops** — observability dashboards, standard tooling, infrastructure plumbing
