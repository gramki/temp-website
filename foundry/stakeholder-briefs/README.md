# Stakeholder Briefs

## What this folder is

This folder captures **customer (and other stakeholder) value perspectives** on the Foundry Platform — written **for builders to internalize**, not for external distribution. The point is to give the platform's product and engineering teams an honest picture of how an executive customer (typically a Bank CIO) would evaluate the platform, so design and build decisions stay anchored in real value rather than internal preference.

These briefs are part of the engineering documentation, not marketing collateral. The tone is candid; the language avoids the gloss of customer-facing material; the content is sourced from existing documentation in this tree, not from imagined customer asks.

## Audience

Primary readers are **Foundry Platform builders** — product, engineering, and engagement leads who need to understand customer-side value to inform their decisions. Secondary readers are anyone joining the team who needs the customer context.

This folder is **not the place** an external CIO would land. External-facing material, if and when it is created, lives elsewhere — typically in marketing or GTM repositories outside this engineering tree.

## Authoring rules

Briefs in this folder must follow these rules to remain useful:

1. **Source from existing engineering material.** Every value claim should trace to an existing source: [../ace/](../ace/README.md), [../foundry-platform/platform.TODO](../foundry-platform/platform.TODO), [../engagement-engineering/](../engagement-engineering/README.md), [../product-information-model/](../product-information-model/README.md), or other documents in this tree. If a claim is not supported by the engineering material, it is either a content gap (file an issue) or out of scope (drop it).
2. **No fabrication of customer asks.** Briefs describe how a stakeholder would evaluate what we are building. They do not invent customer requirements that have not been heard.
3. **Engineering-honest tone.** Avoid superlatives, hype, and language that would sound like a sales pitch. The tone should be the same as a well-written engineering review.
4. **Explicit limits.** A brief should be honest about what the platform does *not* do, especially for the stakeholder being addressed. A CIO will value a clear non-goal as much as a clear goal.
5. **Cross-link.** Every claim should link to its source so a builder reading the brief can drill into the engineering detail.
