# Cognitive Audit Fabric (CAF)
Auditability for Enterprise-wide Cognitive Systems

## Purpose

**Cognitive Audit Fabric (CAF)** is a federated governance and auditability layer that enables enterprises to **safely scale cognitive systems**—including ML-driven decision engines, GenAI-assisted workflows, and agentic systems—by making their behavior **reconstructable, defensible, and governable by design**.

CAF exists to answer a simple but critical question:

> *“If this system exercised judgement at scale, can we later reconstruct what happened, under what policy, with whose authority, and whether it remained controlled over time?”*

CAF is **not** about intelligence, learning, or central knowledge capture. It is about **accountability at scale**.

---

## What CAF Is

CAF is a **fabric**, not a platform or repository. It coordinates and governs memory and auditability **without centralizing domain systems**.

CAF provides:

* A **control plane** for cognitive audit requirements
* **Standards and interfaces** for decision and action records
* **Discovery and replay orchestration** across federated domains
* A common language for regulators, auditors, and risk owners

CAF treats cognition as infrastructure **only to the extent required to defend it**.

---

## What CAF Is Not

CAF explicitly does **not**:

* Centralize enterprise data or knowledge
* Store chain-of-thought or raw prompts
* Replace domain systems or KM platforms
* Act as a system of record for business data
* Attempt to capture “everything”

CAF is about **reasonable reconstruction**, not omniscience.

---

## Why CAF Is Needed Now

Enterprises already had decision systems. What has changed is:

* **Scale**: cognition is exercised millions of times per day
* **Variance**: adaptive systems behave differently over time
* **Opacity**: ML and agentic systems are not fully inspectable ex‑ante
* **Liability**: regulators and boards increasingly require named accountability

Traditional controls (SOPs, static documentation, sampling audits) are no longer sufficient on their own.

CAF addresses this gap without forcing premature centralization.

---

## Core Capabilities (Minimum Viable CAF)

CAF can be adopted incrementally. The minimum viable footprint includes:

1. **Cognitive System Registry**

   * Inventory of systems exercising judgement
   * Risk classification (high / medium / low)

2. **Ownership & Accountability Mapping**

   * Named business owner
   * Named technical owner
   * Escalation and intervention authority

3. **Federated Decision & Action Records**

   * Standardized schemas
   * Stored close to domain systems
   * Referenced, not duplicated

4. **Change & Version Traceability**

   * Model, rule, prompt, and config versions
   * Deployment and rollback metadata

5. **Audit Discovery & Replay Orchestration**

   * Locate relevant records across domains
   * Reconstruct behavior on demand

---

## Architectural Principle

> **Memory lives close to action. Governance lives centrally.**

CAF does not move data. It makes data **discoverable, interpretable, and auditable** across heterogeneous systems.

---

## Who Owns CAF

CAF is a **shared construct**:

* **Risk / Compliance** define requirements and assurance criteria
* **IT / Platform teams** implement fabric primitives
* **Business domains** own their decision memory
* **Internal Audit** consumes CAF outputs

No single function owns all of CAF—by design.

---

## What CAF Enables

With CAF in place, enterprises can:

* Scale agentic and AI-driven decisioning faster
* Respond to audits and regulators with confidence
* Investigate incidents without heroic forensics
* Prove control of discretion over time

CAF turns audit from a **brake** into an **enabler of safe scale**.

> **Cognitive Audit Fabric is a federated governance layer that makes enterprise cognitive systems auditable, reconstructable, and accountable—without centralizing them.**
