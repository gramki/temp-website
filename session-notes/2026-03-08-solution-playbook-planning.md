# Session Notes: Enterprise Solutions Playbook — Planning

**Date:** 2026-03-08 (Phase 1 of multi-phase conversation)  
**Focus:** Scoping, clarifying requirements, and creating a detailed plan for a mini-book on the enterprise solutions business lifecycle  
**Chat reference:** [Solution story & Zeta analysis](82616faf-d44a-4bc2-8ca3-43f7c401226b)

## Summary

The user requested a detailed essay / "short book" on how to approach the full lifecycle of an enterprise solutions business, written from a McKinsey consultant perspective. The book needed to contrast enterprise solutions against Consumer Product, B2B SaaS, and SI/Consulting playbooks — because many Zeta executives apply consumer/SaaS reflexes to what is fundamentally an enterprise solutions business.

## Clarifying Questions & Answers

Eight clarifying questions were asked and answered:

| Question | User's Answer |
|---|---|
| Audience | Internal Zeta executives and leadership teams |
| Key anti-patterns to address | PMs defining features not solutions (mostly); GTM running funnels not consultative discovery (yes); pricing as tiers not value-based (yes); teams unhappy they're "not building for users" (misaligned reference points) |
| Zeta-specific vs general | General strategic guide; Zeta bridging in separate document |
| Industry focus | Industry-agnostic at framework level, banking as primary applied example; banking bias acceptable |
| Case studies | Mix — named companies with public references, archetypes for sensitive/speculative |
| Contrast structure | Four-column contrast approved (Consumer, B2B SaaS, Enterprise Solutions, SI/Consulting) |
| Depth allocation | Emphasis on Opportunity Discovery, Enterprise Buying Dynamics, Deal Shaping, Delivery Models, Economics, Platformization; concise treatment for others with explicit callouts |
| Tone | McKinsey analytical rigor + teaching structure ("the mistake" / "the correction") + enough Zeta voice to be actionable |

## Plan Created

Detailed plan at `.cursor/plans/enterprise_solutions_mini-book_78a37767.plan.md`:

- **Structure**: Prologue + 12 chapters (4 parts) + Epilogue
- **6 Deep chapters** (~15-20 pages each): Discovery, Buying Dynamics, Deal Shaping, Delivery, Economics, Platformization
- **6 Concise chapters** (~5-8 pages each): Problem Archetypes, Opportunity Sizing, Value Pools, Solutions Archetypes, Scaling, Strategic Positioning
- **Epilogue requirement**: No company strictly adheres to any archetype; execs should learn from evolution and shape their own trajectory
- **Bridging document**: `bridging-to-zeta-reality.md` for Zeta-specific observations

## Key Design Decisions

1. **Zeta-specific content separated** — main book is a general guide; Zeta-specific observations captured in a separate bridging document
2. **Four-column contrast** as the signature pedagogical device, threaded through every chapter
3. **Named case studies**: Palantir, Veeva, ServiceNow, Salesforce, SAP, Workday, Accenture, Cognizant, Epic — all with publicly accessible references
4. **Banking as primary applied example** — not the only industry, but the dominant worked example given Zeta's context
