# Appendix P — Studio Council Member (SCM) Roles & Playbook

Purpose: Make continuous improvement institutional, not episodic. Define what the Studio Council Member (SCM) does across the lifecycle — from conception to close‑out — and how improvements move from discovery to adoption with clear evidence and ownership.

Scope
- Applies to multi‑team programs and long‑running streams
- Cross‑references: Section 11 (Continuous Improvement), Appendix H (Governance Audit), Appendix M (Templates), Appendix T (Thresholds), Appendix L (Jira Schema)

---

## P.1 Role Definition
- Mandate: Steward continuous improvement end‑to‑end — discovery → proposal → decision → go‑live → adoption audit → institutionalization
- Authority: May request agenda items in Monthly/Steering; may require Evidence Packs (M.6) for policy changes; owns Manual versioning
- Independence: Reports outcomes to Engagement Owner; operates in partnership with Delivery Manager and DPO

Core Responsibilities
- Maintain Manual Change Register (M.3) and publish release notes for manual updates
- Ensure every improvement item has owner, date, evidence, and adoption audits (3 monthly checks)
- Curate thresholds catalog (Appendix T) and propose tuning based on signal trends
- Run Governance Audits (Appendix H) per schedule; track remediation to closure
- Keep cross‑program learnings discoverable; advocate reuse/adoption

---

## P.2 Operating Model by Phase

### Conception (Program setup)
Objectives
- Install improvement plumbing (registers, audits, versioning) before delivery pressure mounts
Checklist (first 2–4 weeks)
- [ ] Create Manual Change Register (M.3) space; seed workflow and fields
- [ ] Agree Evidence Pack (M.6) standard; add to Definition of Ready for decisions
- [ ] Baseline Governance Audit (Appendix H); record initial maturity
- [ ] Publish initial thresholds (Appendix T) and owners/forums
- [ ] Add “decision backlog age” and “exception register” widgets to ops dashboard
Outputs
- Working register; baseline audit report; thresholds v1.0; comms on cadence and SLAs

### Infancy (0–3 months)
Objectives
- Build the muscle: small wins, visible adoption, no policy churn without evidence
Checklist (monthly)
- [ ] Review decision latency; unblock with Steering if > SLA
- [ ] Audit two improvements to adoption (M.3): evidence before/after; learning note
- [ ] Thresholds: propose one tuning with trend evidence (Appendix T)
- [ ] Exceptions: reversion reviews; convert repeated to policy paper (M.1)
Outputs
- Monthly improvement pack; 1–3 implemented changes; exception closures with notes

### Steady‑State (Quarterly rhythm)
Objectives
- Institutionalize learnings and keep the manual current
Checklist (quarterly)
- [ ] Run Governance Audit (Appendix H); publish remediation plan and owners
- [ ] Manual version release: collate approved changes; publish release notes; update cross‑refs
- [ ] Portfolio view of improvements: % discovery→adoption; time‑to‑adoption; blocked items with causes
- [ ] Thresholds review: keep high‑churn items out of the manual; tune in Appendix T
Outputs
- Manual vX.Y release; audit report; improvements dashboard; threshold deltas

### Close‑Out (Program wind‑down or major milestone)
Objectives
- Preserve institutional memory; hand off sustainably
Checklist
- [ ] Summarize top 10 learnings with evidence and links (what changed, why, impact)
- [ ] Archive registers with stable links; ensure ownership for residual items
- [ ] Final adoption audit (3× monthly checks complete or exception justified)
- [ ] Manual change proposals parked/handed to next program or COE
Outputs
- Close‑out memo; archived evidence; ownership map for residuals

## P.3 RACI (Selected)
- Manual Change Register (M.3): R=SCM; A=Engagement Owner; C=Delivery Manager, DPO; I=Studio Owner
- Governance Audit (Appendix H): R=SCM; A=Studio Owner; C=DM/DPO/EM/QA; I=Steering
- Thresholds Catalog (Appendix T): R=SCM; A=Engagement Owner; C=DM/QA/Commercial; I=Teams
- Evidence Packs (M.6) standard: R=SCM; A=Delivery Manager; C=QA/EM/DPO; I=Steering
- Manual Versioning: R=SCM; A=Engagement Owner; C=DM/DPO/QA; I=Program

## P.4 KPIs & Signals
- Adoption Rate: % improvements that reach “adopted” in ≤ 90 days
- Time‑to‑Adoption: median days discovery → adoption audit pass
- Decision Latency: median/95p time from proposal to decision (CCB/Steering)
- Audit Remediation: % audit items closed by due date
- Version Currency: age since last manual release ≤ 90 days (longer by exception)
- Exception Hygiene: % exceptions with reversion on time; repeat exceptions converted to policy

Guardrails
- Evidence before policy: no policy/threshold change without M.6 Evidence Pack
- Keep it lightweight: 1‑page artifacts; monthly cadence; automate dashboards after manual discipline

---

## P.5 Playbook (How‑to Sequences)

Sequence: Propose a Policy/Threshold Change
1) Draft M.6 Evidence Pack with signals and impacts
2) Create M.1 Decision Paper (2–3 options); link thresholds (Appendix T)
3) Table at Monthly; pre‑wire Steering if material
4) On approval, update Appendix T and, if stable, the manual; publish release notes

Sequence: Close an Improvement Item
1) Verify go‑live date; gather before/after signals
2) Run adoption audits monthly ×3; record outcomes
3) If benefits hold, mark adopted; add to manual notes/release
4) If benefits regress, open a follow‑up or roll back per decision paper
