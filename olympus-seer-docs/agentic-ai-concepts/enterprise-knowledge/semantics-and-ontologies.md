# Semantics, Metadata, and Ontologies

## What this is

This knowledge type captures **meaning systems** that make enterprise knowledge usable and consistent:

- taxonomies and controlled vocabularies
- ontologies and knowledge graphs
- metadata catalogs (schemas, lineage, ownership, definitions)

It answers:

> *“What does this term/value/entity mean in context, and how does it relate to others?”*

## Where this “lives” cognitively

Semantics are **interpretive**, not normative:

- Semantics alone do not constrain behavior.
- Semantics become knowledge when paired with **asserted truth** (owned commitments).

## Typical artifacts

- domain ontologies (entities + relations)
- taxonomies for products, risks, reasons, categories
- data catalog entries with definitions and ownership

## Management characteristics

- **Shared across domains**: semantics enable translation across systems and teams.
- **Governed vocabulary**: control synonyms, ambiguity, and drift.
- **Provenance and lineage**: semantics without source and ownership quickly rot.
