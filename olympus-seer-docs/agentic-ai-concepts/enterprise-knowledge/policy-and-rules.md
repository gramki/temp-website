# Policies, Rules, and Regulatory Knowledge

## What this is

This knowledge type captures **binding constraints** and normative decision criteria:

- policies (“must / must not”)
- rules (“if conditions, then requirement”)
- regulatory obligations (what compliance demands)

It answers:

> *“What is permitted, required, or prohibited?”*

## Typical artifacts

- risk policies (fraud, credit, AML)
- eligibility rules
- security and privacy policies
- regulatory interpretations (with jurisdiction and scope)
- decision criteria and evidence requirements

## Key management characteristics

- **High stability**: changes are intentional and audited.
- **Strong ownership**: explicit accountable owners (function/committee).
- **Versioned and effective-dated**: policies change in time; older versions remain relevant for audits.

## What this is not

- It is not “what we tend to do in practice” (that’s preference/procedural memory).
- It is not “what happened” (that’s episodic enterprise memory).

## Common failure modes

- Policies embedded only in code/config with no semantic ownership (“implicit policy”)
- No scope/jurisdiction metadata (agents can’t apply rules correctly)
- Drift: practice diverges from policy without detection
