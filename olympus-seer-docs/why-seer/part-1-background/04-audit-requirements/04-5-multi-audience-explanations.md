# 4.5 Multi-Audience Explanations

*Generating appropriate explanations for customers, operators, and regulators.*

---

## Purpose

This subsection addresses a critical audit capability: the ability to explain the same decision differently to different audiences. A customer asking "Why was my application denied?" needs a different explanation than an operator investigating a complaint or a regulator examining decision patterns. The underlying decision is the same, but the explanation must be tailored to the audience's needs, expertise, and legal context.

---

## The Multi-Audience Problem

Enterprise agent decisions have multiple stakeholders, each with different needs:

| Audience | Question | Needs |
|----------|----------|-------|
| **Customer** | "Why did this happen to me?" | Plain language, empathy, next steps |
| **Operator** | "What went wrong and how do I fix it?" | Technical detail, actionable information |
| **Regulator** | "Was this decision reasonable and compliant?" | Complete evidence, policy alignment, defensibility |

A single explanation format cannot serve all these audiences:

- Technical explanations confuse customers
- Customer-friendly explanations lack regulatory rigor
- Regulatory explanations are not actionable for operators

---

## Audience Profiles

### Customer Audience

**Context**: The customer received a decision that affects them and wants to understand why.

| Characteristic | Requirement |
|----------------|-------------|
| **Language** | Plain, accessible (8th-grade reading level) |
| **Tone** | Empathetic, respectful |
| **Content** | Key factors, not technical details |
| **Framing** | What this means for them |
| **Actions** | Clear next steps |

**Example customer explanation**:

> *"Hi Sarah,*
> 
> *We reviewed your refund request and approved it. We verified your purchase from December 15 and confirmed the issue you reported. Your refund of $45.00 will appear in your account within 3-5 business days.*
> 
> *If you have questions, please contact us."*

### Operator Audience

**Context**: An operator needs to understand a decision for investigation, quality assurance, or issue resolution.

| Characteristic | Requirement |
|----------------|-------------|
| **Language** | Technical, precise |
| **Tone** | Neutral, informative |
| **Content** | Full factor breakdown, model details, context |
| **Framing** | What the system did and why |
| **Actions** | What can be investigated or changed |

**Example operator explanation**:

> *"Decision ID: dec-12345*
> 
> *Decision: approve_refund (confidence: 0.87)*
> 
> *Factors:*
> - *Purchase verified: ✓ (weight: 0.40)*
> - *No prior disputes: Clean history (weight: 0.30)*
> - *Amount reasonable: $45.00 (weight: 0.30)*
> 
> *Evidence bundle: evd-001*
> *Policy: refund-policy-v2.3, Section 4.2*
> 
> *No overrides. Within autonomous authority bounds."*

### Regulator Audience

**Context**: A regulator is examining the decision for compliance, fairness, or as part of an investigation.

| Characteristic | Requirement |
|----------------|-------------|
| **Language** | Formal, precise, compliance-oriented |
| **Tone** | Neutral, complete |
| **Content** | Full evidence chain, policy references, alternatives considered |
| **Framing** | Demonstrating reasonableness |
| **Actions** | How to retrieve additional evidence |

**Example regulator explanation**:

> *"Decision Record: dec-12345*
> 
> *Date: January 7, 2026, 14:30 UTC*
> 
> *Summary: Automated refund approval decision made by Agent fraud-resolution-agent-v2.1 within Workbench dispute-resolution.*
> 
> *Decision Basis: Decision was based on 3 weighted factors with combined confidence of 87%. Threshold for autonomous approval: 80%.*
> 
> *Evidence Chain: 4 evidence items linked (transaction verification, customer history, policy lookup, risk assessment). All evidence captured at decision time and preserved in evidence bundle evd-001.*
> 
> *Policy Alignment: Decision aligns with Refund Policy v2.3, Section 4.2 (automatic approval criteria for verified purchases under $100 with clean history).*
> 
> *Human Oversight: No human override applied. Decision was within autonomous authority bounds defined in EmploymentSpec.*
> 
> *Evidence Manifest: [Attached]*
> 
> *Export Formats Available: PDF, JSON, XML"*

### Semantic Explainers

Each content schema includes **semantic explainer** metadata that guides explanation generation:

```yaml
semantic_explainer:
  templates:
    customer:
      summary: "We {{decision.action | customer_friendly}} based on {{factors | count}} factors."
      detail: "{{narrative | customer_friendly}}"
    
    technical:
      summary: "Decision: {{decision.action}} (confidence: {{confidence | percent}})"
      factors: "{{factors | technical_format}}"
    
    audit:
      summary: "Decision record {{id}} documents {{decision.action}} on {{timestamp | datetime}}."
      evidence_chain: "{{evidence_count}} evidence items linked."
  
  field_semantics:
    decision.confidence:
      label: "Confidence"
      description: "Certainty level of the decision"
      display_format: "percent"
    
    factors:
      label: "Decision Factors"
      description: "Elements that influenced the decision"
```

## Explanation Types

### Decision Explanation

*"Why was this decision made?"*

Components:
- What was decided
- What factors contributed
- What evidence was considered
- What the outcome means

### Counterfactual Explanation

*"What would have happened if...?"*

Components:
- What conditions would change the decision
- Which factor is most influential
- How stable is the decision

**Example**:

> *"If confidence had exceeded 90%, this could have been auto-approved without human review. Without purchase verification, confidence would drop to ~52%, likely triggering escalation."*

### Case Explanation

*"What happened with this case from start to finish?"*

Components:
- Timeline of events
- Key decisions made
- Outcome and resolution

This requires traversing multiple records linked by `case_id`.

## Quality Requirements

Explanations must meet quality standards:

| Quality Dimension | Description |
|-------------------|-------------|
| **Completeness** | All required elements present for the audience |
| **Accuracy** | Faithful to the underlying decision record |
| **Clarity** | Understandable by the target audience |
| **Actionability** | Includes next steps where appropriate |
| **Consistency** | Similar decisions get similar explanations |

### Audience-Specific Quality

| Audience | Quality Focus |
|----------|---------------|
| **Customer** | Empathy, clarity, actionability |
| **Operator** | Technical accuracy, completeness, actionability |
| **Regulator** | Completeness, policy alignment, defensibility |

## Common Misconceptions

### Misconception 1: "One Explanation Fits All"

**The error**: Generate one explanation and use it everywhere.

**Why it fails**: Technical explanations alienate customers. Customer explanations lack regulatory rigor. Operators need different information than executives.

**The fix**: Design multi-audience explanation from the start.

### Misconception 2: "Explanations Are Just Formatting"

**The error**: The same content with different formatting.

**Why it fails**: Different audiences need different content, not just different styling. A customer doesn't need to see model weights. A regulator does need to see policy references.

**The fix**: Templates select and emphasize different content, not just format it differently.

### Misconception 3: "LLM-Generated Explanations Are Sufficient"

**The error**: Just ask an LLM to explain the decision.

**Why it fails**: LLM-generated explanations may not reflect the actual decision basis. They may hallucinate factors or omit important elements.

**The fix**: Use structured templates grounded in actual decision records. LLM can polish language but should not invent content.

## Practical Implications

### For Enterprise Architects

1. Design explanation capability into the audit architecture
2. Define audience profiles and their specific needs
3. Create template registries for each content type
4. Plan for explanation quality metrics

### For Agent Developers

1. Ensure decision records contain all explainable factors
2. Use clear, labellable factor names
3. Include outcome linkage for context
4. Test explanations for all audiences

### For Compliance Officers

1. Verify explanations are accurate to decision records
2. Test customer explanations for readability
3. Verify regulator explanations include all required elements
4. Audit explanation consistency
