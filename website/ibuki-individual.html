Ibuki / Individual

The personal concierge you were never profitable enough to be given.

A private-banking client gets a person who remembers their goals, watches their accounts, and calls before there’s a problem. Everyone else gets an app and a 1-800 number. Mira is that person — for every customer the bank could never afford to assign one to.

A day with Mira See the customer view
01The individual relationship

One person’s financial life is more coordination than most people can carry.

Renewals lapse. Fees creep. A subscription doubles and no one notices. A windfall sits idle in checking. A bill and a paycheck miss each other by two days and an overdraft follows. None of this is hard — it is just relentless, and humans are forgetful by design. The relationship the customer needs is not more features to navigate. It is someone who holds the whole picture so they don’t have to.

02What Mira does

Not a screen to open. A presence that anticipates.

“Your home insurance renews Tuesday — 18% higher than last year.”

Mira remembers the renewal, notices the jump, and arrives with options, not just an alert — three comparable quotes, or a one-tap “remind me to call them.”

“You’ve got $9,400 sitting in checking earning nothing.”

Idle cash, caught. A suitability-checked nudge toward a high-yield option you already qualify for — your decision, executed in one confirmation, reversible.

“This payment and your paycheck will cross. Want me to shift the date?”

The overdraft that never happens. Mira sees the timing collision days ahead and offers the fix before the fee.

“That subscription just went from $9 to $19. Keep it?”

The quiet creep that drains accounts, surfaced. Cancel, keep, or negotiate — Mira can do any of the three on your say-so.

“Your card was declined abroad. I’ve lifted the travel block — try again.”

A problem that arrives already resolved, never as a dead end. Every rejection comes with the path through it.

“Nice month — you spent less on dining and hit your savings goal.”

Mira notices the good, too. Relationship, not surveillance — the small acknowledgments a good RM would give.

03Wherever the customer already is

Mira meets them in the channel they already use — not one more app to check.

The bank’s app

Embedded where they already bank — proactive, conversational, not buried under sixty menu items.

SMS & email

The channels people actually read. A nudge, a question, a one-tap confirmation.

Voice

Siri, Alexa, Google — “ask my bank” becomes a real answer, in context.

Their own agent

When the customer brings ChatGPT or Apple Intelligence, Mira meets it agent-to-agent, under the customer’s delegation.

04The rule that governs everything

“Solve for the user and the relationship before you solve for the bank.”

Mira will tell a customer to cancel a product the bank sells if it’s wrong for them. That is not a bug in the model — it is the entire point. Trust earned this way is what makes every later recommendation land, and what makes the customer consolidate their life with the one bank that acts in their interest.
05Why the bank wins

Serve the individual first, and the relationship deepens on its own.

A customer who trusts Mira brings more of their life to the bank — not because they were cross-sold, but because the bank became the place that holds the whole picture. The commercial result follows the trust:

Deposits

Idle and outside balances migrate to the bank that notices and advises.

Breadth

Products grow per customer because Mira surfaces only what genuinely fits.

Churn

Leaving means leaving the relationship — not uninstalling an app.

Cost to serve

High-frequency questions resolved by Mira never reach the call center.
