01Where the value sits

Optimizing individuals is linear — and capped. Optimizing the movement of work changes the institution.

A dispute that takes eleven days contains perhaps ninety minutes of human work. The rest is movement: queues between teams, handoffs that shed context, reconciliations between systems that disagree, escalations, and status meetings about all of the above. The proportions repeat across onboarding, credit decisions, exception handling, and product launches.

When a commitment is resolved end-to-end by a governed combination of people and agents operating on one model of the work — context never shed, state never re-keyed — the edge doesn’t get faster. It ceases to exist as work. The handoff was never the job; it was the overhead of dividing the job among parties who couldn’t hold it whole.

Nodes

A copilot makes a person ~20% faster at their step. Same queues, same handoffs, same eleven days.

Edges

Work resolved on one Work Model carries its context end-to-end. The coordination overhead dissolves.

The asymmetry

An assistant is bound to a person. The value is in what happens between persons — across teams, branches, and divisions.

02Words for the agentic enterprise

You cannot build what you cannot describe — and “copilot” describes a tool, not a bank.

The vocabulary on offer was written for every enterprise and no bank in particular. Ten words are enough to describe the agentic bank. Each names work and its movement, not tools and their users — and each unlocks a question that today has no owner.

The work itself
Work Model
The explicit map of a domain’s work — its commitments, disciplines, and the scenarios through which work gets resolved.
“What is all the work of this domain — and how would we know?”
Stream
A commitment to the outside world, fulfilled end-to-end — today, across teams, systems, and divisions.
“What do we owe, end to end — and how many boundaries does it cross to get done?”
Loop
An internal discipline run continuously — reconciliation, fraud vigilance, compliance assurance.
“What keeps this domain healthy — and who tends it?”
Resolution and orchestration
Hub
A bounded business domain — product, distribution, relationship, operations — running on its Work Model.
“Who owns this domain’s transformation, end to end?”
Swarm
A governed team of AI agents, trained on the Work Model, employed with identity, delegated authority, and guardrails.
“Who — exactly — is doing this work, under whose accountability?”
The dial
The explicit, reversible division of every piece of work between people and agents.
“How much of this domain is absorbed — and what moves next?”
The estate and the accountability
Machine
Any system — yours, ours, anyone’s — enrolled into the Work Model behind a stable Tool Contract.
“What can this domain call on, regardless of which vendor built it?”
Induction
The enrollment of your existing systems into the Work Model — wrapped, registered, intelligence extracted; nothing ripped out.
“What do we keep — and on whose schedule do we ever replace it?”
Fabric
A single-concern, contract-exposed system of record or capability — the building blocks the estate evolves toward.
“Can we change one thing without breaking ten?”
Decision record
The replayable account of every consequential decision: what was decided, on what evidence, under whose authority.
“Can we defend this — to the customer, the auditor, the regulator?”

Every one of these words survives a change in technology. Models will improve, vendors will come and go, protocols will be replaced — and the bank’s Streams, its dial positions, its decision records, its inducted Machines remain meaningful. That is the test of enterprise vocabulary: it describes what persists.

03Dare to reimagine

Four rungs, each agent-native: designed around the work, not around the scarcity of human attention.

Departments, queues, and handoffs exist because no person could hold the work whole. When that constraint lifts, the design inverts: work stops moving between resolvers — resolution assembles around the work. What follows is a destination picture, labeled as such. Nothing in it requires a breakthrough.

RUNG 1

A service domain, agent-native

A cardholder’s own assistant files a dispute at 6:40 a.m. — agent to agent. The bank’s servicing Swarm verifies the delegated authority, gathers evidence, evaluates it against policy, and issues a provisional credit before breakfast — recorded, replayable, indistinguishable in governance from a human investigator’s work. The floor behind it is smaller and more senior: the Swarm resolves the volume; people resolve what deserves people, each case arriving with context fully assembled. The eleven days were the edges. The edges are gone.

RUNG 2

A product domain, agent-native

A product stops being a project that crosses departments and becomes a blueprint in a Hub. Variants are configured, simulated against real portfolio data, and launched under governance in days. The annual product calendar becomes a weekly rhythm — not because anyone works faster, but because a launch no longer requires the serialized attention of eleven teams. Products for segments of one become operable, because the marginal product carries no marginal coordination.

RUNG 3

A banking division, agent-native

A division stops being an org chart that work flows through and becomes a federation of Hubs that work resolves inside. The headcount plan becomes a set of dial positions — explicit decisions about which work people hold. Management becomes what it always claimed to be: directing the work and owning the guardrails. The division head runs the business from a scorecard — denominator, absorption, outcomes, cost per resolution — not from status decks.

RUNG 4

A bank, agent-native

Governed domains on a fabric estate. The middle office of pure coordination — handoffs, reconciliations, follow-ups on follow-ups — largely dissolved into Work Models. People concentrated where a bank differentiates: judgment, relationships, risk appetite, accountability. An institution legible from the outside — to its regulator, who can query what its agents do; to its partners, who integrate against contracts; and to its customers’ agents, who are met deliberately, under verified delegation.

That is the difference between a bank with ten thousand copilots and an agentic bank. The first is today’s institution, accelerated. The second is a different institution.
