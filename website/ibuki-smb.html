Ibuki / Business

Every small business needs a CFO. Almost none can afford one.

Banks hand SMBs a business checking account, a card, maybe a credit line — tools, not help — and treat the owner’s personal account as a separate stranger. Mira becomes the CFO the business could never hire: cash flow, receivables and payables, the business-personal picture, and credit readiness — solving scenarios, not shipping features.

The CFO at work See the customer view
01The business relationship

Banks sell SMBs products. SMBs need someone to run the money.

For an owner-operator, the practice’s cash flow is personal cash flow. The owner’s draw, the business credit line backed by a personal guarantee, the retirement plan that depends on this quarter’s profit — these are one integrated question, and the bank splits them into a “business customer” and a “retail customer” who never meet. Meanwhile the real questions — can I make payroll Friday? can I afford to hire? should I pay this vendor early for the discount? — get no answer from anyone, because hiring a CFO costs more than the problem.

02What Mira does for the business

The questions a CFO answers — now answered for everyone.

“You can make payroll Friday — but it’s tight. Two invoices are 9 days late; want me to chase them?”

Daily cash position against real obligations, with the collections action ready to fire — not just a balance.

“Set aside $4,200 from this month for quarterly taxes.”

The number that wrecks variable-income businesses, computed and reserved — before the quarterly bill is a crisis.

“Paying this vendor 10 days early earns a 2% discount — worth $1,800. You have the cash.”

Working-capital optimization a CFO would catch, surfaced with the trade-off and a one-tap execute.

“Can you afford an associate? On current receivables, yes — here’s the cushion and the break-even.”

The growth decision modeled against the actual book, not a gut call — the analysis a practice owner never had.

“Your credit line application is ready — I’ve assembled the practice and personal financials the bank needs.”

Business-personal integration turned into credit readiness: the friction of proving yourself, removed.

“January is your slow month. Build a $12K cushion now and you’ll clear it without stress.”

Seasonal cash-flow planning held across the year — the long view a busy operator can’t keep.

03Built for how SMBs actually differ

One relationship core, three very different businesses.

Professionals

physicians, lawyers, CPAs, consultants, firms
  • Business-personal financial life is one question — Mira treats it that way
  • “Can I afford to hire? Lease or buy? Take a draw this month?”
  • Practice profitability tied to retirement and tax planning
  • No fintech owns this segment — banks treat them as generic SMBs

Platform Earners

gig workers, marketplace sellers, creators
  • Variable, multi-platform income that defeats normal underwriting
  • “How much for taxes? Can I take time off? Prove income for a lease?”
  • Income smoothing across high and low weeks
  • Fintechs sell them advances; Mira manages the whole picture

Local Operators

restaurants, salons, fitness, hospitality
  • High transaction volume, thin margins, brutal cash-flow timing
  • “Can I make payroll? Pay early for the discount? Open a second location?”
  • Seasonal swings and tip distribution complexity
  • Better financial management measurably lowers failure rates
04The integration no one else makes

One owner. One financial life. One relationship.

How banks see it today

Two strangers. A “business customer” with a checking account and a credit line. A “retail customer” with a mortgage and a 401(k). Different systems, different RMs, no shared view — even though the personal guarantee on the business loan and the owner’s draw make them the same person’s money.

How Mira sees it

One relationship. The practice’s cash flow and the owner’s household, integrated. Credit decisions that see the whole picture and move faster. Tax and retirement planning that knows the business. The integrated financial reality, finally served by one coordinator.

05The rule that governs everything

“Actively help — don’t just provide tools.”

Every bank already offers SMBs a dashboard. A dashboard is a tool that makes the owner do the work. Mira does the work and brings the owner the decision — the difference between a business checking account and a CFO. Solve the scenario, never just surface the data.
06Why the bank wins

Become the operating partner, and you own the whole relationship.

An SMB served as an integrated relationship consolidates both sides of its financial life with the bank that runs the money. Deposits deepen, product breadth widens from two or three to five-plus, credit decisions are faster and better-informed, and the relationship becomes nearly impossible to leave — because leaving means losing the CFO.
