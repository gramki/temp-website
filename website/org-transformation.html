How can you change work with AI rather than have AI help employees do the old work?

The old org chart maps to the constraint it was designed around: the scarcity of human attention. When that constraint lifts, the design inverts. Work stops moving between resolvers. Resolution assembles around the work. This is an org transformation, not a tool deployment.

The two models How it unfolds
01The old redesign trap

You can optimize every person and still have an inefficient organization.

Copilots make each analyst 20% faster at their step. But the analyst is one node in a workflow. The cost of your disputes, onboarding, credit decisions lives in the edges: the queues between teams, the handoffs that shed context, the reconciliations between systems that disagree. Optimize the nodes and the edges stay frozen. You get a faster institution doing the same thing.

Real org transformation requires a different question: "What if we designed the organization around the work itself, not around the people resolving it?" Departments, queues, and handoffs exist because no person could hold a complex domain's work whole. When AI handles complexity, the design can invert.

The person-centric org is optimized for what you already do. The work-centric org is designed for what you could become. The transformation is not incremental.

03What changes in the org structure

Four shifts that ripple through the institution.

1. From teams to Hubs

The organizing principle

Intake, Fraud, Investigation were never three jobs. They were one Stream divided among people who couldn't hold it whole. A Hub owns the Stream end-to-end. The Swarm + your best people work inside it. No handoffs between silos.

2. From headcount to dial positions

How you plan capacity

Instead of "we need 5 intake people and 8 fraud analysts," you ask: which work units are resolved by people (H), which by people with tools (H+), which by agents (A/H, A)? Your headcount plan becomes a set of explicit dial positions.

3. From project-based change to domain-based evolution

How change flows

New regulation used to mean a project across teams. Now it maps to Scenarios in the Work Model. Change lands on the model, not on the org chart. The Swarm adapts in days. Your people aren't disrupted by re-engineering every 18 months.

4. From utilization to absorption

What success means

Utilization — how busy your people are — is replaced by absorption: what percentage of the domain's work is resolved by the Swarm (A/H, A, M)? This is the only metric that matters for transformation.

04How the transformation unfolds

It's not top-down or bottom-up. It's domain-by-domain.

You pick one domain that matters (disputes, onboarding, servicing). You discover its Work Model. You induct your existing systems. You employ a Swarm. Your team learns to operate it. Then you repeat with the next domain. No big-bang re-org. No org chart redrawn all at once. The new structure emerges domain by domain.

The first domain is the hardest. You're learning new language (Work Model, Induction, Dial), new governance (who can move the dial, under what guardrails), new accountability (the Swarm is a colleague, not a tool). The second domain is measurably cheaper because it reuses the first's patterns, contracts, and governance. By the third domain, the curve inverts: each domain costs less than the last.

After three to four domains, your org has restructured itself. Not through an org-change initiative. Through the cumulative choice to resolve work at domain scale instead of at department scale.

05What each leader holds

The executive roles don't disappear. They evolve.

The CEO

Trajectory, not throughput
  • Owns the sequencing: which domains to transform, in what order, to unlock franchise value?
  • Reads the board scorecard: denominator (work inventory), absorption (dial), outcomes (apps retired, cost per resolution)
  • Answers: where is the compounding? Is each domain cheaper than the last?

The CIO

The architecture and integration
  • Governs the Work Model and the induction of existing systems (nothing forced out, everything replaceable on schedule)
  • Owns the Hubs and the Swarms: their architecture, governance, integration with bank systems
  • Answers: is the existing estate enrolled or orphaned? Can we change one thing without breaking ten?

The CTO

The platform and composition
  • Builds the platform (Fabrics) that Hubs and Swarms run on
  • Owns the contract structure: how does one domain call on another's capability without tight coupling?
  • Answers: do we have single concern? Are we getting composability? Does the 50th agent cost less than the 1st?
