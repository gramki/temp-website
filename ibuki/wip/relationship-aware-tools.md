# Relationship Management Tools
The following document aims at creating a structural understanding of the relationship management product suite.

## Relationship-aware User Tools
These are day to day financial oprations tools built or enhanced to be relationship aware.
### Day to Day (Opertaional)
* Accounts Hub: Manage and operate all accounts associated with this relationship with the corresponding entitlements. (Savings, Checking, Deposits, Credit Cards, Loans, etc.,)
* Expense Hub: Purpose-based expenses; A set of Payment instruments that auto post expenses to this hub. (Routine, Events, Projects, and Unplanned) 
* Bill Hub: All bills due to be paid and their standing-instructions/auto-pay manadates.
* Subscription Hub: Services subscribed to that may need recurring payments. Sub-part of the Bill hub.
* Loan/Installments Hub: All installments dues to be paid and the history of payments. New Offers and Eligibility assessment tools.  
* Obligations: Personal Loans, Hand-loans payable, School fees and others commitments that are not visible through Bill Hub, 
* Entitlements: Personal Income due (Salary, Rental, Dividends, etc.,), Loans due, Split expenses, etc.,

### Medium Term
* Goals and Plans Hub
* Investment Hub
* Insurance Hub: Medical, Vechicles, Property

### Long Term (Legacy)
* Property Hub: Immovable Asset, Property Documents, etc.,
* Legacy: Wills, Nominations, etc.,

## Relationship Management Tools for the Bank
* Influence Suite: Rewards, Offers, Coach & Advisor (This does not interfere with marketing tools of the bank)
* Communication Suite: Communication Personalization, Prioritization, filtering or supression (This does not interfere with marketing tools of the bank; This just acts as a forwarding proxy recepient for the customer/relationship)
* Relationship Pathways (Observability and Analytics): Predictive models to assess where a relationship is headed and the upcoming financial needs of the relationship.

Ibuki is a modern 'core' for relationship banking. It doesn't compete or replace traditional cores that host financial (savings, investments, credit, and payment/transaction) products. Ibuki hosts relationships. It aggregates the products of a relationship and orchestrates all interactions between the bank and the relationship participants.

Ibuki is a system of record for certain non-financial-product-specific information that is critical for relationship banking.
