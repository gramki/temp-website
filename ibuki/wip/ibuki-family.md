# Ibuki Family Banking: Agentic Product Strategy — Board Note (India & USA)

---

## Executive Summary

- **Objective:** Deliver a board-ready, actionable strategy to launch and scale agentic retail consumer products (PFM concierge) in India and the USA.
- **Why Now:** Real-time payment rails (UPI, FedNow/RTP) and enterprise agent platforms now enable safe, policy-bounded autonomy for financial services.
- **Expected Outcomes (3–5 Years):** +5–10 point NPS, 2–5% revenue uplift, 15–20% lower cost-to-serve, increased retention through proactive/reversible automation.
- **What Ibuki Family Banking Is:** A consumer-centric, agent-first product suite that delivers relationship banking with consented data, signed-intent execution, and continuous controls.
- **What Ibuki Family Banking Is Not:** Not KYC/AML tooling nor a risk platform—those are dependencies controlled externally (see Operations Strategy).
- **References:** See [org-8.0/ibuki/agentic-banking-products.md](org-8.0/ibuki/agentic-banking-products.md) for deeper taxonomy, technical architecture, and OEM requirements. This note focuses on retail decisions and board actions.
- **Autonomy Scope:** L0–L1 only (excludes L2/L3 autonomy).
- **Pilots:** Initiate parallel pilots in India and the USA.
- **Branding:** "Ibuki" is Zeta’s umbrella for agentic banking. This document describes the Family Banking experience, “Ibuki Family Banking.”

---

## Market Context: India & USA

- **India:** India Stack (UPI/UPI Lite), DPDP—public digital infrastructure accelerates rollout of new models.
- **USA:** FedNow/RTP, ACH modernization; GLBA/Reg P and state privacy rules; strong requirements for conduct and suitability.
- **Implication:** Standardize agent platform and operating model, but localize consent, payments, and credit features per region.

## Product Portfolio & Value Proposition

### Ibuki Family Banking

- **Visibility & Control:** Unified cashflow, bills, savings, investments—cross-institution—via consented data.
- **Ease of Banking:** Plan and execute savings, borrowing, investing with signed-intent and (where applicable) reversibility.
- **Goals:** Manage short-, medium-, and long-term goals with suitability-checked nudges and automation.
- **Safety:** Enforced explicit consent, explainability, disclosures, and audit trails.

### Relationship Experiences

- **Relationship Banking:** Household-level interfaces—dashboards, shared goals, controlled delegation.
- **Family Banking:** Segmented guidance for minors/guardians with supervised autonomy.

## Go-to-Market & Distribution

- **Channels:** Consistent agentic banking across mobile, web, branch, and contact centers.
- **Embeddability:** Relationship UI and widgets embeddable in agent flows.
- **Influence Stack:** Features, segmentation, offers, rewards, campaigns orchestrated algorithmically with conduct compliance.

## Product Architecture: Trust-First Principles

- **Resource & Knowledge Repositories:** Relationship knowledgebase, documents, memory.
- **Product Gateways:** Secure interfaces for payments, accounts, cards, investments, insurance with policy hooks and signed actions.
- **Feature Stores:** Governed, explainable features supporting risk-aware personalization and actions.
- **Relationship Toolset:**
  - Relationship Memory
  - Relationship Feature Store
  - Relationship Knowledgebase (Documents)
  - Social Graph/Network
  - Identity, Authentication, and Access Control
  - Relationship Progression Paths
- **Non-functional:** Reliability, observability, auditability, cost controls; reversible actions where feasible; human-in-the-loop for higher-risk flows.

---

## Regulation, Conduct & Privacy

- **Suitability & Disclosures:** Explainability, alternatives, costs/risks; replayable audit logs of agent reasoning and actions.
- **Privacy & Consent:** DPDP (India), GLBA/Reg P (US), state privacy—data minimization and bank-native consent management enforced.
- **Payments Risk:** Strong authentication, signed-intent, out-of-band checks for high-risk actions, velocity limits, anomaly detection.
- **Vulnerable Customers:** Easy opt-outs, supervised autonomy, “cooling-off” features.

---

## KPIs & Economics

- **Board Gates:** NPS and revenue uplift.
- **Customer Experience:** NPS, MAU, feature adoption, intent-to-use, time-to-resolution.
- **Financial:** Revenue uplift, balance growth, retention, cross-sell/upsell conversion, unit cost per task.
- **Safety:** Refusal accuracy, escalation rate, adverse outcome rate, signed-intent coverage.
- **Economics:** Modelled unit costs by use case; cohort-level causal lift analysis; staged rollout with KPI and safety-led gates.

---

## Roadmap (3–5 Years)

- **Phase 0 (0–6 Months):** Launch L0–L1 consumer concierge pilots in India & USA; bank-native consent plumbing, autonomy policies, change communications.
- **Phase 1 (6–18 Months):** Scale best pilots; add UPI and US real-time rails (RTP/FedNow); maintain L1 autonomy (reversible actions); embed safety evaluation.
- **Phase 2 (18–36 Months):** Expand to wealth & mortgage copilots; enhance suitability/pricing, deepen L1 autonomy.
- **Phase 3 (36–60 Months):** Optimize for scale economics; deepen channel consistency and autonomy within L1 standards.

---

## Build/Buy and OEM Requirements

- **Tools:** Policy-hooked, signed-action interfaces; deterministic execution for critical flows.
- **Consent & Audit:** Embedded toolkits, explainable recommendations, suitability checks, rate controls, and guardrails.
- **Interoperability:** Supports UPI, US real-time rails, deep core/card/investment integrations.

---

## Operating Model & Governance

- **Release Governance:** Product release board promotes autonomy (L0 → L1) only with proven safety, audit.
- **Clear Decision Rights:** Across product, risk, compliance, engineering, and legal.
- **Customer Communication:** Focus on change management for agent experience adoption.

---

## Investment & Partnership Model

- **Co-Investment:** 5–10% equity per bank (3+ banks), $200–300M valuation; IP transfer after 5 years (option for equity buyback); 5-year exclusivity per bank.
- **Revenue:** Sourcing fees (MFN to investor banks), customer fees (all touchpoints), partner/platform fees.
- **Bank Costs:** Integration, product specialization, Tachyon/Olympus, infrastructure, and consumables.
- **Execution:** Delivered by banks’ own studios (BoT) or digital factories; strict data isolation per bank; first release in ~18 months with milestone tracking.

---

## Board Decisions Requested

- Approve Phase 0 parallel concierge pilots; endorse KPIs (NPS, revenue uplift).
- Endorse limited autonomy policy (L0–L1 only) and signed-intent requirement.
- Approve investment envelope, co-investment terms, and partner RFPs.
- Support regulator engagement plan; endorse transparency requirements.
