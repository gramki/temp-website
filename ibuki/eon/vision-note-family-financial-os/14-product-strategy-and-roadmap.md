# 14. Product Strategy & Roadmap
FFOS rollout balances business value, regulatory readiness, and technical maturity. Product strategy emphasizes measurable household outcomes, RM adoption, and progressive automation.

## 14.1 Phased Rollout Strategy
Deploy FFOS incrementally, aligning each drop with data readiness, consent/governance maturity, and channel enablement. Phases include structured change management, training, and risk reviews.

### 14.1.1 Pilot Scope and Segments
Begin with priority or affluent households, employee betas, or specific micro-markets with supportive regulators. Limit scope to selected channels and products, focusing on concierge agents, obligation management, and RM visibility. Success metrics include engagement, retention, operational efficiency, and control adherence.

### 14.1.2 Progressive Feature Enablement
Phase 2 expands to cashflow orchestration, governance agents, and broader channel footprints. Later phases automate execution agents, introduce partner integrations, and expose ecosystem APIs. Each step has go/no-go criteria tied to telemetry, control effectiveness, and RM adoption.

## 14.2 Capability Maturity Model for FFOS

### 14.2.1 Level 1: Family View & Basic Automation
Deliver unified family profile, obligation detection, manual approvals, RM-led workflows, and foundational memories (core, graph, consent). Success hinges on data accuracy, governance readiness, and RM trust.

### 14.2.2 Level 2: Agentic Advisory & Orchestration
Foundation agents feed think/orchestrator agents for proactive advice, scenario planning, and semi-automated execution with governance oversight. KPIs include advice adoption, reduced delinquency, and improved RM productivity.

### 14.2.3 Level 3: Fully Personalized, Multi-Generational OS
FFOS becomes the default operating fabric for multi-generational planning, autonomous workflows with explainability, ecosystem extensions, and embedded partner value. KPIs shift to lifetime value, new revenue streams, and regulatory endorsements.

## 14.3 Bank Adoption Models

### 14.3.1 Single-Country Single-Bank Deployment
Deploy FFOS per bank entity with localized compliance artifacts, data residency controls, and channel integrations tailored to national platforms. Suitable for proving value quickly while maintaining tight governance.

### 14.3.2 Multi-Region Strategy Over Time
Introduce regional hubs with shared platform services and localized data planes. Standardize agent templates, product abstractions, and IAM patterns to accelerate rollouts while satisfying jurisdictional policies. Governance boards coordinate cross-market learnings and regulatory engagements.
