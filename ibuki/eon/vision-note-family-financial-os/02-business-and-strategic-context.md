# 2. Business & Strategic Context

## 2.1 Market Context: From Individual Banking to Family Banking
Households now operate as micro-enterprises with blended income streams, shared obligations, and intergenerational decision-making. Regulators push for inclusive finance, caregivers demand oversight tools, and embedded finance ecosystems expect programmable interfaces. Traditional single-account constructs cannot handle power-of-attorney scenarios, caregivers with limited rights, or multi-step approvals. FFOS positions the bank to become the trusted orchestrator of household cashflow, obligations, and goals, enabling new fee models (subscription, advisory retainers), improved deposit stickiness, and deeper relationships with the “family CFO” personas emerging across segments.

## 2.2 Limitations of Current Digital & Relationship Banking Models
Existing digital and RM systems are product-centric and case-by-case. Limitations include:
- **Fragmented memory**: No persistent household state across channels; RMs and agents rebuild context using spreadsheets.
- **Consent chaos**: Delegations live in scanned PDFs or CRM notes, offering no executable entitlements for digital journeys.
- **Channel silos**: Mobile, web, contact center, and RM workbenches enforce different approval and logging behaviors, creating inconsistent guardrails.
- **Limited automation**: AI pilots focus on next-best-offer surfaces but cannot execute multi-actor workflows because they lack IPC fabric, workflow primitives, and auditable reasoning stores.
Without FFOS, banks cannot guarantee compliance or deliver premium experiences for caregivers, teens, or elders, leading to leakage to fintechs that offer shared wallets and coordinated planning.

## 2.3 Why Families as First-Class Financial Entities
Elevating the family to the canonical entity unlocks:
- **Operational clarity**: Household-level KYC, obligations, and risk posture enable proactive intervention on missed payments, caregiving transitions, or succession planning.
- **Product leverage**: Bundled offerings (family credit lines, multi-account sweeps, shared savings goals) require shared oversight and channel-consistent permissions.
- **Data richness**: Core memory and feature stores capturing household timelines improve credit underwriting, marketing attribution, and ESG reporting, all governed by consent.
- **Loyalty and trust**: Families appreciate delegated access with policy-driven guardrails, increasing retention and enabling premium advisory fees.
Families already expect banks to coordinate parents, teens, elders, and assistants—FFOS provides the infrastructure for doing so responsibly.

## 2.4 Role of Agentic Systems in Next-Generation Banking
Agentic systems are the only scalable way to combine real-time sensing, reasoning, and execution across millions of households. FFOS mandates:
- **Foundation agents** that translate core memory, graph memory, and feature store data into authoritative state vectors.
- **Think/Do/Orchestrator/Governance agents** that operate under explicit consent tokens, exchange context via IPC/IAC, and log every decision in audit-ready timelines.
- **External I/O adapters** that feed approvals, exceptions, and notifications through bank-governed channels with consistent policy enforcement.
This multi-agent pattern lowers marginal cost per journey, ensures explainability, and lets banks deploy new experiences or regulatory mandates quickly without rewriting core logic.
