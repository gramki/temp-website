# 15. Governance & Operating Model
FFOS requires disciplined ownership to satisfy regulators and sustain innovation. Governance aligns product, technology, risk, and vendor ecosystems with clear accountability matrices.

## 15.1 Ownership & Stewardship (Bank vs Tech Provider)
Bank retains ownership of data, policies, IAM, runtime operations, and regulatory commitments. Tech provider or internal platform teams supply blueprints, accelerators, and managed tooling where contracted but operate under bank oversight. Joint RACI clarifies responsibility for backlog prioritization, releases, SRE, security hardening, and compliance evidence.

## 15.2 Joint Governance Structures (Product, Risk, Compliance)
Establish an FFOS Program Board with product, risk, compliance, IT, and operations leads. Monthly governance reviews track KPIs, control effectiveness, regulatory interactions, incident learnings, and change requests. Standing committees manage consent policy updates, model governance, data ethics, and incident triage with escalation paths to board committees.

## 15.3 Change Management & Release Governance
Adopt release trains with pre-production validation of workflows, agents, integration adapters, and non-functional tests. Changes require risk/compliance sign-off, automated test evidence, rollback plans, and communication packages for RMs, servicing, and digital teams. Feature flags and staged rollouts provide controlled exposure.

## 15.4 Vendor & SLA Considerations
Vendors providing infrastructure, data tooling, or specialized agents must adhere to bank security policies, data residency requirements, control testing, and SLA targets (availability, latency, response). Contracts include audit rights, sub-processor disclosures, incident notification windows, and remediation obligations. Vendor performance feeds governance dashboards for renewal decisions.
