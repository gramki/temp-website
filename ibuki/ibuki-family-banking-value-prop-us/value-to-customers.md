An intelligent family banking agent—Mira or Ally—delivers **$2,000 to $8,000 in annual value** per family through:

| Value Driver | Annual Impact |
|--------------|---------------|
| Time savings | $1,200 - $4,500 |

## Target Family Profile

Our value model is based on a US middle-class family:

| Attribute | Assumption |
|-----------|------------|
| Family composition | 4 members (2 adults, 2 children/dependents) |
| Household income | $50,000-$150,000/year |
| Banking relationships | 1-2 banks |
| Financial products | Checking, savings, credit cards, 1-2 loans, insurance, investments |
| Digital comfort | Moderate to high (uses mobile banking, payment apps) |
| Financial literacy | Basic to moderate |

This profile represents approximately 300,000-900,000 households per mid-sized bank — the core addressable market for relationship banking.

## Value Dimensions

### 1. Time Savings

#### The Hidden Cost of Financial Administration

Families spend significant time on routine financial tasks. This time is invisible but substantial.

**Current State: Time Spent on Financial Tasks (R5.1)**

| Task | Frequency | Time per Instance | Annual Hours |
|------|-----------|-------------------|--------------|
| Bill payments | 8-12/month | 5-10 min | 12-24 hrs |
| Account checking & reconciliation | Weekly | 15-20 min | 13-17 hrs |
| Investment review & decisions | Monthly | 30-60 min | 6-12 hrs |
| Tax document collection | Annual (2-3 month process) | 5-10 hrs total | 5-10 hrs |
| Dispute resolution & support calls | 2-4/year | 1-2 hrs each | 2-8 hrs |
| Family financial discussions | Monthly | 30-60 min | 6-12 hrs |
| Product research & applications | 2-3/year | 2-4 hrs each | 4-12 hrs |

**Total: 45-81 hours/year** spent on financial administration

That's **1-2 full work weeks** every year, just managing money.

#### With the Intelligent Agent

| Task | How Agent Helps | Time Reduction |
|------|-----------------|----------------|
| Bill payments | Voice command or auto-pay; Mira remembers all billers | 80% |
| Account reconciliation | Proactive summaries ("You spent $3,500 this month, up 10%") | 70% |
| Investment decisions | Advisor synthesizes options with family context | 50% |
| Tax documents | Steward tracks, reminds, inventories automatically | 60% |
| Disputes | Support agent handles; user just confirms | 50% |
| Family discussions | Advisor pre-analyzes trade-offs before family conversation | 40% |
| Product research | Thinker advises based on actual eligibility and fit | 60% |

**Result: 30-60 hours saved per year**

#### Value Calculation

| Metric | Conservative | Moderate | Optimistic |
|--------|--------------|----------|------------|
| Hours saved | 30 hrs | 45 hrs | 60 hrs |
| Value per hour* | $40 | $50 | $75 |
| **Annual value** | **$1,200** | **$2,250** | **$4,500** |

*Value of time based on opportunity cost for US professionals. Even at minimum wage ($15/hr), value exceeds $450/year.

### 2. Error Prevention

#### The Cost of Financial Mistakes

Small errors compound. A missed payment isn't just a fee—it's credit score impact, mental stress, and time to resolve.

**Common Financial Errors (R5.5, R5.6, R5.7, R5.8)**

| Error Type | Frequency (without agent) | Cost per Instance | Annual Cost |
|------------|---------------------------|-------------------|-------------|
| Credit card late payment fee | 1-2/year | $25-$40 | $25-$80 |
| Mortgage late payment fee | 0.5-1/year | $50-$100 + credit impact | $25-$100 |
| Utility late fees | 0.5/year | $10-$50 | $5-$25 |
| Overdraft fees | 1-3/year | $35 | $35-$105 |
| Tax penalties | 0.2-0.5/year | $50-$500 | $10-$250 |
| Credit score impact | 0.2-0.5/year | $200-$1,000 (lifetime cost) | $40-$500 |

**Current annual error cost: $140 - $1,060**

#### How the Agent Prevents Errors

| Error | Prevention Mechanism |
|-------|---------------------|
| Late payments | Steward tracks all due dates; Concierge reminds; Doer can auto-pay |
| Overdrafts | Advisor monitors cash flow; alerts before shortfall |
| Utility disconnection | Proactive reminders well before due date |
| Tax penalties | Steward tracks deadlines; surfaces 30 days before |
| Credit score damage | Protector monitors payment patterns; alerts on risks |

**Error reduction: 70-80%**

#### Value Calculation

| Metric | Conservative | Moderate | Optimistic |
|--------|--------------|----------|------------|
| Current error cost | $140 | $500 | $1,060 |
| Reduction | 60% | 70% | 80% |
| **Annual savings** | **$84** | **$350** | **$848** |

### 3. Fraud Protection

#### The Growing Threat

Digital payments have exploded. So has digital fraud. Most families are vulnerable.

**Fraud Exposure in US Families (R5.9, R5.10)**

| Fraud Type | Annual Probability | Average Loss if Hit |
|------------|-------------------|---------------------|
| Credit card fraud | 5-10% | $500-$1,000 |
| Identity theft | 2-3% | $1,000-$2,000 |
| Account takeover | 1-2% | $1,500-$3,000 |

**Expected annual loss (probability-weighted): $50 - $200**

Many families have at least one fraud incident every 3-5 years. When it happens, the impact is significant—financially and emotionally.

#### How Protector Defends the Family

| Capability | Impact |
|------------|--------|
| Real-time transaction monitoring | Detects anomalies in seconds, not hours |
| Behavioral pattern recognition | Knows "unusual" for each family member |
| Immediate blocking | Stops fraud before completion |
| Family-wide alerts | Warns other members of emerging threats |
| Proactive security posture | Identifies vulnerable patterns before exploitation |

**Fraud reduction: 70-80%**

Additionally, even when fraud occurs:
- Faster detection = lower loss (R5.10: seconds vs. hours/days)
- Immediate Support agent engagement = faster recovery
- Better documentation = higher dispute success rate

#### Value Calculation

| Metric | Conservative | Moderate | Optimistic |
|--------|--------------|----------|------------|
| Expected annual fraud loss | $50 | $125 | $200 |
| Reduction | 50% | 60% | 75% |
| **Annual savings** | **$25** | **$75** | **$150** |

### 4. Better Financial Decisions

#### The Advice Gap

Wealthy families have financial advisors. Everyone else makes decisions alone—or doesn't make them at all.

**Common Suboptimal Decisions (R5.11, R5.12)**

| Decision Area | What Happens | Annual Cost |
|---------------|--------------|-------------|
| Idle savings | $10K-$50K sitting at 0.5% instead of 4-5% | $400-$2,000 opportunity cost |
| Wrong loan timing | Taking loan when rates are high, not prepaying when beneficial | $500-$1,500 over tenure (amortized) |
| Insurance mismatch | Over-insured (wasted premiums) or under-insured (coverage gaps) | $200-$1,000/year |
| Tax inefficiency | Not maximizing deductions, credits, tax-advantaged accounts | $1,000-$3,000/year |
| Emergency fund neglect | No buffer → forced to break investments or take high-interest debt | $200-$1,000 when crisis hits |
| Goal-product mismatch | Wrong instrument for time horizon/risk | $300-$1,000 in suboptimal returns |

**Most families leave $2,600-$8,500/year on the table** through suboptimal decisions.

#### How Advisor + Thinkers Improve Decisions

| Decision Area | Agent Intervention | Improvement |
|---------------|-------------------|-------------|
| Idle funds | Investments Thinker: "You have $20K earning 0.5%. Given your education goal in 5 years, a high-yield savings or balanced fund could earn 4-5%." | $700-$2,000/year |
| Loan optimization | Loans Thinker: "Prepaying $10K now saves $2,000 in interest over remaining tenure." | $200-$500/year (amortized) |
| Insurance right-sizing | Insurance Thinker: "Your term cover is $500K but family needs $1M. Your health cover is adequate." | $200-$500/year |
| Tax optimization | Steward: "You've used $5K of $6.5K IRA limit. $1.5K more saves $300-$450 tax." | $300-$1,000/year |
| Emergency fund | Advisor: "You need 6 months expenses ($15K). Currently at $5K. Prioritize this before new investments." | $200-$500 (crisis avoided) |
| Goal-product alignment | Advisor: "For 15-year retirement goal, shift from savings to equity-heavy allocation." | $500-$1,500/year in better returns |

#### Value Calculation

| Metric | Conservative | Moderate | Optimistic |
|--------|--------------|----------|------------|
| Current decision gap | $2,600 | $5,000 | $8,500 |
| Improvement captured | 60% | 65% | 70% |
| **Annual value** | **$1,560** | **$3,250** | **$5,950** |

### 5. Goal Achievement

#### The Planning-Execution Gap

US families have aspirations. But aspirations don't become achievements without structure.

**Current State of Family Goal Achievement (R5.13)**

| Finding | Statistic |
|---------|-----------|
| Families with written financial goals | ~30-40% |
| Families tracking progress regularly | ~20-30% |
| Goal achievement rate (within 20% of target) | 40-50% |
| Primary reasons for failure | No tracking, no adjustment, competing priorities |

**The gap between intention and achievement is massive.**

#### What Goal-Aware Agents Change

| Current State | With Agents |
|---------------|-------------|
| Goals exist only in mind | Goals recorded, structured, prioritized |
| Progress unknown until deadline | Monthly progress updates from Concierge |
| No course correction | Advisor flags deviations early, suggests adjustments |
| Competing priorities unresolved | Advisor helps family make explicit trade-offs |
| Single-point decisions | Continuous tracking and optimization |

**Result:**
- Goal achievement rate: 40-50% → 70-80%
- Average goal shortfall: 30% → 10%

#### Value Calculation

**Example: $25,000 Education Fund Goal (5-year horizon)**

| Scenario | Outcome |
|----------|---------|
| Without agent | Achieves $17,500 (70% of goal). Shortfall covered by loan at 8% or goal compromise. |
| With agent | Achieves $23,750 (95% of goal). Minor adjustment sufficient. |

**Difference: $6,250** for this one goal.

Most families have 3-5 concurrent goals. Improvement across all goals is substantial.

| Metric | Conservative | Moderate | Optimistic |
|--------|--------------|----------|------------|
| Annual value (across all goals) | $1,000 | $1,500 | $2,000 |

### 6. Stress Reduction

#### The Invisible Burden

Financial stress is pervasive but unmeasured. It affects health, relationships, and work performance.

**Sources of Financial Stress**

| Stressor | Impact |
|----------|--------|
| Uncertainty about financial health | Constant worry |
| Coordination friction | Family arguments about money |
| Fear of missing deadlines | Anxiety about bills, taxes |
| Decision paralysis | Overwhelmed by choices |
| Crisis unpreparedness | Fear of unexpected events |

**How Agents Reduce Stress**

| Stressor | Agent Solution |
|----------|----------------|
| Uncertainty | Proactive financial health updates |
| Coordination | Automatic expense tracking and settlement |
| Deadlines | Proactive reminders and auto-pay |
| Decisions | Clear recommendations with rationale |
| Crises | Emergency fund monitoring and alerts |

**Value:** While difficult to quantify, stress reduction is real and valuable. Estimated at $500-$1,000/year in improved quality of life.

## Strategic Implications

- $2,000-$8,000 in quantifiable annual value

No mid-sized US bank offers:
- Family-aware financial management
