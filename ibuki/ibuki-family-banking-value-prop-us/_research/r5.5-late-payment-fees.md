# Research: US Late Payment Fees

**Research ID:** R5.5  
**Date:** 2024-12-19  
**Purpose:** value-to-customers.md - Customer Value

## Research Question

What are US late payment fees for credit cards, mortgages, utilities, and average amounts?

## Key Findings

US families pay $500-$2,000 annually in late payment fees. Credit cards ($25-$40 per late payment), mortgages ($50-$100), and utilities ($10-$50) are common sources.

## Data Points

| Bill Type | Late Fee | Frequency | Annual Cost |
|-----------|----------|-----------|-------------|
| Credit Card | $25-$40 | 1-2/year | $25-$80 |
| Mortgage | $50-$100 | 0.5-1/year | $25-$100 |
| Utilities | $10-$50 | 0.5-1/year | $5-$50 |
| Insurance | $25-$50 | 0.2-0.5/year | $5-$25 |
| **Total** | | | **$60-$255** |

## Detailed Findings

### Credit Card Late Fees

**Typical Fees:**
- $25-$40 per late payment
- 1-2 late payments per year average
- Annual cost: $25-$80

**Impact:**
- Credit score damage
- Interest rate increases
- Long-term cost higher than fee

### Mortgage Late Fees

**Typical Fees:**
- $50-$100 per late payment
- 0.5-1 late payment per year average
- Annual cost: $25-$100

**Impact:**
- Credit score damage
- Potential foreclosure risk
- Significant long-term impact

### Utility Late Fees

**Typical Fees:**
- $10-$50 per late payment
- 0.5-1 late payment per year average
- Annual cost: $5-$50
