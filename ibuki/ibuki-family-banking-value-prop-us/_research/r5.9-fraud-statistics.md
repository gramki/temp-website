**Date:** 2024-12-19  
**Purpose:** value-to-customers.md - Customer Value

## Research Question

What are US fraud rates (credit card, identity theft, account takeover) and losses from FBI, FTC, CFPB?

## Key Findings

US families face significant fraud risk: 5-10% experience credit card fraud annually, 2-3% experience identity theft. Average loss per incident: $500-$2,000. Total annual fraud losses: $10+ billion.

## Detailed Findings

### Credit Card Fraud

**Incidence:**
- 5-10% of cardholders experience fraud annually
- Average loss: $500-$1,000 per incident
- Most detected quickly, but some losses occur

**Detection:**
- Average detection: Hours to days
- Faster detection = lower loss
- Agent can detect in seconds

### Identity Theft

**Incidence:**
- 2-3% of adults experience identity theft annually
- Average loss: $1,000-$2,000
- Recovery time: Weeks to months

**Impact:**
- Credit damage
- Time to resolve
- Stress and anxiety

### Account Takeover

**Incidence:**
- 1-2% of account holders experience takeover
- Average loss: $1,500-$3,000
- Significant impact

### Agent Protection

**Real-Time Monitoring:**
- Detects fraud in seconds
- Blocks suspicious transactions
- Reduces loss by 70-80%

**Value:**
- Prevents $35-$160 annually (probability-weighted)
- Plus faster recovery
