# Research: US Regulatory Context

**Research ID:** R2.13  
**Date:** 2024-12-19  
**Purpose:** family-banking-product-proposal.md - Market Need

## Research Question

What are the US regulatory requirements (FDIC insurance, CFPB regulations, state banking regulations, privacy laws) relevant to family banking?

## Key Findings

US banking is heavily regulated by FDIC (insurance), CFPB (consumer protection), state regulators, and privacy laws (state-specific). Family banking features must comply with all applicable regulations.

## Data Points

| Regulation | Key Requirement | Impact |
|------------|----------------|--------|
| FDIC Insurance | $250,000 per account type | Account structure considerations |
| CFPB Fair Lending | Non-discrimination | Family features must be fair |
| CFPB Disclosures | Clear, timely disclosures | Agent communications must comply |
| State Banking Regulations | Vary by state | Multi-state compliance needed |
| Privacy Laws (CCPA, etc.) | Data protection | Family data sharing rules |
| GLBA | Financial privacy | Information sharing restrictions |

## Detailed Findings

### FDIC Insurance

**Coverage:**
- $250,000 per account type per bank
- Joint accounts: $250,000 per co-owner
- Different account types insured separately

**Family Banking Implications:**
- Account structure affects insurance coverage
- Need to explain coverage to families
- Coordination across accounts

### CFPB Regulations

**Fair Lending:**
- Equal access to products
- Non-discriminatory practices
- Family features must be available to all eligible customers

**Disclosures:**
- Clear, timely information
- Agent communications must include required disclosures
- Fee transparency

### State Regulations

**Variations:**
- State banking departments regulate state-chartered banks
- Some states have additional consumer protections
- Multi-state operations require compliance

### Privacy Laws

**Federal (GLBA):**
- Financial privacy requirements
- Information sharing restrictions
- Opt-out rights

**State (CCPA, etc.):**
- California Consumer Privacy Act
- Other state privacy laws
- Data protection requirements
