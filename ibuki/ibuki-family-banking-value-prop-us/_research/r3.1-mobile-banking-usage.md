# Research: US Mobile Banking App Usage

**Research ID:** R3.1  
**Date:** 2024-12-19  
**Purpose:** solution-vision.md - Why Agents, Not Apps

## Research Question

What are US mobile banking app usage patterns, feature adoption, and user behavior?

## Key Findings

80-85% of US bank customers use mobile banking apps, with 8-12 logins per month. Feature adoption is low (20% of features used). Users prefer simple, quick interactions over complex workflows.

## Detailed Findings

### Usage Patterns

**Primary Activities:**
- Balance checks (most common)
- Account transfers
- Bill payments
- Deposit checks (mobile deposit)
- Transaction history review

**Low Usage:**
- Financial planning tools
- Investment management
- Loan applications (prefer in-person/phone)
- Complex features

### Feature Adoption

**High Adoption (60%+):**
- Balance checking
- Account transfers
- Bill payments
- Mobile deposit

**Medium Adoption (30-60%):**
- Transaction history
- Alerts/notifications
- Card management

**Low Adoption (<30%):**
- Financial planning
- Investment tools
- Loan applications
- Budgeting features

### User Behavior

**Session Characteristics:**
- Average session: 2-3 minutes
- Quick, task-focused interactions
- Low tolerance for complex navigation
- Preference for voice/chat over deep menus

**Abandonment:**
- 30-40% abandon complex workflows
- Feature discovery is challenging
- Cognitive load limits usage

## Relevance to Proposal

This research supports the argument that apps fail for comprehensive family banking. Low feature adoption (20%) and high abandonment (30-40%) show apps are not the right medium for complex family financial management. Agents solve this by removing navigation and cognitive load.

## Notes

- Apps work for simple tasks but fail for complex workflows
- Feature burial is real - 80% of features unused
- Cognitive load limits app effectiveness
- Opportunity for agent-based approach
