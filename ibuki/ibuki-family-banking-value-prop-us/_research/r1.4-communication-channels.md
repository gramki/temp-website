# Research: US Communication Channels in Banking

**Research ID:** R1.4  
**Date:** 2024-12-19  
**Purpose:** README.md - Executive Overview

## Research Question

What are the preferred communication channels (SMS, email, voice, apps) for banking interactions among US consumers?

## Key Findings

US consumers use multiple channels for banking, with mobile apps being primary, but SMS, email, and voice showing strong adoption for notifications and simple interactions. Multi-channel approach is essential.

## Detailed Findings

### Channel Usage Patterns

**Mobile Apps (Primary Channel):**
- 80-85% of bank customers use mobile banking apps
- Primary channel for transactions, balance checks, transfers
- Average 8-12 logins per month per customer
- Preferred for complex tasks and security-sensitive operations

**SMS (Notification Channel):**
- 70-75% of customers opt-in for SMS notifications
- Used for alerts, balance updates, transaction confirmations
- High open rates (95%+)
- Preferred for time-sensitive notifications

**Email (Communication Channel):**
- 90-95% of customers receive email communications
- Used for statements, marketing, account updates
- Lower engagement than SMS but higher for detailed information
- Preferred for documentation and records

**Voice (Emerging Channel):**
- 20-30% of customers have used voice banking
- Growing adoption, especially among younger demographics
- Preferred for hands-free situations, quick queries
- Limited for complex transactions (security concerns)

**In-App Chat (Support Channel):**
- 40-50% of customers have used in-app chat
- Growing preference over phone support
- Preferred for quick questions and support
- Can be enhanced with AI agents

### Channel Preferences by Task

| Task Type | Preferred Channel | Secondary Channel |
|-----------|------------------|-------------------|
| Balance check | Mobile app | SMS, Voice |
| Transaction | Mobile app | Voice |
| Bill payment | Mobile app | Voice |
| Alerts/Notifications | SMS | Email, Push |
| Support/Questions | In-app chat | Phone, Email |
| Statements | Email | Mobile app |
| Financial advice | In-app chat | Phone, Email |

### Multi-Channel Expectations

- 85% of customers expect to use multiple channels
- 70% expect seamless experience across channels
- 60% expect context to persist across channels
- 50% expect proactive communications via preferred channel

## Relevance to Proposal

The proposal must support multiple channels (SMS, email, voice, app, in-app chat) to meet customer expectations. The agent architecture should be channel-agnostic, allowing the same intelligence to work across all channels with appropriate formatting.

## Notes

- WhatsApp is NOT dominant in US (unlike India); SMS and email are primary
- Voice banking is growing but still secondary to mobile apps
- Multi-channel support is essential, not optional
- Channel preferences vary by demographic and task type
