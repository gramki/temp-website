**Date:** 2024-12-19

## Research Question

What are US family insurance scenarios including health, life, auto, home, and disability insurance patterns?

## Key Findings

US families typically have multiple insurance policies: health ($200-$600/month), auto ($100-$200/month), home ($100-$200/month), and life ($50-$200/month). Many families are underinsured or overinsured.

## Detailed Findings

### Health Insurance

**Coverage Types:**
- Employer-sponsored (most common)
- Marketplace (ACA)
- Medicare/Medicaid
- Private plans

**Family Challenges:**
- Coordinating coverage across family members
- Understanding benefits and costs
- Open enrollment decisions
- Coverage gaps

### Life Insurance

**Types:**
- Term life (most common)
- Whole life
- Universal life

**Coverage Patterns:**
- 50-60% of families have life insurance
- Average coverage: $250,000-$500,000
- Often underinsured
- Beneficiary management

### Auto Insurance

**Coverage:**
- Liability (required)
- Collision
- Comprehensive
- Uninsured motorist

**Family Scenarios:**
- Multiple vehicles
- Teen drivers (higher rates)
- Coverage optimization

### Home Insurance

**Coverage:**
- Dwelling coverage
- Personal property
- Liability
- Additional living expenses

**Family Scenarios:**
- Coverage adequacy
- Deductible decisions
- Claims management

This research informs insurance-related scenarios. Families need help managing multiple policies, optimizing coverage, and ensuring adequacy. The agent can provide insurance review, optimization recommendations, and renewal reminders.
