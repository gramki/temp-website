# Research: US Market Comparables

**Research ID:** R5.14  
**Date:** 2024-12-19  
**Purpose:** value-to-customers.md - Customer Value

## Research Question

What are US financial advisor costs, tax preparer costs, personal finance app costs, and premium banking costs?

## Key Findings

US families pay $200-$5,000 annually for financial services: financial advisors ($1,000-$5,000), tax preparers ($200-$500), personal finance apps ($50-$150), premium banking ($100-$500). Agent provides comprehensive service at lower cost.

## Data Points

| Service | Annual Cost | What It Covers |
|---------|-------------|----------------|
| Financial advisor (fee-only) | $1,000-$5,000 | Investment advice only |
| Tax preparer | $200-$500 | Tax filing only |
| Personal finance app | $50-$150 | Expense tracking, budgeting |
| Premium banking | $100-$500 | Enhanced features, concierge |
| **Total (if using all)** | **$1,350-$6,150** | Partial coverage |

## Detailed Findings

### Financial Advisors

**Fee-Only Advisors:**
- $1,000-$5,000 annually
- Investment advice
- Financial planning
- Limited ongoing support

**Limitations:**
- Expensive
- Limited scope
- Not always available

### Tax Preparers

**Costs:**
- $200-$500 annually
- Tax filing only
- No year-round support

**Limitations:**
- Once-a-year service
- No ongoing tax optimization

### Personal Finance Apps

**Costs:**
- $50-$150 annually
- Expense tracking
- Budgeting
- Limited advice

**Limitations:**
- Manual data entry
- No execution
- Limited family features

### Premium Banking

**Costs:**
- $100-$500 annually
- Enhanced features
- Concierge services
- Limited intelligence

**Limitations:**
- Basic features
- Not family-aware
- Limited AI
