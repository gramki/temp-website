# Research: US Voice Banking Adoption

**Research ID:** R3.2  
**Date:** 2024-12-19  
**Purpose:** solution-vision.md - Why Agents, Not Apps

## Research Question

What is the adoption of voice banking and voice assistant usage in financial context?

## Key Findings

Voice banking adoption is 20-30% but growing. Users are comfortable with voice assistants for general tasks but less so for financial transactions. Voice is preferred for quick queries and hands-free situations.

## Detailed Findings

### Voice Assistant Adoption

**Ownership:**
- Smart speakers: 35-40% of households
- Mobile voice assistants: 50-60% (Siri, Google Assistant)
- Growing adoption, especially younger demographics

**Usage Patterns:**
- General queries: High usage
- Financial queries: Lower but growing
- Transactions: Low usage (security concerns)

### Voice Banking Use Cases

**High Adoption:**
- Balance checks
- Account information
- Quick questions

**Low Adoption:**
- Transactions (security concerns)
- Complex queries
- Sensitive information

### User Preferences

**Voice Preferred For:**
- Quick queries
- Hands-free situations
- Simple information requests

**Voice Not Preferred For:**
- Complex transactions
- Sensitive operations
- Detailed information

## Relevance to Proposal

Voice is an important channel but not sufficient alone. Multi-channel approach (voice + text + app) is needed. Agents can work across all channels, adapting to user preferences and task complexity.

## Notes

- Voice adoption is growing but still secondary to mobile apps
- Security concerns limit voice transaction usage
- Multi-channel approach is essential
- Agents can work across channels seamlessly
