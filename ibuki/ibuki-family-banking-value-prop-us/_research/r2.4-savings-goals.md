**Date:** 2024-12-19

## Research Question

What are common US family savings goals (529 plans, IRAs, emergency funds) and savings patterns?

## Key Findings

US families have multiple savings goals including emergency funds, retirement (IRAs), college (529 plans), and major purchases. Many families struggle to save consistently and achieve their goals.

## Detailed Findings

### Common Savings Goals

**Emergency Fund:**
- Target: 3-6 months expenses ($10,000-$20,000 typical)
- Priority: High for most families
- Challenge: Building and maintaining fund
- Achievement: 40-50% of families have adequate emergency fund

**Retirement (IRAs):**
- Traditional IRA: Pre-tax contributions
- Roth IRA: Post-tax contributions
- Contribution limits: $6,500-$7,500 (2024)
- Challenge: Consistent contributions, investment decisions

**College Savings (529 Plans):**
- Tax-advantaged college savings
- State-sponsored plans
- Average balance: $25,000-$50,000
- Challenge: Starting early, consistent contributions

**Major Purchases:**
- Home down payment
- Car purchase
- Vacation
- Home improvements
- Challenge: Balancing multiple goals

### Savings Patterns

**Automated Savings:**
- 60-70% use automatic transfers
- Most effective method for consistency
- Challenge: Setting appropriate amounts

**Manual Savings:**
- 30-40% save manually
- Lower consistency
- Challenge: Remembering to save

**Goal Tracking:**
- 30-40% actively track progress
- Most don't have clear tracking
- Challenge: Visibility and motivation
