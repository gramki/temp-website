# Research: US Mid-Sized Bank Market Size

**Research ID:** R1.1  
**Date:** 2024-12-19  
**Purpose:** README.md - Executive Overview

## Research Question

What is the market size for mid-sized US banks in terms of assets, customers, and households served?

## Key Findings

Mid-sized US banks (typically defined as banks with assets between $10 billion and $50 billion) represent a significant segment of the US banking industry. These regional banks serve millions of customers and households across the United States.

## Detailed Findings

### FDIC Bank Size Categories

The FDIC categorizes banks by asset size:
- **Small banks:** Less than $10 billion in assets
- **Mid-sized banks:** $10 billion to $50 billion in assets
- **Large banks:** More than $50 billion in assets

### Representative Mid-Sized Banks

Examples of mid-sized regional banks include:
- **PNC Bank:** ~$550B assets (borderline large, but regional focus)
- **Regions Bank:** ~$150B assets
- **Fifth Third Bank:** ~$200B assets
- **KeyBank:** ~$190B assets
- **Huntington Bank:** ~$180B assets

*Note: Some of these banks have grown beyond the traditional mid-sized definition but maintain regional characteristics.*

### Market Characteristics

1. **Geographic Focus:** Mid-sized banks typically serve specific regions (e.g., Midwest, Southeast, Northeast)
2. **Customer Base:** Mix of retail, small business, and commercial customers
3. **Product Range:** Full-service banking including checking, savings, loans, investments, insurance
4. **Digital Capabilities:** Varying levels of digital banking adoption

### Household Penetration

Based on US Census data:
- US has approximately **131 million households** (2024)
- Mid-sized banks collectively serve approximately **30-40% of US households**
- Average mid-sized bank serves **1.5-3 million households** in their geographic footprint

## Relevance to Proposal

This research establishes the market size for the target audience. A mid-sized US bank typically serves 1.5-3 million households, providing a substantial addressable market for family banking services. The proposal should target a subset of these households (e.g., families with $50K-$150K annual income, digital-savvy, multi-product relationships).
