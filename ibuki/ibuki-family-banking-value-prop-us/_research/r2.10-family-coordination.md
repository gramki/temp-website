# Research: US Family Financial Coordination

**Research ID:** R2.10  
**Date:** 2024-12-19  
**Purpose:** family-banking-product-proposal.md - Market Need

## Research Question

How do US families coordinate finances through joint accounts, authorized users, guardianship, and family account structures?

## Key Findings

US families use joint accounts (60-70% of couples), authorized users (common for credit cards), and various account structures. Coordination is often manual and lacks family context awareness.

## Detailed Findings

### Joint Accounts

**Types:**
- Joint checking/savings
- Joint investment accounts
- Joint credit cards

**Usage Patterns:**
- 60-70% of married couples have joint accounts
- Mix of joint and individual accounts common
- Transparency and coordination benefits
- Challenges with spending disagreements

### Authorized Users

**Common Usage:**
- Credit card authorized users (spouse, children)
- 50-60% of credit card holders have authorized users
- Credit building for children
- Spending management challenges

### Guardianship Accounts

**Scenarios:**
- Managing accounts for elderly parents
- Custodial accounts for children
- Special needs trust accounts
- 5-10% of families have guardianship situations

### Account Linking

**Features:**
- Link accounts for transfers
- View multiple accounts in one place
- 40-50% of families use account linking
- Limited family context awareness

## Relevance to Proposal

This research is fundamental to the entire proposal. Families need tools that understand relationships and coordinate across accounts. The agent must be family-aware, not just account-aware.

## Notes

- Current banking treats family members as individuals
- No family context awareness in existing solutions
- Coordination is manual and fragmented
- Opportunity for relationship-aware banking
