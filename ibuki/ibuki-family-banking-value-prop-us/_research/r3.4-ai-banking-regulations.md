# Research: US AI Banking Regulations

**Research ID:** R3.4  
**Date:** 2024-12-19  
**Purpose:** solution-vision.md - Why Agents, Not Apps

## Research Question

What are the regulatory considerations for AI in banking (explainability, bias, fairness)?

## Key Findings

US banking AI must comply with fair lending laws, provide explainability, avoid bias, and ensure fairness. CFPB and other regulators are increasing focus on AI in financial services.

## Data Points

| Requirement | Key Aspect | Impact |
|-------------|------------|--------|
| Fair Lending | Non-discrimination | AI must not discriminate |
| Explainability | Understandable decisions | AI decisions must be explainable |
| Bias Testing | Regular testing required | AI models must be tested for bias |
| Fairness | Equal treatment | AI must treat similar customers similarly |
| Transparency | Clear disclosures | Customers must understand AI usage |

## Detailed Findings

### Fair Lending Requirements

**Key Laws:**
- Equal Credit Opportunity Act (ECOA)
- Fair Housing Act
- CFPB fair lending guidance

**AI Implications:**
- AI models must not discriminate
- Protected classes must be treated fairly
- Regular bias testing required

### Explainability

**Requirements:**
- AI decisions must be explainable
- Customers have right to understand decisions
- Model transparency requirements

**Challenges:**
- Complex models are hard to explain
- Balance between accuracy and explainability
- Need for human oversight

### Bias and Fairness

**Testing Requirements:**
- Regular bias testing
- Fairness metrics
- Disparate impact analysis

**Monitoring:**
- Ongoing monitoring required
- Regular audits
- Remediation when bias found

The agent architecture must incorporate compliance requirements: explainability, bias testing, fairness monitoring. This is critical for regulatory approval and customer trust.
