## Research Question

What are US family investment scenarios including 401k, IRAs, brokerage accounts, and college savings patterns?

## Key Findings

US families invest through 401k plans (employer-sponsored), IRAs (individual), brokerage accounts, and 529 plans (college). Average 401k balance is $100,000-$150,000. Many families lack investment strategy and coordination.

## Detailed Findings

### 401k Plans

**Characteristics:**
- Employer-sponsored retirement plans
- Pre-tax contributions (traditional) or post-tax (Roth 401k)
- Employer matching common (3-6% of salary)
- Contribution limits: $23,000 (2024)
- Average participation: 60-70% of eligible employees

**Family Challenges:**
- Coordinating 401k contributions across spouses
- Understanding investment options
- Rebalancing and optimization
- Rollover decisions when changing jobs

### IRAs

**Types:**
- Traditional IRA: Pre-tax contributions
- Roth IRA: Post-tax contributions
- Contribution limits: $6,500-$7,500 (2024, age-dependent)

**Usage Patterns:**
- 50-60% of families have IRAs
- Average balance: $100,000-$130,000
- Often used to supplement 401k
- Spousal IRAs for non-working spouses

### Brokerage Accounts

**Characteristics:**
- Taxable investment accounts
- 30-40% of families have brokerage accounts
- Used for non-retirement investing
- More flexibility than retirement accounts

**Investment Types:**
- Stocks
- Mutual funds
- ETFs
- Bonds

### College Savings (529 Plans)

**Characteristics:**
- Tax-advantaged college savings
- State-sponsored plans
- 30-40% of families have 529 plans
- Average balance: $25,000-$50,000
