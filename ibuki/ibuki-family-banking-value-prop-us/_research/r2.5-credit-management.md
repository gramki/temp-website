# Research: US Credit Management

**Research ID:** R2.5  
**Date:** 2024-12-19  
**Purpose:** family-banking-product-proposal.md - Market Need

## Research Question

What are US family credit management patterns including credit scores, debt consolidation, and credit card usage?

## Key Findings

US families manage multiple credit products (credit cards, loans) with average credit scores around 715. Credit card debt averages $5,000-$8,000 per household. Many families struggle with debt management and credit optimization.

## Detailed Findings

### Credit Score Management

**Score Ranges:**
- Excellent: 750+
- Good: 700-749
- Fair: 650-699
- Poor: Below 650

**Factors Affecting Score:**
- Payment history (35%)
- Credit utilization (30%)
- Length of credit history (15%)
- Credit mix (10%)
- New credit (10%)

**Family Challenges:**
- Coordinating credit usage across family members
- Authorized users affecting credit scores
- Joint accounts and credit impact
- Credit monitoring and alerts

### Credit Card Usage

**Usage Patterns:**
- 80-85% of households have credit cards
- Average 3-4 cards per household
- Mix of rewards cards, balance transfer cards, store cards
- Average utilization: 30-35%

**Payment Patterns:**
- 60-70% pay in full monthly
- 30-40% carry balances
- Average interest rate: 20-25% APR

### Debt Management

**Common Debt Types:**
- Credit card debt: $5,000-$8,000 average
- Auto loans: $20,000-$30,000 average
- Student loans: $30,000-$50,000 average
- Personal loans: $10,000-$20,000 average

**Debt Consolidation:**
- 10-15% use debt consolidation
- Balance transfer cards
- Personal loans for consolidation
- Home equity loans (HELOCs)
