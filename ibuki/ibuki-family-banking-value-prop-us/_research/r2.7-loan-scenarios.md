## Research Question

What are US family loan scenarios including mortgages, auto loans, student loans, HELOCs, and refinancing patterns?

## Key Findings

US families commonly have mortgages ($200,000-$300,000 average), auto loans ($20,000-$30,000), and student loans ($30,000-$50,000). Refinancing decisions are complex and often suboptimal.

## Detailed Findings

### Mortgages

**Characteristics:**
- Most common family loan
- Average balance: $200,000-$300,000
- 30-year fixed most common
- Average rate: 6.5-7.5% (2024)

**Family Scenarios:**
- First-time homebuyers
- Refinancing decisions
- Home equity extraction (HELOCs)
- Mortgage payoff vs. investment decisions

### Auto Loans

**Characteristics:**
- 60-70% of families have auto loans
- Average: $20,000-$30,000
- Terms: 3-7 years
- Average rate: 5-8%

**Family Scenarios:**
- Multiple vehicles per family
- New vs. used decisions
- Refinancing opportunities
- Lease vs. buy decisions

### Student Loans

**Characteristics:**
- 40-50% of families have student loans
- Average: $30,000-$50,000
- Federal and private loans
- Income-driven repayment options

**Family Scenarios:**
- Parent PLUS loans
- Children's student loans
- Refinancing decisions
- Payoff strategies

### HELOCs (Home Equity Lines of Credit)

**Characteristics:**
- 10-15% of homeowners use HELOCs
- Access to home equity
- Variable rates
- Used for home improvements, debt consolidation
