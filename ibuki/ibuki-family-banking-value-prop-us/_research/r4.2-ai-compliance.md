# Research: US AI Compliance Requirements

**Research ID:** R4.2  
**Date:** 2024-12-19  
**Purpose:** agents-and-their-roles.md - Architecture

## Research Question

What are the explainability, bias testing, and fairness requirements for AI agents in US banking?

## Key Findings

US banking AI must provide explainability, undergo regular bias testing, ensure fairness, and maintain transparency. CFPB and other regulators are increasing oversight of AI in financial services.

## Data Points

| Requirement | Standard | Frequency |
|-------------|----------|-----------|
| Bias Testing | Regular testing | Quarterly/Annually |
| Fairness Metrics | Disparate impact <80% | Ongoing |
| Explainability | Understandable decisions | Per decision |
| Transparency | Clear disclosures | Ongoing |
| Human Oversight | Review of AI decisions | Ongoing |

## Detailed Findings

### Explainability

**Requirements:**
- AI decisions must be explainable
- Customers have right to understand
- Model transparency
- Human-readable explanations

**Implementation:**
- Explainable AI techniques
- Decision documentation
- Customer-facing explanations
- Regulatory reporting

### Bias Testing

**Testing Requirements:**
- Regular bias testing (quarterly/annual)
- Protected class analysis
- Disparate impact testing
- Fairness metrics

**Metrics:**
- Demographic parity
- Equalized odds
- Calibration
- Individual fairness

### Fairness

**Fair Lending:**
- Equal Credit Opportunity Act compliance
- No discrimination by protected class
- Similar customers treated similarly
- Regular audits

**Monitoring:**
- Ongoing monitoring
- Alert systems
- Remediation processes
- Documentation

## Relevance to Proposal

The agent architecture must incorporate compliance: explainability, bias testing, fairness monitoring. This is built into the architecture, not added later.

## Notes

- Compliance is architectural, not afterthought
- Regular testing and monitoring required
- Explainability is critical for trust
- Human oversight remains important
