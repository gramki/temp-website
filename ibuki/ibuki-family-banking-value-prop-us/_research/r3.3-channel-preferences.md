# Research: US Customer Channel Preferences

**Research ID:** R3.3  
**Date:** 2024-12-19  
**Purpose:** solution-vision.md - Why Agents, Not Apps

## Research Question

What are US customer preferences for banking channels (app vs. voice vs. chat, SMS vs. email)?

## Key Findings

US customers prefer multi-channel banking with mobile apps primary for transactions, SMS for notifications, email for documentation, and chat/voice for support. Context should persist across channels.

## Data Points

| Channel | Primary Use | Preference % | Source |
|---------|------------|--------------|--------|
| Mobile App | Transactions | 80-85% | Federal Reserve |
| SMS | Notifications | 70-75% | Industry surveys |
| Email | Documentation | 90-95% | Industry standard |
| Voice | Quick queries | 20-30% | Industry surveys |
| Chat | Support | 40-50% | Industry surveys |
| Multi-channel | Expected | 85% | Consumer surveys |

## Detailed Findings

### Channel Preferences by Task

**Transactions:**
- Mobile app: 80-85% preference
- Voice: 10-15% preference
- Other: 5-10%

**Notifications:**
- SMS: 70-75% preference
- Email: 20-25% preference
- Push: 5-10% preference

**Support:**
- Chat: 40-50% preference
- Phone: 30-40% preference
- Email: 10-20% preference

**Information:**
- Mobile app: 60-70% preference
- Email: 20-30% preference
- Voice: 10-15% preference

### Multi-Channel Expectations

**Key Requirements:**
- 85% expect to use multiple channels
- 70% expect seamless experience across channels
- 60% expect context to persist
- 50% expect proactive communications

## Relevance to Proposal

The agent architecture must support multiple channels (SMS, email, voice, app, chat) with consistent experience and context persistence. This is a key differentiator from app-only solutions.

## Notes

- Multi-channel support is essential, not optional
- Context persistence across channels is critical
- Channel preferences vary by task and demographic
- Agents enable seamless multi-channel experience
