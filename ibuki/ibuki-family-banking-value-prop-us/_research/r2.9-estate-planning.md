# Research: US Estate Planning

**Research ID:** R2.9  
**Date:** 2024-12-19  
**Purpose:** family-banking-product-proposal.md - Market Need

## Research Question

What are US family estate planning patterns including wills, trusts, beneficiaries, and estate planning?

## Key Findings

Only 30-40% of US families have wills. Estate planning is often neglected despite being critical for family financial security. Beneficiary designations are often outdated.

## Detailed Findings

### Wills

**Characteristics:**
- Legal document for asset distribution
- Only 30-40% of families have wills
- Often outdated or incomplete
- Need regular updates

**Family Challenges:**
- Procrastination
- Complexity
- Cost concerns
- Updating when life changes

### Trusts

**Types:**
- Revocable living trusts
- Irrevocable trusts
- Testamentary trusts

**Usage:**
- 10-15% of families have trusts
- More common for higher net worth
- Tax and probate avoidance

### Beneficiary Designations

**Accounts Requiring Beneficiaries:**
- Retirement accounts (401k, IRAs)
- Life insurance
- Bank accounts (POD/TOD)
- Investment accounts

**Common Issues:**
- Outdated beneficiaries (40-50%)
- Missing beneficiaries
- Incorrect designations
- Family coordination
