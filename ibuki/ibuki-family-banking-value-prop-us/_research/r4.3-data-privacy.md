# Research: US Data Privacy Requirements

**Research ID:** R4.3  
**Date:** 2024-12-19  
**Purpose:** agents-and-their-roles.md - Architecture

## Research Question

What are state-specific privacy laws (CCPA, etc.) and data sharing regulations for US banking?

## Key Findings

US banking must comply with federal privacy laws (GLBA) and state-specific laws (CCPA in California, others). Family data sharing requires careful handling of privacy and consent.

## Data Points

| Law | Scope | Key Requirements |
|------|-------|------------------|
| GLBA | Federal | Financial privacy, opt-out rights |
| CCPA | California | Consumer privacy rights, data access |
| Other State Laws | Various states | Similar to CCPA |
| HIPAA | Health data | If health information included |

## Detailed Findings

### Federal Privacy (GLBA)

**Gramm-Leach-Bliley Act:**
- Financial privacy requirements
- Information sharing restrictions
- Opt-out rights
- Annual privacy notices

**Family Banking Implications:**
- Family data sharing must comply
- Consent requirements
- Opt-out mechanisms
- Privacy notices

### State Privacy Laws

**California (CCPA):**
- Consumer privacy rights
- Data access rights
- Deletion rights
- Opt-out of sale

**Other States:**
- Similar laws in other states
- Varying requirements
- Multi-state compliance needed

### Family Data Sharing

**Consent Requirements:**
- Explicit consent for data sharing
- Family member permissions
- Minor protections
- Elder care considerations

**Privacy Controls:**
- Granular privacy settings
- Who can see what
- Opt-in/opt-out mechanisms
- Audit trails
