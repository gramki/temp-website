# Research: US Financial Opportunity Costs

**Research ID:** R5.11  
**Date:** 2024-12-19  
**Purpose:** value-to-customers.md - Customer Value

## Research Question

What are US opportunity costs from idle cash and suboptimal investments?

## Key Findings

US families often keep $10,000-$50,000 in low-yield savings (0.01-0.5% APY) when they could earn 4-5% in high-yield savings or investments. Opportunity cost: $400-$2,000 annually.

## Data Points

| Scenario | Idle Amount | Current Yield | Better Yield | Opportunity Cost |
|----------|-------------|---------------|--------------|------------------|
| Low-yield savings | $20,000 | 0.5% | 4.5% | $800/year |
| Idle cash | $10,000 | 0% | 4% | $400/year |
| Suboptimal investments | $30,000 | 3% | 7% | $1,200/year |
| **Total** | | | | **$400-$2,000/year** |

## Detailed Findings

### Idle Cash

**Common Pattern:**
- $10,000-$50,000 in low-yield savings
- Earning 0.01-0.5% APY
- Could earn 4-5% in high-yield savings
- Opportunity cost: $400-$2,000 annually

### Suboptimal Investments

**Common Issues:**
- Too conservative (low returns)
- Too aggressive (high risk)
- Poor allocation
- Opportunity cost: $500-$1,500 annually

### Agent Optimization

**Recommendations:**
- Move idle cash to high-yield
- Optimize investment allocation
- Balance risk and return
- Saves: $400-$2,000 annually
