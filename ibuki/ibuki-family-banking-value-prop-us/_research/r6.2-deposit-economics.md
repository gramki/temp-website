## Research Question

What are CASA ratios, cost of funds, and net interest margin for mid-sized US banks?

## Key Findings

Mid-sized US banks have CASA ratios of 30-50%, cost of funds of 2-4%, and net interest margin of 2.5-3.5%. Deposit growth and CASA improvement drive profitability.

## Detailed Findings

### CASA (Current Account Savings Account) Ratio

**Definition:**
- Percentage of deposits in low-cost accounts
- Higher CASA = lower cost of funds
- Target: 40-50%

**Current State:**
- Mid-sized banks: 30-50%
- Opportunity to improve through family banking

### Cost of Funds

**Components:**
- Interest on deposits: 2-4%
- Operational costs
- Target: Lower is better

**Impact:**
- Lower cost = higher margin
- CASA improvement reduces cost

### Net Interest Margin

**Calculation:**
- Interest income - Interest expense
- Typical: 2.5-3.5%
- Key profitability metric
