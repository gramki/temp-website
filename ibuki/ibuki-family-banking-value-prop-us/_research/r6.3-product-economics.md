# Research: US Product Economics

**Research ID:** R6.3  
**Date:** 2024-12-19  
**Purpose:** value-to-bank.md - Business Case

## Research Question

What are credit card, loan, and investment product margins and revenue per product for US banks?

## Key Findings

US banks generate significant revenue from products: credit cards ($300-$800/year), loans ($1,000-$4,000/year), investments ($200-$500/year). Margins vary by product type.

## Data Points

| Product | Annual Revenue | Margin | Source |
|---------|---------------|--------|--------|
| Credit Card | $300-$800 | 15-25% | Industry data |
| Personal Loan | $500-$2,000 | 3-5% | Industry data |
| Mortgage | $2,000-$4,000 | 1-2% | Industry data |
| Investment | $200-$500 | 0.5-1% | Industry data |
| Insurance | $100-$300 | 10-20% | Industry data |

## Detailed Findings

### Credit Cards

**Revenue Sources:**
- Interchange fees: $200-$500/year
- Interest: $100-$300/year
- Annual fees: $0-$100/year
- Total: $300-$800/year

**Margins:**
- 15-25% margin
- High profitability

### Loans

**Personal Loans:**
- Interest spread: 3-5%
- Average balance: $15,000-$40,000
- Annual revenue: $500-$2,000

**Mortgages:**
- Interest spread: 1-2%
- Average balance: $200,000-$300,000
- Annual revenue: $2,000-$4,000

### Investments

**Revenue:**
- Asset management fees: 0.5-1%
- Average assets: $20,000-$50,000
- Annual revenue: $200-$500
