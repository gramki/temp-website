**Date:** 2024-12-19  
**Purpose:** family-banking-product-proposal.md - Market Need

## Research Question

What are the US tax filing requirements, deductions, credits, and deadlines that families need to manage?

## Key Findings

US families face complex tax scenarios with federal, state, and local taxes. Key documents include W-2s, 1099s, and deduction receipts. Tax filing deadline is April 15. Many families struggle with organization and miss deductions.

## Detailed Findings

### Tax Documents Required

**Income Documents:**
- W-2 (wage income)
- 1099-MISC (contractor income)
- 1099-INT (interest income)
- 1099-DIV (dividend income)
- 1099-B (brokerage transactions)

**Deduction Documents:**
- Mortgage interest statements (Form 1098)
- Property tax receipts
- Charitable contribution receipts
- Medical expense receipts
- Education expense receipts (1098-T)
- Child care receipts

**Tax Credits:**
- Earned Income Tax Credit (EITC)
- Child Tax Credit
- Education credits (American Opportunity, Lifetime Learning)
- Child and Dependent Care Credit

### Common Tax Scenarios

**Married Filing Jointly:**
- Most common for married couples
- Combined income, shared deductions
- Coordination needed for document collection

**Head of Household:**
- Single parents
- Different tax brackets and deductions

**Dependent Management:**
- Claiming children as dependents
- Coordination when children have income
- Education-related tax benefits

### Tax Preparation Challenges

**Document Organization:**
- Collecting documents from multiple sources
- Organizing receipts and statements
- Tracking deadlines
- Missing documents

**Deduction Optimization:**
- Understanding eligible deductions
- Maximizing tax credits
- Timing of deductions (itemized vs. standard)

**Family Coordination:**
- Coordinating tax documents across family members
- Ensuring all income is reported
- Avoiding double-claiming of dependents
