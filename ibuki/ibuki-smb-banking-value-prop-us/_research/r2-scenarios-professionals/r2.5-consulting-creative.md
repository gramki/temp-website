# Research: Consulting & Creative Practice Scenarios

**Research ID:** R2.5  
**Date:** 2025-01-15  
**Purpose:** Scenario inventory - Consulting and Creative verticals

## Research Question

What are the financial scenarios and needs specific to consulting and creative practices (consultants, designers, developers, writers)?

## Key Findings

Consulting and creative practices have **25-30 distinct financial scenarios** across 14 journey types, spanning:
- Daily operations and cash flow management
- Multi-project cash flow coordination
- Project-based billing and payments
- Retainer vs. project billing decisions
- Variable income smoothing
- Travel and expense management
- Owner compensation and business-personal separation
- Tax and compliance
- Financing and credit management
- Practice growth and investment decisions
- Crisis management
- Practice lifecycle transitions
- Banking operations and treasury management

**Key insight:** 15 of 25-30 scenarios have **High or Very High expectation** from banks. These are core banking responsibilities where mid-sized banks must excel to win and retain consulting/creative practice relationships. Poor experience in these areas drives practices to fintech alternatives.

## Journey Type 1: Daily Cash Flow Operations

### 1.1 Managing Cash Flow Across Multiple Projects

**User Story:** As a consultant, I need to manage cash flow across multiple client projects, each with different billing schedules and payment terms.

**Key Needs:**
- Track projects by client
- Monitor project billing status (invoiced, paid, overdue)
- Understand cash flow across all projects
- Identify cash flow gaps
- Plan for project completion and payment
- Project revenue projections
- Client payment history tracking

**Emotional Drivers:**
- Cash flow uncertainty
- Multi-client coordination
- Need for visibility
- Stress from project timing mismatches

**Frequency:** Weekly to monthly  
**Impact:** High (affects practice viability)

**Current Bank Coverage:** None - Banks don't provide multi-project cash flow management

**Current Alternatives & Solutions:**

1. **Project Management Software (Asana, Monday, Basecamp)**
   - Pros:
     - Project tracking
     - Client management
     - Some billing integration
   - Cons:
     - Limited financial analysis
     - Doesn't integrate with banking
     - May not track payments
   - Cost: $10-$30/user/month
   - Effectiveness: Medium (good for projects, limited for finance)

2. **Accounting Software (QuickBooks, Xero)**
   - Pros:
     - Financial tracking
     - Client invoicing
     - Cash flow statements
   - Cons:
     - Manual project setup
     - Limited project-specific views
     - Not real-time
   - Cost: $30-$200/month
   - Effectiveness: Medium-High (good for accounting, limited for project cash flow)

3. **Freelance Management Platforms (FreshBooks, HoneyBook)**
   - Pros:
     - Project and client management
     - Invoicing and payments
     - Some cash flow features
   - Cons:
     - Additional cost
     - May not integrate with banking
     - Limited for larger practices
   - Cost: $15-$50/month
   - Effectiveness: Medium-High (good for freelancers, limited for agencies)

4. **Manual Tracking (Spreadsheet)**
   - Pros:
     - Full control
     - Free
     - Can customize
   - Cons:
     - Time-consuming
     - Error-prone
     - No automation
   - Cost: $0
   - Effectiveness: Low (inefficient)

**Business Owner Insights:**
- **Would they expect a bank solution?** **Moderate** - Some see as natural extension of cash management, others as outside bank's core competency
- **Would they benefit?** Yes - Integration with banking data would provide unified view; real-time project cash flow would improve decision-making
- **What would make them switch?**
  - Seamless integration with existing project management tools
  - Lower cost than current solutions
  - Better financial analysis
  - Real-time data
  - Multi-project cash flow visibility
- **Trust factors:** Banks are trusted for financial data security
- **Convenience factors:** Single dashboard for projects and cash flow; automated updates; proactive alerts

### 1.2 Cash Position Visibility

**User Story:** As a consultant, I need to know my current cash position across all accounts so I can make decisions about expenses and owner draws, especially given variable project income.

**Key Needs:**
- Real-time view of all business accounts (checking, savings, money market)
- Integration with personal accounts (for owner-operators)
- Cash flow projection (expected project payments, upcoming expenses)
- Alerts for low cash balance
- Variable income tracking

**Emotional Drivers:**
- Need for control and visibility
- Fear of cash shortfall (especially with variable income)
- Desire for proactive financial management

**Frequency:** Daily  
**Impact:** High (affects all financial decisions)

**Current Bank Coverage:** Partial - Banks provide account balances but not integrated view or projections

**Current Alternatives & Solutions:**

1. **Bank Online Banking (Multiple Logins)**
   - Pros:
     - Free (included with accounts)
     - Real-time balances
     - Secure and reliable
   - Cons:
     - Separate logins for each account
     - No aggregated view
     - No cash flow projections
     - No integration with project management
   - Cost: $0
   - Effectiveness: Low (fragmented view)

2. **Accounting Software (QuickBooks, Xero)**
   - Pros:
     - Business-focused
     - Can connect to bank accounts
     - Cash flow statements and projections
   - Cons:
     - Requires manual categorization
     - Not real-time (updates daily or manually)
     - Limited integration with personal accounts
     - Doesn't show receivables in real-time
   - Cost: $30-$200/month
   - Effectiveness: Medium-High (good for historical, limited for real-time)

3. **Personal Finance Apps (Mint, YNAB)**
   - Pros:
     - Aggregates multiple accounts
     - Free or low cost
     - Budgeting features
   - Cons:
     - Designed for personal use, not business
     - Limited business-specific features
     - Security concerns
   - Cost: $0-$15/month
   - Effectiveness: Medium (not business-focused)

4. **Fintech Business Banking (Mercury, Relay, Novo)**
   - Pros:
     - Modern interface
     - Multiple account views
     - Some cash flow features
   - Cons:
     - Requires moving banking relationship
     - May lack full-service capabilities
   - Cost: Free to $30/month
   - Effectiveness: Medium-High (better UX, limited integration)

**Business Owner Insights:**
- **Would they expect a bank solution?** **High** - This is seen as core banking functionality; banks should provide unified account view
- **Would they benefit?** Yes - Single dashboard would save time; integration with project receivables would provide complete picture
- **What would make them switch?**
  - Seamless aggregation of all accounts (business + personal)
  - Real-time updates
  - Integration with project receivables
  - Proactive alerts and recommendations
  - Better than current bank online banking
- **Trust factors:** High trust in banks for account aggregation
- **Convenience factors:** Single login; real-time updates; mobile app access; automated categorization
