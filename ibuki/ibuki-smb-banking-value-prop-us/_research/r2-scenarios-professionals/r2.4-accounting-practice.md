# Research: Accounting Practice Scenarios

**Research ID:** R2.4  
**Date:** 2025-01-15  
**Purpose:** Scenario inventory - Accounting vertical

## Research Question

What are the financial scenarios and needs specific to accounting practices (CPA firms, solo accountants)?

## Key Findings

Accounting practices have **25-30 distinct financial scenarios** across 14 journey types, spanning:
- Daily operations and cash flow management
- Tax season cash flow management (highly seasonal - 60-70% of revenue in Q1)
- Client receivables management
- Owner compensation and business-personal separation
- Tax and compliance
- Financing and credit management
- Practice growth and investment decisions
- Crisis management
- Practice lifecycle transitions
- Banking operations and treasury management

**Key insight:** 15 of 25-30 scenarios have **High or Very High expectation** from banks. These are core banking responsibilities where mid-sized banks must excel to win and retain accounting practice relationships. Poor experience in these areas drives practices to fintech alternatives.

## Journey Type 1: Daily Cash Flow Operations

### 1.1 Tax Season Cash Flow Management

**User Story:** As a CPA, I need to manage cash flow across tax season (Jan-April) when 60-70% of my annual revenue comes in, ensuring I can cover expenses year-round.

**Key Needs:**
- Understand tax season revenue spike (Jan-April)
- Plan for slow months (May-December)
- Set aside funds for year-round expenses
- Smooth cash flow across the year
- Owner draw planning (when to take draws)
- Project tax season revenue based on client base
- Budget for slow months

**Emotional Drivers:**
- Cash flow stress during slow months
- Need for financial stability
- Balancing current needs with future security
- Anxiety about slow months

**Frequency:** Monthly (with seasonal planning)  
**Impact:** Very High (affects practice viability)

**Current Bank Coverage:** None - Banks don't provide seasonal cash flow planning

**Current Alternatives & Solutions:**

1. **Accounting Software (QuickBooks, Xero)**
   - Pros:
     - Can track revenue by month
     - Cash flow statements
     - Historical data
   - Cons:
     - Doesn't provide seasonal planning
     - Manual analysis required
     - No automated recommendations
   - Cost: $30-$200/month
   - Effectiveness: Medium (shows data but no planning)

2. **CPA/Financial Advisor Consultation**
   - Pros:
     - Expert seasonal planning
     - Comprehensive analysis
     - Tax optimization
   - Cons:
     - Expensive ($150-$300/hour)
     - Not real-time
     - May be conservative
   - Cost: $150-$300/hour
   - Effectiveness: High (but expensive and delayed)

3. **Manual Planning (Spreadsheet)**
   - Pros:
     - Full control
     - Free
     - Can customize
   - Cons:
     - Time-consuming
     - Error-prone
     - No integration with actual data
   - Cost: $0
   - Effectiveness: Low-Medium (depends on diligence)

4. **No Systematic Planning**
   - Pros:
     - No cost
   - Cons:
     - High risk of cash flow problems
     - Stressful
     - Poor financial management
   - Cost: High risk
   - Effectiveness: Low (very risky)

**Business Owner Insights:**
- **Would they expect a bank solution?** **Moderate** - Some see as valuable service, others as outside core banking
- **Would they benefit?** Very High - Automated seasonal planning, real income data, automated savings, lower cost than advisor
- **What would make them switch?**
  - Automated seasonal cash flow planning
  - Real-time revenue tracking
  - Automated savings during tax season
  - Slow month budgeting
  - Lower cost than CPA consultation
- **Trust factors:** Moderate - Banks trusted for financial data, but seasonal planning seen as advisory
- **Convenience factors:** Automated; integrated with banking; real-time; reduces stress

### 1.2 Cash Position Visibility

**User Story:** As a CPA, I need to know my current cash position across all accounts so I can make decisions about expenses and owner draws, especially during slow months.

**Key Needs:**
- Real-time view of all practice accounts (checking, savings, money market)
- Integration with personal accounts (for owner-operators)
- Cash flow projection (expected receivables, upcoming expenses)
- Alerts for low cash balance
- Seasonal cash position tracking

**Emotional Drivers:**
- Need for control and visibility
- Fear of cash shortfall (especially in slow months)
- Desire for proactive financial management

**Frequency:** Daily  
**Impact:** High (affects all financial decisions)

**Current Bank Coverage:** Partial - Banks provide account balances but not integrated view or projections

**Current Alternatives & Solutions:**

1. **Bank Online Banking (Multiple Logins)**
   - Pros:
     - Free (included with accounts)
     - Real-time balances
     - Secure and reliable
   - Cons:
     - Separate logins for each account
     - No aggregated view
     - No cash flow projections
     - No integration with receivables
   - Cost: $0
   - Effectiveness: Low (fragmented view)

2. **Accounting Software (QuickBooks, Xero)**
   - Pros:
     - Business-focused
     - Can connect to bank accounts
     - Cash flow statements and projections
   - Cons:
     - Requires manual categorization
     - Not real-time (updates daily or manually)
     - Limited integration with personal accounts
     - Doesn't show receivables in real-time
   - Cost: $30-$200/month
   - Effectiveness: Medium-High (good for historical, limited for real-time)

3. **Personal Finance Apps (Mint, YNAB)**
   - Pros:
     - Aggregates multiple accounts
     - Free or low cost
     - Budgeting features
   - Cons:
     - Designed for personal use, not business
     - Limited business-specific features
     - Security concerns
   - Cost: $0-$15/month
   - Effectiveness: Medium (not business-focused)

4. **Fintech Business Banking (Mercury, Relay, Novo)**
   - Pros:
     - Modern interface
     - Multiple account views
     - Some cash flow features
   - Cons:
     - Requires moving banking relationship
     - May lack full-service capabilities
   - Cost: Free to $30/month
   - Effectiveness: Medium-High (better UX, limited integration)

**Business Owner Insights:**
- **Would they expect a bank solution?** **High** - This is seen as core banking functionality; banks should provide unified account view
- **Would they benefit?** Yes - Single dashboard would save time; integration with receivables would provide complete picture
- **What would make them switch?**
  - Seamless aggregation of all accounts (business + personal)
  - Real-time updates
  - Integration with receivables from practice management
  - Proactive alerts and recommendations
  - Better than current bank online banking
- **Trust factors:** High trust in banks for account aggregation
- **Convenience factors:** Single login; real-time updates; mobile app access; automated categorization

**User Story:** As a CPA, I need to access bank statements, transaction history, and documentation for tax preparation, audits, loan applications, and general record-keeping.

- Access current and historical statements

- Search transaction history
- Get specific documentation (verification letters, account letters)
- Access tax forms (1099-INT, etc.)
- Provide documentation to clients, auditors, lenders
- Integration with accounting software

**Emotional Drivers:**
- Stress from audit or tax deadlines
- Need for organized records
- Frustration finding old documents
- Professional requirements

**Frequency:** Monthly for statements, as-needed for special docs  
**Impact:** High (required for compliance and external requirements)

**Current Bank Coverage:** Yes - Banks provide statements but access to historical/special docs varies

**Current Alternatives & Solutions:**

1. **Bank Online Statement Portal**
   - Pros:
     - Authoritative source
     - Usually 7 years history
     - PDF download
   - Cons:
     - Limited export formats
     - Special docs may require branch visit
     - Search may be limited
   - Cost: Free (paper statements may have fee)
   - Effectiveness: Medium-High (for statements, less for special docs)

   - Pros:
     - Can get any documentation needed
     - Official bank letters
   - Cons:
     - Time-consuming
     - May take days
     - Branch visit sometimes required
   - Cost: May have fees ($10-$50 per document)
   - Effectiveness: Medium (reliable but inconvenient)

3. **Accounting Software Archive**
   - Pros:
     - Integrated with accounting
     - Searchable
     - Always available
   - Cons:
     - Not authoritative (bank feeds can have issues)

     - Requires setup
   - Cost: Included with software
   - Effectiveness: Medium (for internal use, less for external)

  - Easy access to full history
  - Multiple export formats (PDF, CSV, QBO, OFX)
  - Self-service for common documentation
  - Fast turnaround on special requests
  - Integration with accounting/tax software
- **Trust factors:** Very High - Bank statements are authoritative
- **Convenience factors:** Self-service; online access; multiple formats; fast turnaround

- Tax deadline stress (CPAs understand this well)

- Professional requirements

**Frequency:** Annually (January-April)  
**Impact:** High (affects tax liability and compliance)

**Current Bank Coverage:** Partial - Banks provide 1099s but comprehensive tax support limited

**Current Alternatives & Solutions:**

   - Pros:
     - All bank 1099s in one place
     - Usually available by February
     - Download capability
   - Cons:
     - Only bank-related forms
     - No aggregation across institutions
     - Limited analysis
   - Cost: Free
   - Effectiveness: Medium-High (for bank forms)

2. **Tax Software Integration**
   - Pros:
     - Direct import
     - Comprehensive
     - Professional tools
   - Cons:
     - May not have all documents
     - Requires manual collection
   - Cost: Included in tax software
   - Effectiveness: Medium-High (good but may miss items)

     - Easy to miss documents
