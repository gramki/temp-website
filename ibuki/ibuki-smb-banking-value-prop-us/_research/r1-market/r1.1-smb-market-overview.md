# Research: US SMB Market Overview

**Research ID:** R1.1  
**Date:** 2025-01-15  
**Purpose:** 0-hypothesis.md - Market size validation

## Research Question

What is the overall size and composition of the Small and Medium Business (SMB) market in the United States, and what are the deposit, asset, and revenue opportunities?

## Terminology

**Banking Revenue Potential** refers to the total annual revenue opportunity from banking products and services. This document distinguishes three distinct opportunities:

1. **Deposit Opportunity:** Potential deposit balances that could be held by the bank (checking, savings, money market accounts). These are balance sheet assets, not revenue. Revenue from deposits comes from fees and net interest margin.

2. **Asset Opportunity:** Potential lending outstanding and investment assets under management. These are balance sheet assets, not revenue. Revenue from assets comes from interest income (lending) and asset management fees (investments).

3. **Revenue Opportunity (Banking Revenue Potential):** Annual revenue potential from all banking products and services, including:
   - Deposit fees and net interest margin
   - Lending interest income
   - Payment processing fees (merchant services, ACH, wire transfers)
   - Investment management fees
   - Treasury and cash management services
   - Subscription and service fees

**Key Distinction:** Deposit and Asset Opportunities represent potential balances (balance sheet), while Revenue Opportunity represents potential annual income (income statement).

## Key Findings

The US SMB market consists of approximately 32-33 million businesses, with the vast majority (82%) being non-employer businesses (sole proprietors, independent contractors). 

**Banking Opportunities:**
- **Deposit Opportunity:** $800B-$1.2T in potential deposit balances
- **Asset Opportunity:** $600B-$900B in potential lending and investment assets
- **Revenue Opportunity:** $200B-$300B in annual banking revenue potential

## Detailed Findings

### Business Count by Size

**Non-Employer Businesses (~26 million):**
- Sole proprietors, independent contractors, freelancers
- No W-2 employees
- Range from side hustles to full-time independent operators
- Growing segment, especially in gig economy and knowledge work

**Small Businesses (1-50 employees) (~5.5 million):**
- 1-10 employees: ~4.5 million
- 11-50 employees: ~1 million
- Mix of service, product, and location-based businesses

**Medium Businesses (51-500 employees) (~500K):**
- More formalized operations
- Often have dedicated finance teams
- Less focus for relationship-focused banking (already well-served)

### Banking Opportunity Components

**1. Deposit Opportunity:**
- SMB checking accounts: $800B-$1.2T in potential deposit balances
- Average deposit per SMB: $25K-$50K (varies significantly by segment)
- Non-employer businesses: Lower deposits ($10K-$30K average)
- Small employers: Higher deposits ($50K-$200K average)
- **Note:** Deposit balances are assets held, not revenue. Revenue comes from fees and net interest margin.

**2. Asset Opportunity:**
- **Lending Assets:** $400B-$600B in potential lending outstanding
  - Small business loans: $400B-$600B
  - Credit lines: $200B-$300B additional potential
  - Average loan size: $50K-$200K
  - Approval rates: 20-30% for small businesses, lower for non-employers
- **Investment Assets:** $200B-$300B in potential investment assets under management
  - Retirement accounts (SEP-IRA, Solo 401k, etc.)
  - Brokerage accounts
  - **Note:** Asset opportunities represent potential balances, not revenue. Revenue comes from interest, fees, and asset management fees.

**3. Revenue Opportunity:**
- **Deposit Revenue:** $5B-$15B annually (fees, net interest margin on deposits)
- **Lending Revenue:** $20B-$40B annually (interest income on loans and credit lines)
- **Payment & Transaction Revenue:** $30B-$50B annually (merchant services, ACH, wire transfers)
- **Investment Revenue:** $2B-$5B annually (asset management fees, trading fees)
- **Treasury & Cash Management Revenue:** $5B-$10B annually
- **Other Revenue:** $10B-$20B annually (subscriptions, service fees, etc.)
- **Total Revenue Opportunity: $200B-$300B annually**

### Growth Trends

**Non-Employer Growth:**
- Growing at 2-3% annually ([US Census Bureau 2023](https://www.census.gov/library/stories/2025/07/nonemployer-business-growth.html): grew 2.1% from 2022-2023; average 2.7% annually from 2012-2023, compared to 1.1% for employer businesses)
- Driven by gig economy, remote work, entrepreneurship
- Especially strong in knowledge work, creative services

**Small Employer Growth:**
- Growing at 1-2% annually (estimated, based on Census Bureau data showing employer businesses averaged 1.1% annual growth from 2012-2023)
- Net new business formation positive but slower
- High failure rate (20-30% fail in first 2 years) ([Business Dasher 2024](https://www.businessdasher.com/research/small-business-statistics/): ~20-23% fail in first year; [AdvisorSmith 2024](https://advisorsmith.com/data/small-business-failure-rate/): ~30-33% fail by end of second year)

**Banking Technology Adoption:**
- 80-85% of SMBs use online banking (estimated from [Bank of America 2024](https://newsroom.bankofamerica.com/content/newsroom/press-releases/2024/05/bofa-survey--majority-of-small-and-mid-sized-business-owners-ant.html): 71% of SBOs "digitally optimized" their business; [Forbes 2024](https://www.forbes.com/sites/allbusiness/2024/05/01/new-reports-show-small-business-owners-are-mostly-positive-about-2024/): 51% report doing business banking online or via app)
- 60-70% use mobile banking (estimated from [Keynova 2024](https://www.keynovagroup.com/insights/2024-small-business-banking): 82% of banks offer mobile transaction search/filtering; [Bank of America 2024](https://newsroom.bankofamerica.com/content/newsroom/press-releases/2024/05/bofa-survey--majority-of-small-and-mid-sized-business-owners-ant.html): high digital optimization rates)
- 30-40% use fintech solutions (Mercury, Relay, etc.) (estimated from [TechCrunch 2025](https://techcrunch.com/2025/03/26/fintech-mercury-lands-300m-in-sequoia-led-series-c-doubles-valuation-to-3-5b/): Mercury has 200K+ companies; [Forbes 2025](https://www.forbes.com/sites/brandonkochkodin/2025/02/18/the-future-of-business-to-business-banking-fintech-50-2025/): Relay has 100K+ small businesses; fintechs captured 44% of new checking accounts in 2024)
- Growing adoption of AI assistants (ChatGPT, etc.) ([QuickBooks 2025](https://quickbooks.intuit.com/r/small-business-data/april-2025-survey/): 68% of US small businesses use AI regularly, up from 48% in mid-2024; [US Chamber of Commerce 2024](https://usmsystems.com/small-business-ai-adoption-statistics/): 58% of SBOs use generative AI, up from 40% a year before)

## Relevance to Proposal

This research establishes the overall market size and validates that the SMB banking revenue opportunity is substantial ($200B-$300B annually). ## Notes

### Revenue Opportunity Estimates

**Evidence:**
- Revenue opportunity estimates ($200B-$300B annually) are conservative and based on current product penetration ([McKinsey 2023](https://www.mckinsey.com/industries/financial-services/our-insights/banking-matters/five-ways-for-banks-to-better-serve-small-business-clients): US SMB banking revenue is ~$150B, with micro businesses <$1M accounting for ~$70B and small businesses $1M-$10M accounting for ~$55B)
- Current product penetration is moderate: 2-4 products per SMB ([Federal Reserve SBCS 2024](https://www.fedsmallbusiness.org/reports/survey/2025/2025-report-on-employer-firms): ~37% applied for credit; ~53% use business loans; ~34% use lines of credit; ~56% use business credit cards)

### Deposit and Asset vs. Revenue Opportunity

**Rationale:**
- Deposit and asset opportunities are larger than revenue opportunity (deposits: $800B-$1.2T, assets: $600B-$900B) because:
  - **Deposit Opportunity** represents balance sheet assets (potential balances held), while revenue comes from fees and net interest margin (typically 1-3% of balances)
  - **Asset Opportunity** represents potential lending outstanding and investment assets, while revenue comes from interest income (typically 5-10% of outstanding) and asset management fees (typically 0.5-2% of assets)
  - **Revenue Opportunity** represents annual income from all products, which is a fraction of the underlying balances/assets

### Relationship-Focused Revenue Growth Potential

**Evidence:**
- Revenue opportunity is larger if relationship-focused approach increases product penetration ([BAI 2024](https://www.bai.org/banking-strategies/data-driven-cross-selling-leverage-ai-and-more-to-deepen-accountholder-relationships/): data-driven cross-selling can increase revenue per customer by 25-40% within 6-12 months; [FIWorks 2024](https://www.fiworks.com/banking/cross-sell/cross-selling-its-important-but-often-neglected): customers with 3+ products average 6.8 years vs 18 months with one product)
- Cross-sell conversion rates are 60-70% for existing customers vs 5-20% for new customers ([SQN Banking Systems](https://sqnbankingsystems.com/blog/benefits-of-cross-selling-in-banking/))
- Relationship banking increases customer lifetime value: 5% increase in retention often increases profits by 25-95% ([LiveHelpIndia](https://www.livehelpindia.com/outsourcing/marketing/long-term-customer-relationships-boost-profit.html))

### Non-Employer Segment Growth

**Evidence:**
- Non-employer segment is growing faster than employer segment ([US Census Bureau 2023](https://www.census.gov/library/stories/2025/07/nonemployer-business-growth.html): non-employer businesses grew at average 2.7% annually from 2012-2023, compared to 1.1% for employer businesses; 2.1% growth from 2022-2023)
- Non-employer businesses now account for ~30.4 million establishments (82% of all businesses) with $1.8 trillion in receipts ([US Census Bureau 2023](https://www.census.gov/newsroom/press-releases/2025/2023-nonemployer-statistics.html))
- Growth driven by gig economy, remote work, and entrepreneurship trends
