# Research: Professionals Banking Revenue Potential

**Research ID:** R1.4  
**Date:** 2025-01-15  
**Purpose:** 0-hypothesis.md - H1.4 Value validation

## Research Question

What are the deposit opportunity, asset opportunity, and revenue opportunity per professional practice, and what products do professionals currently use?

## Key Findings

Professional practices represent significant banking opportunities:
- **Deposit Opportunity:** $175B-$675B in potential deposit balances
- **Asset Opportunity:** $87.5B-$450B in potential lending and investment assets
- **Revenue Opportunity:** $50B-$70B in annual banking revenue potential

Current product penetration is low (2-3 products per practice), with significant opportunity to increase to 5-8 products through relationship-focused banking.

## Detailed Findings

### Deposit Products

**Business Checking:**
- Average balance: $30K-$80K (solo), $100K-$300K (firms)
- Monthly fees: $10-$50 (often waived with minimum balance)
- Transaction volume: 50-200 transactions/month
- **Revenue per account: $200-$600/year**

**Business Savings:**
- Average balance: $20K-$70K (solo), $50K-$200K (firms)
- Interest income: Minimal (low rates)
- **Revenue per account: $50-$200/year**

**Money Market / Treasury:**
- Average balance: $50K-$150K (firms only, typically)
- Higher interest, sweep accounts
- **Revenue per account: $200-$500/year**

**Total Deposit Revenue: $300-$1,300/year per practice**

### Lending Products

**Business Credit Lines:**
- Average outstanding: $25K-$100K
- Interest rate: 6-12% (varies by credit)
- Utilization: 30-60% average
- **Revenue per line: $500-$2,000/year**

**Business Term Loans:**
- Average outstanding: $50K-$200K
- Interest rate: 5-10%
- **Revenue per loan: $2,500-$10,000/year (amortized)**

**Equipment Financing:**
- Average outstanding: $25K-$150K
- Interest rate: 6-12%
- **Revenue per loan: $1,500-$9,000/year (amortized)**

**Personal Guarantees (Business Credit):**
- Many professionals use personal credit for business
- Opportunity to consolidate with business credit

**Total Lending Revenue: $500-$2,000/year per practice (average)**

### Payment & Transaction Products

**Merchant Services:**
- Transaction volume: $50K-$500K annually
- Processing fees: 2-3% of volume
- **Revenue per practice: $1,000-$15,000/year**

**ACH/Wire Transfers:**
- Transaction volume: $100K-$1M annually
- Fees: $0.25-$5 per transaction
- **Revenue per practice: $200-$1,000/year**

**Payment Cards:**
- Business credit cards: $10K-$50K annual spend
- Interchange revenue: 1-2% of spend
- **Revenue per practice: $100-$1,000/year**

**Total Payment Revenue: $1,300-$17,000/year per practice**

### Investment & Retirement Products

**SEP-IRA / Solo 401k:**
- Average contribution: $10K-$50K annually
- Assets under management: $100K-$500K
- Management fees: 0.5-1% of AUM
- **Revenue per practice: $500-$5,000/year**

**Brokerage Accounts:**
- Average balance: $50K-$200K
- Trading fees, management fees
- **Revenue per practice: $200-$1,000/year**

**Total Investment Revenue: $700-$6,000/year per practice**

### Current vs. Potential Product Penetration

**Current State (2-3 products per practice):**
- Business checking: 80-90% penetration
- Business savings: 40-50% penetration
- Business credit line: 30-40% penetration
- Merchant services: 20-30% penetration
- Investment products: 10-20% penetration

**Potential State (5-8 products per practice):**
- Business checking: 95%+ (already high)
- Business savings: 80%+ (increase)
- Business credit line: 60%+ (increase)
- Merchant services: 50%+ (increase)
- Investment products: 50%+ (significant increase)
- Treasury/cash management: 30%+ (new)
- Business credit card: 40%+ (increase)
- Personal banking integration: 70%+ (new)

### Revenue Model

**Current Revenue per Practice:**
- Deposits: $300-$1,300/year
- Lending: $500-$2,000/year
- Payments: $1,300-$17,000/year (highly variable)
- Investments: $700-$6,000/year
- **Total: $500-$1,500/year (low end) to $2,800-$26,000/year (high end)**
- **Average: $500-$1,500/year**

**Potential Revenue per Practice (Relationship-Focused):**
- Deposits: $500-$2,000/year (higher balances, more products)
- Lending: $1,000-$3,000/year (higher penetration, utilization)
- Payments: $2,000-$20,000/year (higher volume, optimization)
- Investments: $1,500-$8,000/year (higher penetration)
- Subscription/service fees: $600-$1,800/year (new revenue stream)
- **Total: $3,000-$10,000/year (conservative) to $5,600-$34,800/year (high end)**
- **Average: $3,000-$10,000/year**

### Banking Opportunity Calculation

**Per Practice:**
- **Deposit Opportunity:** $50K-$150K average balance
- **Asset Opportunity:** 
  - Lending: $25K-$100K average outstanding
  - Investment assets: $100K-$500K (for those with investments)
- Transaction volume: $100K-$1M annually

**Total Market (3.5-4.5M practices):**

**1. Deposit Opportunity:**
- Total deposits: $175B-$675B
- **Note:** Deposit balances are assets held by the bank, not revenue. Revenue comes from fees and net interest margin.

**2. Asset Opportunity:**
- Total lending outstanding: $87.5B-$450B
- Total investment assets: $350B-$2.25T (for practices with investments)
- **Total Asset Opportunity: $437.5B-$2.7T**
- **Note:** Asset opportunities represent potential balances. Revenue comes from interest income and asset management fees.

**3. Revenue Opportunity:**
- Total transaction volume: $350B-$4.5T annually
- **Total Revenue Opportunity: $50B-$70B annually**
- **Note:** Revenue opportunity represents annual revenue potential from all banking products and services (deposit fees, interest income, lending revenue, payment processing, investment management, etc.)

## Relevance to Proposal

This research validates H1.4: Professionals represent a $3,000-$10,000 annual revenue opportunity per relationship (vs. $500-$1,500 today). The revenue opportunity is substantial ($50B-$70B annually), with even larger deposit opportunity ($175B-$675B) and asset opportunity ($437.5B-$2.7T). There's significant opportunity to increase product penetration from 2-3 to 5-8 products per practice.

## Notes

- Revenue estimates are conservative and based on current product penetration
- Relationship-focused approach can increase penetration significantly
- High-end practices (firms, high-revenue solo) have much higher potential value
- Subscription/service fees represent new revenue stream not captured today
- Deposit consolidation opportunity is significant (many professionals split across multiple banks)
