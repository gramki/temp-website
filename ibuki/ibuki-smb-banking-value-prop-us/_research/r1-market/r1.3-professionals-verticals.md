# Research: Professionals - Top 5 Verticals Deep Dive

**Research ID:** R1.3  
**Date:** 2025-01-15  
**Purpose:** 0-hypothesis.md, scenario research prioritization

## Research Question

What are the characteristics of the top 5 professional verticals (Healthcare, Legal, Accounting, Consulting, Creative) that inform banking needs and scenario research?

## Key Findings

The top 5 professional verticals represent ~80% of the professional market and have distinct financial characteristics:
1. **Healthcare** - Highest revenue, insurance billing complexity, equipment-intensive
2. **Legal** - High revenue, contingency vs. hourly billing, trust accounts
3. **Accounting** - Steady revenue, tax seasonality, client receivables
4. **Consulting** - Project-based revenue, retainer models, travel expenses
5. **Creative** - Variable revenue, project-based, lower barriers to entry

## Vertical 1: Healthcare

### Market Size
- **Solo:** ~750K-980K (physicians, dentists, therapists, other)
- **Firms:** ~300K-425K practices
- **Total:** ~1-1.4 million practices

### Financial Characteristics
- **Average Revenue:** $200K-$800K (solo), $1M-$5M (firms)
- **Billing Model:** Insurance-based (Medicare, Medicaid, private), fee-for-service
- **Key Expenses:** Equipment (medical devices, dental chairs), supplies, malpractice insurance
- **Cash Flow Pattern:** Receivables-driven, insurance payment delays (30-90 days)
- **Seasonality:** Moderate (healthcare is steady, but some seasonality in elective procedures)

### Banking Needs
- **Receivables Management:** Insurance claims, patient billing, collections
- **Equipment Financing:** Medical devices, dental equipment, office equipment
- **Cash Flow:** Managing insurance payment delays
- **Business-Personal:** Owner draws, retirement planning (SEP-IRA, Solo 401k)
- **Compliance:** HIPAA considerations, practice structure (LLC, S-Corp)

### Key Scenarios
- "How do I handle slow-paying insurance companies?"
- "Should I lease or buy this $50K medical device?"
- "When can I take an owner draw given receivables?"
- "How do I plan for retirement with practice income?"

## Vertical 2: Legal

### Market Size
- **Solo:** ~400K-500K attorneys
- **Firms:** ~100K-150K firms (2-50 attorneys)
- **Total:** ~500K-650K practices

### Financial Characteristics
- **Average Revenue:** $150K-$500K (solo), $500K-$10M (firms)
- **Billing Model:** Hourly billing, contingency fees, flat fees, retainers
- **Key Expenses:** Office space, legal research tools, malpractice insurance, staff
- **Cash Flow Pattern:** Receivables-driven, contingency creates lumpy income
- **Seasonality:** Moderate (litigation has cycles, transactional work is steady)

### Banking Needs
- **Trust Accounts:** IOLTA (Interest on Lawyers' Trust Accounts) management
- **Receivables Management:** Client billing, collections, contingency payouts
- **Cash Flow:** Managing contingency fee timing, retainer drawdowns
- **Business-Personal:** Owner draws, partnership distributions
- **Compliance:** Trust account regulations, practice structure

### Key Scenarios
- "How do I manage trust account balances?"
- "When will this contingency case settle and pay out?"
- "Should I take a retainer or bill hourly?"
- "How do I handle partner distributions?"

## Vertical 3: Accounting

### Market Size
- **Solo:** ~200K-250K CPAs
- **Firms:** ~50K-75K firms (2-50 CPAs)
- **Total:** ~250K-325K practices

### Financial Characteristics
- **Average Revenue:** $100K-$300K (solo), $500K-$5M (firms)
- **Billing Model:** Hourly billing, monthly retainers, project-based
- **Key Expenses:** Software (QuickBooks, tax software), office space, staff
- **Cash Flow Pattern:** Highly seasonal (tax season: Jan-April), steady rest of year
- **Seasonality:** Very high (60-70% of revenue in Q1)

### Banking Needs
- **Seasonal Cash Flow:** Managing tax season revenue spike
- **Receivables Management:** Client billing, collections
- **Business-Personal:** Owner draws, retirement planning
- **Tax Planning:** SEP-IRA, Solo 401k, S-Corp elections
- **Compliance:** Practice structure, professional liability

### Key Scenarios
- "How do I smooth cash flow across tax season?"
- "Should I set aside money for slow months?"
- "When can I take owner draws outside tax season?"
- "How do I plan for retirement with seasonal income?"

## Vertical 4: Consulting

### Market Size
- **Solo:** ~450K-650K consultants
- **Firms:** ~50K-75K consulting firms
- **Total:** ~500K-725K practices

### Financial Characteristics
- **Average Revenue:** $75K-$250K (solo), $500K-$5M (firms)
- **Billing Model:** Project-based, retainer, hourly, value-based
- **Key Expenses:** Travel, software, marketing, subcontractors
- **Cash Flow Pattern:** Project-based (lumpy), retainer-based (steady)
- **Seasonality:** Moderate (some industries have cycles)

### Banking Needs
- **Project Cash Flow:** Managing project milestones, payments
- **Expense Management:** Travel, subcontractor payments
- **Receivables Management:** Client billing, collections
- **Business-Personal:** Owner draws, retirement planning
- **Multi-Client Coordination:** Managing multiple project cash flows

### Key Scenarios
- "How do I manage cash flow across multiple projects?"
- "When can I take an owner draw given project timing?"
- "How do I handle travel expenses and reimbursements?"
- "Should I take a retainer or bill per project?"

## Vertical 5: Creative

### Market Size
- **Solo:** ~550K-800K creative professionals
- **Firms:** ~50K-75K agencies
- **Total:** ~600K-875K practices

### Financial Characteristics
- **Average Revenue:** $50K-$200K (solo), $500K-$3M (firms)
- **Billing Model:** Project-based, hourly, retainer, royalties
- **Key Expenses:** Software, equipment (computers, cameras), marketing
- **Cash Flow Pattern:** Highly variable, project-based
- **Seasonality:** Moderate (some industries have cycles, e.g., holiday campaigns)

### Banking Needs
- **Project Cash Flow:** Managing project payments, milestones
- **Expense Management:** Equipment, software subscriptions
- **Receivables Management:** Client billing, collections
- **Business-Personal:** Owner draws, retirement planning
- **Income Smoothing:** Managing variable income

### Key Scenarios
- "How do I manage cash flow with variable project income?"
- "When can I afford to buy new equipment?"
- "How do I handle slow-paying clients?"
- "Should I take a retainer or bill per project?"

## Cross-Vertical Patterns

### Common Banking Needs Across All Verticals
1. **Receivables Management** - Client/patient billing, collections
2. **Owner Compensation** - When/how much to pay yourself
3. **Business-Personal Integration** - Tax planning, retirement, personal guarantees
4. **Cash Flow Management** - Timing of income and expenses
5. **Tax & Compliance** - Quarterly estimates, year-end planning
6. **Practice Growth** - Hiring, equipment, expansion decisions

### Vertical-Specific Needs
- **Healthcare:** Insurance billing, equipment financing
- **Legal:** Trust accounts, contingency fee management
- **Accounting:** Tax season cash flow, client receivables
- **Consulting:** Multi-project coordination, travel expenses
- **Creative:** Variable income smoothing, equipment purchases
