# Research: Primary Research Design - Professionals

**Research ID:** R6.1  
**Date:** 2025-01-15  
**Purpose:** Primary research methodology to validate and refine professional scenario inventory

## Research Objectives

1. **Validate Scenario Inventory:** Confirm 80-90% of scenarios identified in secondary research
2. **Quantify Pain Severity:** Understand which scenarios cause most pain
3. **Identify Unmet Needs:** Discover scenarios not found in secondary research
4. **Validate Frequency & Impact:** Quantify how often scenarios occur and their impact
5. **Understand Current Solutions:** Learn what professionals do today to address scenarios

## Methodology

### Method 1: In-Depth Interviews

**Sample:** 20-25 professionals

**Distribution:**
- Healthcare: 4-5 (physicians, dentists, therapists)
- Legal: 4-5 (solo attorneys, small firm partners)
- Accounting: 4-5 (solo CPAs, small firm owners)
- Consulting: 3-4 (management consultants, IT consultants)
- Creative: 3-4 (designers, developers, writers)

**Practice Size:**
- Solo: 8-10
- 1-5 employees: 8-10
- 5-10 employees: 4-5

**Geography:**
- Urban: 12-15
- Suburban: 8-10

**Banking Relationship:**
- Single bank: 8-10
- Multi-bank: 8-10
- Fintech users: 4-5

**Duration:** 60-90 minutes per interview

---

### Method 2: Day-in-Life Observation

**Sample:** 5-8 professionals

**Purpose:** Observe actual financial workflows and pain points

**Activities:**
- Shadow professional for 2-4 hours
- Observe financial tasks (checking accounts, paying bills, managing receivables)
- Note workarounds and friction points
- Document time spent on financial tasks

---

### Method 3: Expert Interviews

**Sample:** 5-8 experts

**Types:**
- CPAs who serve professionals (2-3)
- Bookkeepers who serve professionals (2-3)
- Practice management consultants (1-2)

**Purpose:** Fill knowledge gaps, understand common issues

**Duration:** 45-60 minutes per interview

---

### Method 4: Validation Survey

**Sample:** 150-200 professionals

**Purpose:** Quantify frequency and impact of scenarios

**Distribution:** Similar to interviews (by vertical, size, geography)

**Method:** Online survey

**Duration:** 15-20 minutes

---

## Interview Protocol

### Screener Questions

1. What type of professional practice do you run? (Healthcare, Legal, Accounting, Consulting, Creative)
2. How many employees does your practice have? (Solo, 1-5, 5-10, 10+)
3. What's your annual practice revenue? ($50K-$150K, $150K-$300K, $300K-$500K, $500K+)
4. Where is your practice located? (Urban, Suburban, Rural)
5. Which banks do you use for business banking? (List)
6. Do you use any fintech solutions? (Mercury, Relay, Novo, etc.)

**Criteria:**
- Must be owner-operator or partner
- Must have been in practice for 1+ years
- Mix of solo and small practices (1-10 employees)
- Mix of banking relationships

---

### Discussion Guide

**Section 1: Practice Overview (10 minutes)**
- Tell me about your practice
- How long have you been in practice?
- What's your revenue and employee count?
- What are your main sources of revenue?

**Section 2: Current Banking Relationship (15 minutes)**
- What banks do you use for business banking?
- What products do you have?
- What do you like/dislike about your current banking?
- Do you use personal banking at the same bank?

**Section 3: Financial Workflows (30 minutes)**
- Walk me through how you manage money in your practice
- How do you track cash flow?
- How do you decide when to pay yourself?
- How do you handle client billing and collections?
- How do you manage expenses?
- How do you handle taxes?

**Section 4: Pain Points (20 minutes)**
- What's the most stressful financial moment in your month/quarter/year?
- When was the last time you wished your bank could help with something but couldn't?
- What financial tasks take the most time?
- What financial decisions are hardest to make?

**Section 5: Scenario Validation (15 minutes)**
- Review key scenarios from inventory
- Which of these do you experience?
- How often?
- How painful/stressful?
- What do you do today to address these?

**Section 6: Unmet Needs (10 minutes)**
- What financial needs does no one address?
- What would make your financial life easier?
- What would you pay for?

---

### Journey Mapping Exercise

**Activity:** Map a typical month/quarter in the professional's financial life

**Elements:**
- Key financial events (paycheck, client payments, owner draws, tax payments)
- Decisions made (when to draw, when to pay vendors, when to hire)
- Tools used (bank, QuickBooks, spreadsheets, CPA)
- Pain points and friction
- Time spent on financial tasks

---

### Pain Severity Rating

**Scale:** 1-10 (1 = no pain, 10 = extremely painful)

**For each scenario:**
- How painful/stressful is this scenario?
- How much time does it take?
- What's the financial impact if not handled well?

## Analysis Plan

### Scenario Validation

**Criteria:**
- Scenario exists if confirmed by >70% of interviewees
- Frequency validated through survey (median, distribution)
- Impact validated through survey (pain severity, time cost, financial impact)

**Output:**
- Validated scenario list
- Frequency distribution
- Impact scores
- Priority ranking

---

### Pain Point Clustering

**Analysis:**
- Group similar pain points
- Identify common themes
- Quantify pain severity
- Identify most critical pain points

**Output:**
- Pain point clusters
- Priority pain points
- Common themes

---

### Journey Type Refinement

**Analysis:**
- Validate journey type framework
- Identify missing journey types
- Refine journey type definitions
- Reorganize scenarios if needed

**Output:**
- Refined journey type framework
- Scenario-to-journey mapping

---

### Gap Severity Quantification

**Analysis:**
- For each scenario, quantify:
  - Current solution quality (1-10)
  - Pain if not addressed (1-10)
  - Time cost of current workarounds
  - Financial impact of gaps

**Output:**
- Gap severity scores
- Priority gaps
- Opportunity sizing

---

## Deliverables

1. **Validated Scenario Inventory:** Confirmed scenarios with frequency and impact
2. **Pain Point Analysis:** Clustered pain points with severity scores
3. **Journey Type Framework:** Refined framework based on research
4. **Gap Analysis:** Quantified gaps with severity scores
5. **Unmet Needs:** New scenarios identified in research
