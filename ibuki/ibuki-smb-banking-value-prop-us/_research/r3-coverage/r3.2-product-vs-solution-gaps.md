# Research: Product vs. Solution Gaps

**Research ID:** R3.2  
**Date:** 2025-01-15  
**Purpose:** Gap analysis - Products offered vs. scenarios solved

## Research Question

What's the gap between products banks offer and solutions professionals need?

## Key Finding

Banks offer **products** (checking, credit, merchant services) but professionals need **solutions** (scenarios solved). This is the fundamental gap that relationship-focused banking addresses.

## Gap Pattern Analysis

### Pattern 1: Product Exists, Solution Missing

**Example: Business Checking Account**

**What Banks Offer:**
- Account to hold money
- Transactions (deposits, withdrawals, transfers)
- Online/mobile access
- Basic reporting

**What Professionals Need:**
- "Can I take an owner draw this month?" (needs cash flow analysis)
- "When will my receivables pay?" (needs receivables management)
- "What's my cash position across all accounts?" (needs integration)

**Gap:** Bank provides the account, but not the financial decision support.

---

### Pattern 2: Multiple Products, No Coordination

**Example: Business Credit Line**

**What Banks Offer:**
- Credit line product
- Interest rate
- Draw and payback capability

**What Professionals Need:**
- "Should I use my credit line now or wait?" (needs cash flow projection)
- "How much interest am I paying?" (needs cost analysis)
- "When should I pay it down?" (needs optimization)

**Gap:** Bank provides credit, but not strategic credit management.

---

### Pattern 3: Product Doesn't Address Scenario

**Example: Receivables Management**

**What Banks Offer:**
- Payment processing
- ACH transfers
- Basic invoicing (some banks)

**What Professionals Need:**
- "Which clients haven't paid?" (needs receivables aging)
- "When will insurance pay?" (needs insurance claim tracking)
- "How do I collect from slow payers?" (needs collections workflow)

**Gap:** Bank doesn't provide receivables management at all.

---

### Pattern 4: Generic Product, Specific Need

**Example: Tax Planning**

**What Banks Offer:**
- Payment processing (for estimated tax payments)
- Basic savings accounts

**What Professionals Need:**
- "How much should I set aside for quarterly taxes?" (needs tax calculation)
- "What expenses are deductible?" (needs expense categorization)
- "How do I optimize my SEP-IRA?" (needs retirement planning integration)

**Gap:** Bank provides payment capability, but not tax planning support.

---

## Scenario-by-Scenario Gap Analysis

| Scenario | Bank Product | Gap Type | Gap Severity |
|----------|--------------|----------|--------------|
| When can I take owner draw? | Checking account | Solution missing | Very High |
| Real-time cash position | Account balances | Integration missing | High |
| Receivables management | Payment processing | Product doesn't address | Very High |
| Slow-paying clients | Basic invoicing | Solution missing | Very High |
| Quarterly estimated taxes | Payment processing | Solution missing | High |
| Business-personal integration | Separate accounts | Integration missing | Very High |
| Line of credit management | Credit line | Solution missing | High |
| Invoice creation | Payment processing | Partial solution | Medium-High |

**Summary:**
- Very High gap: 4-5 scenarios
- High gap: 3-4 scenarios
- Medium-High gap: 1 scenario

---

## Root Cause Analysis

**Why the Gap Exists:**

1. **Product-Centric Model:** Banks are organized around products, not customer needs
2. **Siloed Products:** Each product is managed separately, no coordination
3. **Generic Solutions:** Products designed for all SMBs, not segment-specific
4. **Reactive Model:** Banks wait for customers to come to them, not proactive
5. **No Integration:** Business and personal banking are separate businesses
6. **Limited Intelligence:** No AI/automation to provide proactive insights

---

## Opportunity

**Relationship-Focused Banking Addresses:**
- Scenario-solving instead of product-selling
- Integration across products and accounts
- Proactive insights and recommendations
- Segment-specific solutions
- Business-personal coordination
