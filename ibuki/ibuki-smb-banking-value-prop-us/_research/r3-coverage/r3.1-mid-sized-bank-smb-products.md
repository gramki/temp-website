# Research: Mid-Sized Bank SMB Product Offerings

**Research ID:** R3.1  
**Date:** 2025-01-15  
**Purpose:** Coverage analysis - What banks offer today

## Research Question

What SMB banking products do mid-sized US banks currently offer?

## Key Findings

Mid-sized banks offer standard business banking products (checking, savings, credit, merchant services) but lack:
- Segment-specific solutions
- Business-personal integration
- Scenario-solving capabilities
- Proactive financial management

## Core Products Offered

### Business Checking Accounts

**Standard Offerings:**
- Basic business checking: $10-$25/month (waived with minimum balance, typically $1,500-$5,000)
- Premium business checking: $25-$50/month (includes more transactions, waived with higher balance)
- Analysis checking: Fee based on activity (per transaction, per deposit)

**Features:**
- Online banking
- Mobile banking
- Bill pay
- ACH transfers
- Wire transfers
- Check writing

**Limitations:**
- No business-personal integration
- No cash flow projections
- No receivables management
- Generic, not professional-specific

---

### Business Savings Accounts

**Standard Offerings:**
- Business savings: Low interest (0.01-0.10% APY), minimal fees
- Money market accounts: Higher interest (0.10-0.50% APY), higher minimums ($5K-$25K)

**Features:**
- Interest earning
- Limited transactions (6 per month typically)
- Online access

**Limitations:**
- Low interest rates
- No cash flow optimization
- No integration with checking

---

### Business Credit Products

**Business Credit Lines:**
- Amount: $25K-$500K
- Interest rate: 6-12% (varies by credit)
- Terms: Revolving, typically 1-3 year terms
- Requirements: Personal guarantee, business financials, credit check

**Business Term Loans:**
- Amount: $50K-$1M
- Interest rate: 5-10%
- Terms: 1-10 years
- Requirements: Business plan, financials, collateral often required

**SBA Loans:**
- Government-backed
- Lower rates (4-8%)
- Longer terms
- More paperwork

**Limitations:**
- Generic underwriting (doesn't account for professional-specific patterns)
- No variable income consideration (for gig workers)
- No integrated decision support

---

### Merchant Services

**Payment Processing:**
- Credit/debit card processing: 2-3% of transaction volume
- Point-of-sale systems: Integrated or standalone
- Online payment processing
- Mobile payment acceptance

**Features:**
- Transaction processing
- Settlement (next day typically)
- Reporting

**Limitations:**
- Rates not optimized
- No cash flow integration
- Generic, not industry-specific

---

### Payroll Services

**Payroll Processing:**
- Employee payroll: $20-$100/month per employee
- Direct deposit
- Tax filing (payroll taxes)
- Year-end forms (W-2s)

**Limitations:**
- Basic processing only
- No tip management
- No seasonal staffing support
- Not integrated with cash flow

---

### Cash Management

**Services:**
- ACH transfers: $0.25-$5 per transaction
- Wire transfers: $15-$50 per transaction
- Sweep accounts: Automatic transfer between accounts
- Zero balance accounts: For larger businesses

**Limitations:**
- Primarily for larger businesses
- Not integrated with cash flow planning
- No proactive management

---

## Segment-Specific Offerings

### Professionals

**Limited Offerings:**
- Some banks offer "professional banking" packages
- Typically includes: Higher transaction limits, specialized support, sometimes lower fees
- **Gap:** No business-personal integration, no scenario-solving, no practice-specific features

### Gig Workers

**Very Limited:**
- Treated as retail customers
- No variable income consideration
- No platform integration
- **Gap:** Complete lack of gig-specific features

### Local Operators

**Moderate:**
- Merchant services (core product)
- Some industry-specific loans (restaurant, retail)
- **Gap:** No cash flow timing support, no location-specific services

---

## Integration Gaps

**Business-Personal:**
- Business and personal accounts are separate
- No integrated view
- Personal guarantees required but personal financials not visible
- No coordinated tax planning

**Multi-Product:**
- Products sold individually
- No relationship pricing
- No cross-product optimization

**Scenario-Solving:**
- Banks provide products, not solutions
- No proactive cash flow management
- No decision support
- No integrated financial planning

---

## Digital Capabilities

**Online Banking:**
- Account management: 80-90% adoption
- Bill pay: 60-70% adoption
- Basic reporting

**Mobile Banking:**
- Account access: 60-70% adoption
- Mobile deposits
- Basic transactions

**Limitations:**
- No AI/automation
- No proactive insights
- No scenario-solving
- Limited integration with other tools
