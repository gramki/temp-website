# Research: Fintech Competitive Landscape - Professionals

**Research ID:** R4.1  
**Date:** 2025-01-15  
**Purpose:** Competitive analysis - Fintechs targeting professionals

## Research Question

What do fintechs offer for professionals, and what are the gaps?

## Key Findings

Fintechs (Mercury, Relay, Novo, Found, Collective) focus on startups and tech companies, not traditional professionals. They offer modern UX and some integrations but lack:
- Business-personal integration
- Scenario-solving (still product-focused)
- Professional-specific features
- Full-service banking (lending, investments)

## Competitive Analysis

### Mercury

**Target:** Startups, tech companies, VC-backed businesses

**Products:**
- Business checking (FDIC-insured)
- Debit cards
- Wire transfers
- API access
- Integrations (QuickBooks, etc.)

**Strengths:**
- Modern UX
- Fast account setup
- Good integrations
- API-first approach

**Gaps:**
- No business-personal integration
- No scenario-solving
- Limited to tech/startup segment
- No lending (partner only)
- No professional-specific features

## Competitive Positioning

**Fintech Strengths:**
- Modern UX
- Fast setup
- Good integrations
- Lower fees (some)

**Fintech Gaps:**
- No business-personal integration
- No scenario-solving (still product-focused)
- Limited to specific segments (startups, freelancers)
- No full-service banking (lending, investments)
- No professional-specific features

**Opportunity:**
- Traditional professionals (doctors, lawyers, CPAs) are underserved
- Business-personal integration is missing
- Scenario-solving is missing
- Full-service banking advantage (banks have lending, investments)

## Relevance to Proposal

Fintechs haven't won the professional segment. Opportunity exists for banks to combine full-service banking with modern UX and scenario-solving.
