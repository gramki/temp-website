# Family Banking Scenarios - Product Manager Review

**Reviewer Perspective:** Banking Product Manager  
**Initiative:** Family-Focused Relationship Banking  
**Date:** [Current Date]

## Executive Summary

The current scenario set covers **34 scenarios** across 9 priority tiers, focusing primarily on **financial coordination and goal planning** within families. While comprehensive in covering day-to-day financial activities, there are **significant unmet needs** in family financial journeys:

1. **"When Things Go Wrong" Journeys** - Fraud, disputes, errors, unauthorized transactions
2. **"Setting Up Our Financial Foundation" Journeys** - Opening accounts, configuring access, establishing family banking structure
3. **"Seeing Our Complete Picture" Journeys** - Viewing all family finances together, understanding full financial health
4. **"Making Financial Decisions" Journeys** - Choosing products, comparing options, understanding what's best for the family
5. **"Protecting Ourselves" Journeys** - Insurance claims, fraud protection, financial safety nets
6. **"Managing Informal Financial Relationships" Journeys** - Hand loans, family lending, informal receivables
7. **"Life Transitions" Journeys** - Marriage, children, career changes, retirement transitions
8. **"Getting Help When We Need It" Journeys** - Service requests, complaints, getting support from the bank
9. **"Optimizing What We Have" Journeys** - Maximizing rewards, minimizing fees, optimizing existing products
10. **"Planning for the Future" Journeys** - Estate planning, legacy management, ensuring family security

**Overall Assessment:** **Strong in operational coordination (payments, savings, goals) but missing critical journey moments** - especially crisis management, setup/onboarding, decision-making support, and life transitions.

### ❌ Unmet Needs & Incomplete Journeys

#### 1. "When Things Go Wrong" - Crisis & Problem Resolution Journeys

**The Need:** Families need to handle financial problems, errors, and fraud quickly and confidently, often under stress.

**Missing Scenarios:**
- **"I see a transaction I didn't make"** - Discovering and reporting unauthorized transactions, understanding what happened, getting money back
- **"My card was used fraudulently"** - Reporting card fraud, blocking card, getting replacement, understanding liability
- **"A merchant charged me incorrectly"** - Disputing merchant charges, getting refunds, chargeback process
- **"I paid the wrong person"** - Realizing payment error, trying to recover money, understanding options
- **"My account was frozen/blocked"** - Understanding why, getting it resolved, accessing funds during freeze
- **"I lost my card/phone"** - Immediate blocking, getting replacements, ensuring security
- **"There's an error in my statement"** - Identifying errors, reporting them, getting corrections

**Why This Matters:** These are high-stress moments where families need immediate, clear guidance. Current scenarios assume everything works smoothly.

---

#### 2. "Setting Up Our Financial Foundation" - Onboarding & Configuration Journeys

**The Need:** Families need to establish their banking structure, configure access, and set up the right accounts for their situation.

**Missing Scenarios:**
- **"We just got married - how do we combine our finances?"** - Opening joint accounts, deciding account structure, merging financial lives
- **"I want my spouse to help manage bills"** - Setting up account access, defining permissions, establishing trust boundaries
- **"We need separate accounts but want to see everything together"** - Configuring family view, setting up aggregation, maintaining privacy
- **"I want to give my teenager a card with limits"** - Issuing add-on cards, setting spending limits, teaching financial responsibility
- **"We're moving to a new city - what do we need?"** - Account transfers, updating addresses, changing banking relationships
- **"I want to add my parents as beneficiaries"** - Updating nominations, managing beneficiaries, ensuring smooth transitions
- **"We need to close an account"** - Understanding closure process, transferring funds, ensuring nothing is missed

**Why This Matters:** These are foundational moments that determine how families interact with banking long-term. Getting it wrong creates ongoing friction.

---

#### 3. "Seeing Our Complete Picture" - Visibility & Understanding Journeys

**The Need:** Families need to understand their complete financial situation across all members, accounts, and institutions.

**Missing Scenarios:**
- **"I want to see all our family accounts in one place"** - Aggregating accounts, viewing family dashboard, understanding total financial position
- **"How are we doing financially as a family?"** - Financial health assessment, credit score monitoring, debt-to-income analysis, early warning indicators
- **"Where did all our money go this month?"** - Cash flow analysis, spending patterns, identifying leaks, understanding family spending
- **"Can we afford this?"** - Affordability checks, impact analysis, trade-off evaluation before major decisions
- **"What are we missing?"** - Identifying gaps in coverage, missing insurance, underutilized benefits, optimization opportunities
- **"How do we compare to similar families?"** - Benchmarking, understanding norms, setting realistic expectations

**Why This Matters:** Without visibility, families make decisions in the dark. Current scenarios focus on individual activities but not holistic understanding.

---

#### 4. "Making Financial Decisions" - Product Selection & Comparison Journeys

**The Need:** Families need to choose the right financial products, compare options, and make informed decisions together.

**Missing Scenarios:**
- **"Which loan should we take?"** - Comparing loan offers, understanding terms, evaluating total cost, making joint decision
- **"What credit card is best for our spending?"** - Analyzing spending patterns, comparing card benefits, choosing family-appropriate card
- **"Should we invest in FD or mutual funds?"** - Understanding risk-return, comparing options, aligning with family goals, making investment decisions
- **"Do we have enough insurance?"** - Coverage adequacy assessment, comparing policies, understanding gaps, making coverage decisions
- **"Should we prepay our loan or invest?"** - Evaluating trade-offs, understanding opportunity cost, making strategic decisions
- **"Which bank account type is right for us?"** - Comparing account features, understanding fees, choosing appropriate tier
- **"Should we upgrade our banking relationship?"** - Evaluating premium banking, understanding benefits, making upgrade decisions

**Why This Matters:** These are critical decision points where families need guidance. Wrong decisions have long-term financial impact.

---

#### 5. "Protecting Ourselves" - Safety & Security Journeys

**The Need:** Families need to protect themselves from fraud, ensure adequate insurance coverage, and handle claims when things go wrong.

**Missing Scenarios:**
- **"Someone used my card - what do I do?"** - Immediate fraud response, blocking access, reporting, understanding protection
- **"We need to file an insurance claim"** - Understanding claim process, filing claims, tracking status, getting reimbursed
- **"Is our insurance coverage enough?"** - Coverage adequacy assessment, understanding gaps, making coverage decisions
- **"How do we protect our accounts?"** - Setting up security features, understanding fraud protection, managing passwords/PINs
- **"What if something happens to me?"** - Estate planning basics, nomination management, ensuring family can access accounts
- **"Our house was damaged - how do we claim?"** - Property insurance claims, understanding coverage, getting repairs covered

**Why This Matters:** Protection is fundamental to financial security. Families need proactive protection and reactive support when things go wrong.

---

#### 6. "Managing Informal Financial Relationships" - Hand Loans & Informal Lending Journeys

**The Need:** Families frequently lend to and borrow from family/friends. This needs tracking and management without formal banking products.

**Missing Scenarios:**
- **"I lent money to my brother - how do I track it?"** - Documenting hand loans, setting reminders, managing informal receivables
- **"My friend owes me money - how do I remind them?"** - Tracking informal loans, sending reminders, managing relationships around money
- **"We're lending to our child for education"** - Documenting family loans, understanding tax implications, managing repayment expectations
- **"My parents gave us money for the house"** - Tracking family gifts/loans, understanding implications, managing expectations
- **"I need to borrow from family for an emergency"** - Understanding options, documenting loans, managing repayment

**Why This Matters:** Informal lending is common in families but creates relationship stress when not managed. Current scenarios only cover formal banking.

---

#### 7. "Life Transitions" - Major Change Journeys

**The Need:** Families go through major life changes that require financial restructuring and new approaches.

**Missing Scenarios:**
- **"We're having a baby - what changes?"** - Financial planning for new child, updating insurance, adjusting budgets, planning for education
- **"I lost my job - how do we manage?"** - Emergency fund usage, expense reduction, income gap management, maintaining stability
- **"We're getting divorced - how do we separate finances?"** - Account separation, asset division, financial independence, managing transition
- **"I'm retiring - what do I need to do?"** - Pension management, retirement account transitions, income planning, healthcare considerations
- **"Our child is going to college"** - Education funding, student accounts, managing expenses, financial independence transition
- **"We're moving abroad"** - Account management, remittances, currency management, maintaining Indian banking
- **"A family member passed away"** - Account access, beneficiary claims, estate settlement, financial transition

**Why This Matters:** Life transitions are when families need the most support but are least equipped to handle complexity.

---

#### 8. "Getting Help When We Need It" - Service & Support Journeys

**The Need:** Families need to request services, get documents, resolve issues, and access support from their bank.

**Missing Scenarios:**
- **"I need my account statement for visa application"** - Requesting statements, getting documents, understanding formats
- **"I want to change my EMI date"** - Service requests, understanding options, making changes
- **"We have a complaint about service"** - Filing complaints, tracking resolution, escalating when needed
- **"I need a demand draft for admission"** - Requesting banking instruments, understanding fees, getting documents
- **"Can we get this fee waived?"** - Understanding fees, requesting waivers, negotiating charges
- **"I forgot my password"** - Account recovery, security verification, regaining access
- **"We need to talk to someone"** - Accessing relationship manager, getting human support, understanding service levels

**Why This Matters:** Service moments define the banking relationship. Poor experiences here erode trust built elsewhere.

---

#### 9. "Optimizing What We Have" - Maximizing Value Journeys

**The Need:** Families want to get the most value from existing products - rewards, benefits, fee optimization.

**Missing Scenarios:**
- **"We're not using our credit card rewards"** - Understanding rewards, redeeming points, optimizing earning, avoiding expiry
- **"We're paying too many fees"** - Identifying fees, understanding charges, minimizing costs, requesting waivers
- **"Our FD is maturing - what should we do?"** - FD renewal decisions, interest rate optimization, laddering strategies
- **"We have money sitting idle"** - Identifying idle funds, moving to better products, optimizing returns
- **"We're missing out on benefits"** - Understanding product benefits, utilizing features, maximizing value
- **"Our card gives cashback we're not using"** - Understanding cashback, tracking earnings, optimizing spending

**Why This Matters:** Families often don't realize the value they're leaving on the table. Optimization scenarios help maximize existing relationships.

---

#### 10. "Planning for the Future" - Estate & Legacy Journeys

**The Need:** Families need to plan for what happens after they're gone, manage important documents, and ensure smooth transitions.

**Missing Scenarios:**
- **"What happens to our accounts if something happens to us?"** - Nomination management, will planning, ensuring family access
- **"Where do we keep important documents?"** - Document storage, digital vault, organizing financial documents, sharing with family
- **"We need to update our will"** - Estate planning, asset documentation, ensuring proper distribution
- **"Our parents passed away - how do we access accounts?"** - Beneficiary claims, account access, estate settlement
- **"We want to set up a trust for our children"** - Trust planning, estate management, long-term family security

**Why This Matters:** Estate planning is often ignored until it's too late. Families need proactive support for legacy planning.

## Conclusion

The current scenario set provides an **excellent foundation** for understanding **day-to-day financial coordination** within families (payments, savings, goals, debt management). However, there are **critical unmet needs** in family financial journeys:

### What's Well Covered ✅
- **Operational Coordination:** Monthly bills, grocery expenses, shared expenses, subscriptions
- **Goal Planning:** Big purchases, vacations, emergency funds, education savings
- **Debt Management:** Loan prepayment, credit card debt, property EMIs
- **Family Relationships:** Parental support, extended family coordination, teenage financial literacy

### Critical Gaps - Unmet User Needs ❌

1. **"When Things Go Wrong"** - Crisis moments (fraud, disputes, errors) that define trust
2. **"Setting Up Our Financial Foundation"** - Onboarding and configuration that determines long-term relationship quality
3. **"Seeing Our Complete Picture"** - Visibility and understanding that enables informed decisions
4. **"Making Financial Decisions"** - Product selection and comparison where guidance is most valuable
5. **"Getting Help When We Need It"** - Service and support that defines ongoing satisfaction
6. **"Protecting Ourselves"** - Security and insurance that provides peace of mind
7. **"Managing Informal Financial Relationships"** - Hand loans and family lending that create relationship stress
8. **"Life Transitions"** - Major changes where families need the most support
9. **"Optimizing What We Have"** - Maximizing value from existing products
10. **"Planning for the Future"** - Estate and legacy planning for long-term security

**Recommendation:** Add **30-40 additional scenarios** organized by user journey needs rather than product features. Focus first on **trust and safety journeys** (crisis resolution, setup, service), then **understanding and decision journeys** (visibility, product selection, protection).

**Priority Framework:**
- **🔴 Critical:** Trust-defining moments (crisis, setup, service) - Must have for MVP
- **🟡 High:** Value-enabling moments (visibility, decisions, protection) - Important for V1
- **🟢 Medium:** Optimization moments (rewards, informal lending, transitions, legacy) - V2+
