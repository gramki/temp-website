
## Example - the Mehta Family:

### 1. Head of the Family (Primary Earner)

- **Role:** Primary income provider and decision-maker for the family's financial security.
- **Responsibilities:** Oversees big-ticket expenses, investments, and essential recurring payments like bills.
- **Challenges:** Time-constrained but requires clear visibility and control over the family's financial health to make informed decisions.

### 2. Spouse (Secondary Earner)

- **Role:** Supports the household with secondary income and manages operational expenses.
- **Responsibilities:** Balances work, family responsibilities, and the children's routine needs. Plays a collaborative role in financial planning.
- **Challenges:** Needs shared access to financial information for effective contribution and decision-making while managing daily family expenses.

### 3. Teenage Child

- **Role:** Dependent but beginning to develop financial literacy and autonomy.
- **Responsibilities:** Manages allowances, occasional spending, and starts saving for personal goals.
- **Challenges:** Requires structured guidance to build responsible financial habits and the right mix of independence and oversight.

# Scenarios (Stories)

## Scenario 1: Planning for a Big Purchase (Fridge)

**Persona:** Spouse (Secondary Earner)

**User Story:**
As the secondary earner, I need to plan for buying a ₹30,000 fridge by assessing our household's financial situation, cutting unnecessary expenses, and saving systematically without impacting essential needs.

### User Journey

1. **Review Spending:** Check the last three months of expenses (e.g., groceries ₹15,000/month, utilities ₹5,000/month, dining out ₹6,000/month). Identify potential areas to save (e.g., reducing dining out to ₹2,000).
2. **Evaluate Feasibility:** Discuss with the primary earner to confirm a savings timeline (e.g., 5 months). Factor in upcoming expenses (e.g., ₹10,000 for school fees next month).
3. **Adjust Budget:** Propose cutting Swiggy orders (₹4,000/month) and pausing an OTT subscription (₹500/month). Gain family alignment on these changes by explaining how it supports the fridge purchase.
4. **Plan Savings:** Allocate ₹6,000/month for 5 months to reach ₹30,000. Use a separate bank account to ensure savings aren't spent on other needs.
5. **Monitor Progress:** Track savings monthly, adjusting for unexpected costs (e.g., a ₹2,000 medical bill). Reassess discretionary expenses if the savings goal isn't on track.

### User Motivations/Anxieties

- **Ownership:** Feels responsible for finding ways to fund the purchase.
- **Frustrations:** Balancing family expectations with the discipline to cut spending.
- **Satisfaction:** Achieving the goal gives a sense of contribution and financial control.

## Scenario 2: Planning to Pay Monthly Household Bills

**Personas:** Head of Family (Primary Earner) and Spouse (Secondary Earner)

**User Story:**
As a couple, we want to organize and pay our household bills (₹25,000 total) at the start of the month, so we can avoid late fees, maintain financial discipline, and ensure our household runs smoothly.

### Process

1. **List Monthly Bills:** Together, they review recurring bills:
   - Rent (₹20,000)
   - Utilities like electricity (₹3,000) and Wi-Fi (₹1,000)
   - Household help salary (₹5,000)
   - *Assume: We can identify the bills using access to the user's bank account statement or SMS inbox*
2. **Divide Responsibilities:** The primary earner focuses on high-value payments (e.g., rent, electricity). The secondary earner manages smaller, day-to-day bills (e.g., Wi-Fi, maid salary).
3. **Coordinate Payment Timing:** Schedule payments immediately after salary credits to avoid delays, starting with rent and utilities. Align payment schedules with cash flow (e.g., ensure smaller bills are paid after fixed commitments are handled).
4. **Allocate Buffer for Variations:** Discuss possible variations (e.g., increased electricity usage during summer) and set aside a small buffer (₹1,000–₹2,000) for adjustments.
5. **Track Completion:** Both review a list or digital tracker to confirm all payments are cleared. Address any pending or missed bills to avoid late fees.

### Emotional Insights

- **Relief Through Organization:** Completing bill payments early reduces stress for both partners and ensures smooth household operations.
- **Shared Responsibility:** Splitting bill management fosters collaboration and shared accountability in financial management.
- **Anticipation of Variances:** Seasonal or unexpected changes (e.g., higher utility bills) can create anxiety, requiring proactive discussions.
- **Satisfaction in Discipline:** Successfully paying all bills on time brings a sense of accomplishment and reinforces trust in their financial planning.

## Scenario 3: Pocket Money Management

**Persona:** Teenage Child

**User Story:**
As a teenager, I want to manage my ₹1,000 monthly allowance for snacks, outings, and saving for a gaming accessory, so I can enjoy independence and learn financial responsibility.

### Process

1. **Budgeting:** Allocate ₹300 for school snacks, ₹200 for movie, and ₹500 for saving toward a ₹2,500 gaming accessory/skin.
2. **Spending Decisions:** Balance impulsive purchases (e.g., treats with friends) with saving for larger goals.
3. **Handling Shortfalls:** If overspending occurs, negotiate with parents for extra money or adjust future spending plans.

### User Motivations/Anxieties

- **Independence:** Enjoys the freedom of managing their money but feels peer pressure on choices.
- **Impulse vs. Goals:** Struggles to resist instant gratification for longer-term rewards.
- **Pride:** Feels accomplished when achieving savings goals but nervous about parental judgment over poor decisions.

## Scenario 4: Planning to Repay/Prepay a Small Loan

**Personas:** Head of Family (Primary Earner) and Spouse (Secondary Earner)

**User Story:**
As a couple, we want to evaluate how to repay or prepay our ₹3,00,000 car loan faster, so we can reduce financial stress and save on interest without impacting essential expenses or other family goals.

### Process

1. **Understand Loan Details:** Together, review the remaining loan amount (₹3,00,000), monthly EMI (₹8,000), and interest rate (10%). Discuss potential savings from prepayment (e.g., paying ₹50,000 upfront saves ₹15,000 in interest).
2. **Assess Household Finances:** The primary earner reviews monthly surplus after fixed expenses. The secondary earner provides insights into discretionary spending (e.g., dining out ₹5,000) and possible savings (e.g., canceling an unused subscription ₹500/month).
3. **Weigh Trade-Offs:** Balance prepayment with other priorities like emergency savings, child's education, or planned purchases (e.g., a family vacation). Discuss and agree on how much to allocate toward prepayment versus ongoing savings.
4. **Plan Execution:** Decide to use a bonus month or festival savings (e.g., Diwali bonus ₹25,000) for a partial lump-sum payment. Agree to add ₹2,000 to the monthly EMI using household surplus for faster repayment.
5. **Track Progress:** The couple reviews the loan balance periodically to ensure prepayment aligns with household cash flow.

### User Motivations/Anxieties

- **Shared Responsibility:** Both feel the weight of the loan and a desire to reduce its burden on the family.
- **Collaborative Tensions:** Discussions about trade-offs (e.g., deferring a vacation) may lead to disagreements but also foster alignment on priorities.
- **Relief in Progress:** Successfully reducing the loan balance brings a shared sense of accomplishment and financial control.
- **Long-Term Thinking:** They feel motivated by the vision of a debt-free future, balancing present needs with future stability.

## Scenario 5: Monthly Money Allocation

**Persona:** Head of Family (Primary Earner)

**User Story:**
As the primary earner, I need to organize my ₹80,000 monthly salary by allocating funds for bills, savings, and household expenses at the start of the month, so I can ensure financial stability while addressing family needs.

### User Journey

1. **Salary Allocation:** Divide ₹80,000 into fixed bills (₹35,000), household expenses (₹20,000), savings (₹15,000), discretionary spending (₹8,000), and a ₹2,000 buffer.
2. **Pay Fixed Bills First:** Settle rent, utilities, school fees, and household help immediately via online transfers to avoid delays.
3. **Plan Expenses:** Estimate household needs (e.g., groceries ₹12,000, transport ₹5,000) and set discretionary limits for outings or dining out.
4. **Save Proactively:** Transfer ₹15,000 to savings pots: emergency fund, education fund, and long-term goals like a vacation.
5. **Monitor & Adjust:** Track spending mid-month and reallocate funds (e.g., reduce outings) if a category overshoots the budget.

### User Motivations/Anxieties

- **Heavy Responsibility:** Feels the weight of ensuring both immediate needs (bills, groceries) and long-term goals (education, emergencies) are met without fail.
- **Fear of the Unexpected:** Anxiety about unplanned expenses disrupting carefully laid plans.
- **Personal Sacrifice:** Often puts family needs above personal desires, leading to quiet self-neglect.
- **Collaboration Challenges:** Aligning plans with the spouse can create tension if spending priorities differ.
- **Relief Through Planning:** Structuring finances at the start of the month provides peace of mind and a sense of control.
- **Pressure to Perform:** Constantly feels the need to hit savings targets, fearing the consequences of falling short.
