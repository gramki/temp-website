### 3.4 QR as Alternative Merchant Acceptance Channel

QR codes are emerging as a universal low-cost acceptance mechanism:
- **Brazil**: QR codes on 41% of all PIX transactions; zero merchant fees for PIX QR acceptance (vs. ~2% for cards)
- **ASEAN**: QR captures 45% of digital payments; interoperable across borders without card network involvement
- **Europe**: Wero positions QR as an alternative to Visa/Mastercard at POS, built on SEPA Instant rails (no interchange)
- **India**: UPI QR codes surged 126% (280M to 633M) between Jul 2023 and Dec 2024 (Entrepreneur India, 2025)

### 6.3 Why Banks / Corporates Build Private Networks

| Motivation | Detail | Source |
|-----------|--------|--------|
| **Interchange avoidance / control** | Closed-loop transactions bypass Visa/Mastercard interchange; reduce per-transaction costs by 1–3% | SmartDev / Investopedia, 2025 |
| **Data ownership** | Full visibility into customer purchase behavior; richer analytics than card network data feeds | SmartDev / Latterly.org, 2025 |
| **Loyalty and retention** | Integrated rewards create switching costs; stored-value balances drive repeat visits | SmartDev / PRWeb, 2025 |
| **Revenue control** | Organizations keep more value internally; create new revenue streams (float income, premium services) | SmartDev, 2025 |
| **Offset interchange regulation** | With Visa/Mastercard capping fees (U.S. settlement through 2030), "Loyalty 2.0" value-added services create alternate revenue | Finextra, 2025 |
| **Underwriting advantage** | Closed-loop data enables better credit decisions (AmEx model) | Latterly.org, 2025 |
