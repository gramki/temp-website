## 1. Legacy Incumbents

### 1.1 FIS (Fidelity National Information Services)

| Dimension | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Revenue** | $10.7B total (FY2025). Banking Solutions: $7.3B. Capital Markets: $3.2B. Guided $13.8B for 2026 (+30%) after absorbing TSYS. |
| **Payments-specific** | Acquired Global Payments' Issuer Solutions (formerly TSYS) for $13.5B enterprise value, closing Jan 2026. Processes 40B+ txns/year across 75+ countries for 150+ FIs. Expected $500M incremental free cash flow in Year 1, $700M by 2028. |
| **Primary customers** | Tier 1 and Tier 2 banks globally. 150+ financial institution clients via TSYS issuer business. |
| **Core strengths** | Massive scale in issuer processing (TSYS adds dominant U.S. card-issuing franchise). Deep bank relationships. End-to-end banking-to-capital-markets stack. Banking Solutions grew 8.3% organically in 2025. |
| **Key weaknesses** | Integration risk — absorbing a $13.5B acquisition while managing existing complexity. Sold Worldpay merchant-acquiring stake to Global Payments ($6.6B), losing direct merchant-side capabilities. Legacy monolithic architectures in core products. Slow innovation velocity relative to modern challengers. |
| **Vulnerability** | Cloud-native specialists (Volante, Form3) attacking the payments hub layer. Modern card issuers (Marqeta, i2c) competing for next-gen issuer programs. Mid-market banks may find FIS over-engineered and expensive. |
| **Recent moves** | Sold remaining 45% Worldpay stake to Global Payments (Apr 2025). Acquired TSYS/Issuer Solutions from Global Payments (Jan 2026). Targeting $3B free cash flow by 2028. |

**Sources:** FIS 10-K (Feb 2026), FIS press releases (Jan 2026), Reuters, StockTitan

## 3. Specialists

### 3.1 Volante Technologies

| Dimension | Detail |
|---|---|

| **Recent moves** | Successfully migrated customers for Fedwire ISO 20022 (Jul 2025), EU SEPA Instant (Oct 2025), SWIFT SRG 2025 (Nov 2025). Multi-cloud Resiliency Service launch. |

## 4. India-Specific Vendors

### 4.1 NPCI and the UPI Ecosystem

| Dimension | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Scale** | 21.7B transactions in Jan 2026 (+28% YoY). ~700M daily transactions. ₹28.33 lakh crore monthly value. 709M active UPI QR codes. |
| **International expansion** | Live in 8 countries (Bhutan, Nepal, Mauritius, Sri Lanka, Singapore, UAE, Qatar, France). Cyprus joined Jun 2025. 2M+ international merchants onboarded. Cross-border txn volumes: 1.48M in FY2025-26 (vs. 37,060 in FY2024). |

| **Competitive implications** | UPI is infrastructure, not a vendor — but shapes the entire payments stack. Vendors must be UPI-native. International expansion creates opportunity for UPI infrastructure exporters. |

## 6. Competitive Gaps — Underserved by Current Vendors

### 6.1 Mid-Size and Regional Bank Payment Modernization

**The Gap:** Mid-market banks (Tier 2–3, community, regional) face a "modernization trap" — legacy incumbent platforms (FIS, Fiserv) are too expensive and over-engineered for their needs, while modern challengers (Marqeta, Stripe) are designed for fintechs, not traditional banks. Key evidence:

- Large FIs are **6x more likely** to deploy real-time payments than smaller banks (74% vs. 44%)
- **37% of credit union leaders** say their payment offerings don't meet member needs
- **73% of banking professionals** identify fintechs as top competitive threats
- Cost and integration complexity are the primary barriers to real-time payment adoption
- **60% of credit unions** self-identify as "fast followers" — they want to adopt but lack viable vendor options at their price point

### 6.2 Unified Payments + Core Banking for Mid-Market

**The Gap:** No vendor delivers a genuinely integrated, affordable, cloud-native payments platform combined with core banking for mid-market banks. Temenos comes closest but payments remains secondary to core. Fiserv bundles them but on legacy architecture. Volante and ACI do payments hubs only. This forces mid-market banks into multi-vendor integrations.

### 6.3 Real-Time Payments as a Service (for non-Tier 1 banks)

**The Gap:** FedNow adoption among smaller banks is drastically underperforming expectations. Finzly is the only vendor purpose-built for this segment, but has limited scale ($10M funding). ACI and FIS solutions are priced for Tier 1. The mid-market needs a managed service model — "FedNow-as-a-Service" — that handles connectivity, compliance, fraud, and scheme management, with simple pricing.

### 6.4 Modern Card Issuing for Traditional Banks

**The Gap:** Marqeta, i2c, and Galileo have built modern card-issuing platforms — but their go-to-market is oriented toward fintechs and neobanks. Traditional banks needing to modernize their card programs have limited modern options: FIS/TSYS and Fiserv/First Data remain the default, with decades-old architectures. The i2c-Finastra partnership is an early attempt to bridge this gap, but is still nascent.

### 6.5 Cross-Rail Payment Orchestration

**The Gap:** Banks increasingly need to orchestrate across multiple payment rails (ACH, FedNow, RTP, Fedwire, SWIFT, SEPA, UPI) through a single platform with intelligent routing. ACI Connetic and Volante offer this for large banks, but mid-market solutions are scarce. Juspay's HyperSwitch does this for merchant-side orchestration but not for bank-side payment operations.

### 6.6 ISO 20022 Migration Support for Mid-Market

**The Gap:** Major migration deadlines are arriving (Fedwire: Jul 2025, EU SEPA Instant: Oct 2025, SWIFT SRG: Nov 2025) and Tier 1 banks are served by Volante, ACI, and SWIFT. Tier 2–3 banks lack affordable, managed migration paths. Many are dependent on their core banking vendor (often Fiserv or Jack Henry) to handle ISO 20022, creating bottlenecks.

### 6.7 Embedded Finance Infrastructure for Banks

**The Gap:** Stripe Treasury and BaaS providers enable fintechs to embed banking. But banks wanting to become the embedded-finance providers (offering their own BaaS capabilities to platforms) have limited modern options. This is a strategic gap: banks want to be the platform, not just power someone else's platform.

### 6.8 Fraud and Financial Crime as an Integrated Layer

**The Gap:** Payment fraud, AML, and sanctions screening are typically handled by separate vendors (NICE Actimize, Featurespace, ComplyAdvantage). No payments platform vendor offers deeply integrated, AI-native fraud prevention as a core layer of the payments hub — it's always an add-on or separate system. This creates latency, data duplication, and operational complexity.

## Key Takeaways for Competitive Strategy

1. **The Great Reshuffling (2025–2026):** FIS acquiring TSYS while Global Payments acquires Worldpay has fundamentally reshaped the legacy incumbent landscape. FIS is now the dominant issuer processor; Global Payments is the dominant acquirer. Banks must choose sides.

2. **Cloud-native payments hubs are winning:** Volante (Gartner Leader), ACI Connetic, and Form3 are displacing on-premise payments middleware. The market is shifting from "build" to "subscribe."

3. **Modern card issuers serve fintechs, not banks:** Marqeta ($625M), i2c, and Galileo have built excellent modern platforms but their sales motions, pricing models, and product design cater to fintechs and neobanks. Traditional banks remain underserved by modern issuers.

4. **Mid-market banks are the most underserved segment:** Too small for FIS/Fiserv enterprise sales, too traditional for Marqeta/Stripe fintech platforms. This segment needs affordable, managed, cloud-native payment modernization — and few vendors deliver it.

5. **Real-time payments adoption gap is widening:** Tier 1 banks are 6x more likely to have deployed real-time payments. The mid-market needs "real-time-payments-as-a-service" with managed compliance, fraud, and scheme connectivity.

6. **India and Brazil are structurally different markets:** UPI (India) and Pix (Brazil) have created government-backed real-time payment rails that reshape vendor dynamics. Vendor value shifts from processing to orchestration, fraud prevention, and value-added services on top of free rails.

7. **Open banking creates a parallel payment system:** Token.io, Form3, and the UKPI initiative are building A2A payment infrastructure that bypasses card networks entirely. This is a slow-burn disruption but accelerating — open banking payments projected to exceed $116B by 2026.
