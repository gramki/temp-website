section-6-governance-override-in-seer-overviewSection 6: Governance & Override in Seer — Overview

purpose-of-this-sectionPurpose of this Section

This section demonstrates how Seer provides granular governance, policy enforcement, and human override capabilities for enterprise agents. Enterprise agents must operate within strict boundaries while remaining adaptable to exceptional situations requiring human judgment.

Governance in Seer is not a single feature but a pervasive architectural property—woven throughout the agent lifecycle, context assembly, and runtime execution. This section focuses on the specific mechanisms: policy enforcement, guardrails, human-in-the-loop override, and emergency controls.

core-questions-addressedCore Questions Addressed

  • How does Seer enforce policies at runtime?
  • What are guardrails, and how are they implemented in Seer?
  • How can humans intervene and override agent decisions?
  • What emergency controls exist (kill switches, dual control)?
  • How are governance decisions audited?

This section is organized into the following sub-sections:

  • 6.2 Guardrails: Input, output, and behavioral guardrails.
  • 6.3 Human Override: Intervention mechanisms and escalation.
  • 6.4 Kill Switches & Dual Control: Emergency controls for critical situations.
