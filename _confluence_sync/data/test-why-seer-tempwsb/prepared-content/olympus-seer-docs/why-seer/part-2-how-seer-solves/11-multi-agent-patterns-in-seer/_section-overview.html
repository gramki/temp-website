section-11-multi-agent-patterns-in-seer-overviewSection 11: Multi-Agent Patterns in Seer — Overview

purpose-of-this-sectionPurpose of this Section

This section demonstrates how Seer enables structured collaboration between multiple AI agents and between AI agents and humans. Part 1, Section 5.9 established the requirements for multi-agent coordination; this section shows how Seer delivers those capabilities through Hub's collaboration infrastructure.

Enterprise processes rarely involve a single agent working in isolation. Real-world operations require specialized agents coordinating with each other and with humans, transferring context, escalating decisions, and maintaining accountability across handoffs. Seer provides structured patterns for these collaborations.

core-questions-addressedCore Questions Addressed

  • Why do enterprise agents work in teams rather than isolation?
  • What are the agent archetypes, and how do they differ?
  • What coordination patterns does Seer support?
  • How is context preserved and transferred during agent handoffs?
  • How do AI agents and humans collaborate effectively?

structure-of-this-sectionStructure of this Section

This section is organized into the following sub-sections:

  • 11.1 Agents Work in Teams: Why and how agents collaborate in teams.
  • 11.2 Agent Archetypes: The four functional perspectives—Thinker, Doer, Orchestrator, Governor.
  • 11.3 Coordination Patterns: Scenario-as-Tool, Scenario-as-Agent, Workbench-as-Machine, Parent-Child Requests.
  • 11.4 Handoff Context: Preserving context during agent-to-agent transfers.
  • 11.5 Human-AI Teaming: Integrating human and AI agents in shared operational contexts.
