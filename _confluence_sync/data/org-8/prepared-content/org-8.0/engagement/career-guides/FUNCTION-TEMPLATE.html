Function Coaching Template

Use this structure for each function coaching doc. Repeat the three-block structure for every level in the designation ladder (from ).

Section Structure (per seniority level)

Repeat the three-block structure below for every level. Use ## for the level name and ### for subsections.

## [Level Name]

### Required Craft, Skills, Competencies
### Roles Expected to Play and Challenges
### How to Learn

Required Craft, Skills, Competencies

What someone at this designation must be able to do. Be specific to the Engagement Operating Model:

  • Craft: Technical or domain abilities (e.g. "can design integration adapters that extend a Product Line without forking").
  • Skills: Observable, trainable capabilities (e.g. "can run a gap analysis with the EA and produce an inner source vs custom recommendation").
  • Competencies: Behaviours and judgment (e.g. "can manage the tension between delivery pressure and EA's inner source bar without escalating prematurely").

Tie every item to a construct in the model (squad types, artifact groups, lifecycle phases, formation, inner source, verification, etc.).

Roles Expected to Play and Challenges

  • Which Engagement roles are typically staffed from this level (reference the designation-to-complexity table in ).
  • What challenges are typical when playing those roles — name the specific interactions, tensions, and decisions from the model.

How to Learn

Practical mechanisms that exist in this model:

  • On the job: Rotation across PL/CP/Studio/Verification contexts, multi-Engagement exposure, stretch role assignments.
  • Feedback: Execution-axis input from EO/EPM; functional-axis evaluations from your function's leadership.
  • Knowledge sharing: PAC Practice Mode, retrospectives, inner source contributions, archetype and variability documentation.
  • External reading: 1-2 curated references with stable links and one-line "why relevant" notes.

Further Reading (end of doc)

Aggregate the most relevant external references for the function. Each entry:

  • Title, author, stable link
  • One-line "why relevant to this function in the Engagement Operating Model"

Only include references that suit this operating model (product-line assembly, inner source, dual-axis, verification/certification, Engagement Success, etc.). Do not add generic titles.

Checklist Before Publishing

  • [ ] Every section references at least one concrete construct from the guide.
  • [ ] Business context (assembly not services, extend don't fork, Engagement Success, platforms as accelerators) is reflected where it explains why the level or behaviour matters.
  • [ ] No standalone platitudes; every piece of advice tied to a specific interaction, decision, or artifact.
  • [ ] "How to learn" points to mechanisms that exist in the model (dual-axis feedback, rotation, formation, PAC, inner source, multi-Engagement experience).
  • [ ] Further reading entries have stable links and one-line "why relevant" notes tied to the model.
