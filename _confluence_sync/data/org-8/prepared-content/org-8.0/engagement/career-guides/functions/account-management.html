Account Management — Function Coaching Guide

|

Purpose

This guide helps Account Management practitioners understand what the Engagement Operating Model expects of them and how to grow within the function. The Account Management function supplies the Client Partner and CPA (Client Partner Associate) roles — the two client-facing roles that sit above individual Engagements. Unlike other functions, Account Management is scoped per-client, not per-Engagement: a Client Partner may span multiple Engagements with the same client.

The Account Management function also has representation in ERC, influencing Engagement formation and role assignment.

Early-Career Account Management Practitioner

The specific designation ladder for Account Management is organisationally defined. This section covers the career progression leading toward Client Partner and CPA readiness.

Required Craft, Skills, Competencies

  • Craft: Understand the commercial relationship between the organisation and the client. Map client stakeholders — decision-makers, influencers, budget holders, technical counterparts. Prepare governance materials and executive briefings.
  • Skills: Track scope changes and their commercial impact — understand how delivery scope changes affect contract terms and flag misalignment to senior practitioners. Coordinate across EPMs and EOs when the client has multiple Engagements.
  • Competencies: Distinguish between delivery communication (EPM's domain) and relationship management (Client Partner's domain). Early-career AM practitioners must understand both but not confuse their scope. Build trust with both internal delivery teams (EO, EPM) and client stakeholders.

Roles Expected to Play and Challenges

  • CPA (Client Partner Associate) — the primary entry point for AM practitioners into the Engagement Operating Model. CPA is generalist support for the Client Partner: governance preparation, stakeholder coordination, commercial tracking, cross-Engagement alignment, executive briefings.
  • Challenge: Building context quickly when joining a client with existing Engagements and stakeholders. The CPA must absorb relationship history, ongoing commitments, and political dynamics fast.
  • Challenge: Juggling multiple workstreams (governance, commercial, cross-Engagement) without direct authority over delivery. The CPA influences through preparation and coordination, not through decision-making.

How to Learn

  • On the job: Serve as CPA for a Client Partner managing 2+ Engagements. Prepare governance materials, attend client-side governance meetings, and debrief with the Client Partner afterward. Request feedback on what you captured vs. what the Client Partner saw in the room.
  • Feedback: Client Partner provides execution-axis input. AM leadership evaluates growth across client assignments.
  • Knowledge sharing: Document client stakeholder maps and relationship patterns. Contribute cross-Engagement coordination templates.
  • External reading: The Trusted Advisor (Maister, Green, Galford) — trustedadvisor.com — framework for building trust-based client relationships; directly applicable to the Client Partner's advisory stance and the CPA's supporting role.

Mid-Career Account Management Practitioner

Required Craft, Skills, Competencies

  • Craft: Own client-side governance preparation end-to-end — agenda, materials, stakeholder briefings, action items, follow-ups. Track commercial alignment across multiple Engagements for the same client. Identify and flag contract-delivery misalignment before it becomes a dispute.
  • Skills: Coordinate across EOs and EPMs for multi-Engagement clients — consolidate status, surface cross-Engagement dependencies, and prepare the Client Partner with an integrated client view. Support the Client Partner in executive-level conversations — provide data, context, and talking points.
  • Competencies: Navigate the Client Partner → EO authority boundary from the support side — understand when the Client Partner should engage the EO and when the CPA can coordinate directly with EPM. Know the escalation model: Client Partner ↔ EO disagreements go to ERC; Client Partner ↔ EPM communication alignment is resolved by EO.

Roles Expected to Play and Challenges

  • CPA with increasing scope — handling multi-Engagement coordination and representing the Client Partner in operational meetings (not decision-making meetings).
  • Challenge: Managing the boundary between CPA coordination and Client Partner decision-making. The CPA prepares; the Client Partner decides. Over-stepping erodes trust in both directions (with the Client Partner and with EOs).
  • Challenge: Commercial tracking without being a commercial negotiator. The CPA tracks scope-commercial alignment; Account Management leadership and Client Partner negotiate.

How to Learn

  • On the job: Take CPA assignments with increasingly complex clients (multi-Engagement, multiple stakeholder groups). Lead a governance preparation cycle independently.
  • Feedback: Client Partner rates coordination quality and stakeholder management. AM leadership evaluates multi-client breadth and commercial awareness.
  • Knowledge sharing: Contribute governance preparation templates and cross-Engagement coordination patterns.

Senior Account Management Practitioner / Client Partner Readiness

Required Craft, Skills, Competencies

  • Craft: Own the strategic client relationship — stakeholder mapping, executive alignment, relationship health. Navigate the authority boundary with EO: client-facing decisions (stakeholder management, commercial alignment, relationship strategy) are Client Partner's domain; internal delivery authority is EO's domain. Ensure delivery and commercial terms stay in sync.
  • Skills: Represent the client perspective in ERC — advise on Engagement formation, capacity priorities, and risk. Span multiple Engagements with the same client — maintain context across EOs, EPMs, and delivery timelines. Influence without direct delivery authority — the Client Partner's effectiveness depends on the quality of the relationship with EO and EPM, not on directing their work.
  • Competencies: Know when to escalate to client leadership vs. when to resolve internally with EO and EPM. Balance client expectations with delivery reality — the client wants more, faster; the EO owns what is feasible. Accept that AVA release-block authority is independent — Client Partner cannot override it, even under client pressure.

Roles Expected to Play and Challenges

  • Client Partner — the senior-most per-client role. One Client Partner per client; may span multiple Engagements.
  • Challenge: Spanning multiple Engagements with the same client — prioritisation and context-switching across EOs. The Client Partner must maintain an integrated client view that no single EO has.
  • Challenge: Exercising client-facing authority while respecting EO's delivery authority. When delivery decisions affect client commitments, EO recommends and Client Partner decides. When client demands affect delivery feasibility, Client Partner brings to EO and EO assesses. The boundary is clear but pressure makes it blur.
  • Challenge: Managing the CPA team — at least one CPA per Client Partner, more for complex clients. Delegating effectively to CPAs while retaining accountability.

How to Learn

  • On the job: Shadow an experienced Client Partner before taking a CP assignment. Take a Client Partner assignment on a single-Engagement client before a multi-Engagement client.
  • Feedback: AM leadership evaluates client relationship depth, commercial alignment quality, and multi-Engagement client portfolio management.
  • Knowledge sharing: Mentor CPAs. Contribute client engagement patterns to the AM community.
  • External reading: Handbook of Strategic Account Management (Wilson & Woodburn) — strategic account management practices directly applicable to the Client Partner role's per-client scope and multi-Engagement portfolio view.

Account Management Leadership

  • Craft: Own the AM function's career path. Evaluate Client Partners and CPAs across client assignments.
  • Key model construct: Represent AM in ERC. Advise on Client Partner and CPA assignment — which practitioner is the right fit for which client based on relationship complexity, commercial sophistication, and multi-Engagement dynamics. Ensure CPA support is always in place for Client Partners.
  • Challenge: The AM function interacts with every other function (through Client Partner → EO → EPM → EA → ELs) but doesn't manage any of them. Influence through the quality of client insight and commercial alignment, not through delivery authority.
  • Challenge: Defining career progression in a function where scope is per-client (not per-Engagement) and where the senior role (Client Partner) is a core operating model role, not a management layer.
